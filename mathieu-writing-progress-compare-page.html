How to go even further

What to do to make Version 2 even better

You already improved the ideas. Now the next step is to polish the language.

Corrected Version 2

In my view, films and documentaries can help people learn idioms, new vocabulary, and the way native speakers pronounce words and link them together. However, they are not a substitute for an English teacher or for speaking practice. For example, someone who regularly watches films and documentaries in English can improve their comprehension, and their ear may become better able to recognise different sounds. However, their speaking skills will not improve without practice. Just as a baby needs to listen and repeat in order to learn how to speak, an English learner also needs to listen and repeat to improve fluency. Overall, learning English is about becoming fluent, and fluency means being able to connect your ideas smoothly when speaking with other people. Therefore, films and documentaries alone are not enough to help a learner become fluent.

What to focus on now

  1. Use more natural phrasing: write link words together, not connect words between them.
  2. Choose the right preposition: write a substitute for, not a substitute to.
  3. Make endings smoother: write becoming fluent, not to get fluent.
  4. Keep the balance: continue showing both the advantages and the limits.

Stronger model answer

In my opinion, films and documentaries can be a very useful way to learn English. First of all, they help learners hear natural pronunciation, rhythm, and everyday expressions. They can also introduce useful vocabulary and idioms in real contexts. However, watching alone is not enough to become fluent. Although learners may improve their listening comprehension, their speaking skills will only improve if they actively practise. For example, repeating useful sentences, speaking aloud, and interacting with other people in English are all important. Just as children learn language by listening and repeating, English learners also need regular practice in order to make progress. In conclusion, films and documentaries are an excellent support tool, but they should be combined with active speaking practice.
