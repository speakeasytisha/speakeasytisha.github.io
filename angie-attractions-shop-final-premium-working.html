🛍️ Boutique vocabulary

🪄 Grammar Spell 1 — Polite professional language

Can I + verb?

Use: offer help.

Can I help you?

Would you like + noun?

Use: offer a choice politely.

Would you like a bag?

Would you like to + verb?

Use: make a polite suggestion.

Would you like to wait here?

Should + verb

Use: give advice or guidance.

You should go this way.

Can I means I offer help. Would you like means I give a polite choice. Should means I give advice or guidance.

Choose the best word

A guest is lost: “You ___ go this way.”

You offer help: “___ help you?”

You offer a bag: “___ a bag?”

➡️ Grammar Spell 2 — Instructions at attractions

Rule: use the imperative. Start with the verb. No “you”.

✅ Please wait here. ✅ Follow me. ✅ Turn left. ✅ Keep your ticket.

Instruction verb bank

Make it professional

Rewrite: “You go straight.”

Rewrite: “You wait here.”

Rewrite: “You follow me.”

❓ Grammar Spell 3 — How to formulate questions

Can + subject + verb?

Can I help you?

Do + subject + verb?

Do you need help?

Are + subject + -ing?

Are you looking for something?

WH + auxiliary + subject?

Where do you want to go?

Build the question: “you / need / help”

Build the question: “you / looking / for something”

Ask where something is.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Pronoun magic — his, her, their

his = for a man or boy. her = for a woman or girl. their = for several people.

My brother is here. ___ ticket is in his bag.

A little girl is lost. I need to help ___.

The guests are looking for ___ family.
