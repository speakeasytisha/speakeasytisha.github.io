1) Start

You will describe your volunteer work with injured wild animals: animals, injuries, tasks, and a simple procedure (arrival → care → transfer → release).

2) Lesson — vocabulary + grammar BEFORE practice

A) The basic procedure (simple English)

Step 1 Arrival: someone brings an injured animal.

Step 2 Record info: species, location, symptoms.

Step 3 First care: keep warm, hydrate, treat wounds.

Step 4 Monitor: food, breathing, stress.

Step 5 Transfer (sometimes): to a wildlife rescue center.

Step 6 Release: when it can survive in nature.

Useful sentence: “If it’s urgent, I alert the vet.”

Mini check (QCM)

B) Grammar tools you need (A2)

Routine: I clean cages. I monitor recovery.

Frequency: once a week • on weekends

Ability / rule: I can handle basic tasks. I can’t treat serious cases alone.

Comparative: more urgent than • bigger than

Superlative: the most rewarding • the best

Time clause: When it is ready, we release it.
