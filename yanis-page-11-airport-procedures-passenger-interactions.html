3) Grammar booster — procedures and polite service

Read the rule, study the examples, then write your own sentence.

Lisez la règle, étudiez les exemples, puis écrivez votre propre phrase.

A. Imperatives = short instructions

Rule: We use the base verb to give a short instruction or direction.

On utilise la base du verbe pour donner une instruction courte ou une direction.

✅ Please show your passport.

✅ Go to gate 12.

✅ Please wait here.

✅ Fasten your seat belt.

Compare:

Instruction: Please take your seat.

Statement: You take your seat. ❌ not natural here

B. Can I...? / May I...? / Let me...

Rule: Use these forms to offer help or act politely in a service situation.

Utilisez ces formes pour proposer de l’aide ou agir poliment dans une situation de service.

✅ Can I help you?

✅ May I see your boarding pass?

✅ Let me check the gate for you.

✅ Let me help you with your bag.

Compare:

More direct: Show me your passport.

More professional: May I see your passport, please?

C. Sequence words = clear procedures

Rule: Use sequence words to explain the steps clearly.

Utilisez des mots d’ordre pour expliquer les étapes clairement.

✅ First, check in online.

✅ Then, go through security.

✅ After that, wait at the gate.

✅ Finally, board the plane.
