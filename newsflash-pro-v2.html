2) Story selector — choose a real recent news story

Science • Reuters • March 5, 2026

Scientists grow chickpeas in simulated “moon dirt”

FR: Des scientifiques ont fait pousser des pois chiches dans un “sol lunaire” simulé.

A2+/B1

Headline → guess the story

Key question: Why is this unusual and important?

Use this before reading: ask the learner to predict where, why, and what for.

  • space farming
  • future missions
  • food production

Short summary

Researchers grew chickpeas in a soil mixture containing simulated moon dust. The test suggests that future astronauts may be able to grow food during long lunar missions.

Full summary

A research team working with Texas A&M used a climate-controlled chamber and a mixture of simulated lunar regolith, organic material, and fungi-coated seeds to grow chickpeas. The plants were able to grow in mixtures containing up to 75% simulated moon soil, although pure regolith did not work well. The experiment matters because long space missions will require more autonomy. Instead of sending all food from Earth, astronauts may eventually produce part of their own food supply.

Grammar focus

Present perfect: Scientists have grown chickpeas in simulated moon soil.

Modal of possibility: This discovery could help future lunar missions.

FR: présent perfect pour un résultat récent; could/may pour l’hypothèse.

Speaking angle

  • Would you trust food grown outside Earth?
  • What are the advantages and risks?
  • Is space research worth the cost?

Wildlife / Environment • AP • February 20, 2026

Giant tortoises return to Floreana Island in the Galápagos

FR: Des tortues géantes sont revenues sur l’île de Floreana aux Galápagos.

A2+/B1

Headline → guess the story

Key question: Is this only about animals, or also about ecosystems?

  • reintroduction
  • biodiversity
  • ecosystem restoration

Short summary

Juvenile giant tortoises with Floreana ancestry were released on their native island to help restore the local ecosystem.

Full summary

Conservation teams released 158 juvenile hybrid tortoises on Floreana Island, in Ecuador’s Galápagos archipelago. The project aims to rebuild a population linked to the original Floreana giant tortoise lineage and to restore the island’s ecological balance. Reintroducing these animals is not only symbolic: tortoises help shape vegetation and seed dispersal, so they can actively support ecosystem recovery over time.

Grammar focus

Present perfect passive: Tortoises have been released on Floreana Island.

Purpose language: They were released to help restore the ecosystem.

FR: voix passive très utile pour parler des actions scientifiques ou officielles.

Speaking angle

  • Can humans repair damage done to nature?
  • Should species be reintroduced?
  • What makes a conservation project successful?

History / Heritage • AP • March 5, 2026

Greece acquires rare photos showing Nazi executions in Athens

FR: La Grèce a acquis de rares photos montrant des exécutions nazies à Athènes.

B1/B2

Headline → guess the story

Key question: Why are archives emotionally and historically important?

  • memory
  • evidence
  • families and history

Short summary

Greece obtained a rare archive of wartime photographs showing the final moments of Greek prisoners executed by Nazi forces in 1944.

Full summary

The Greek state acquired a collection of 262 wartime photographs, including rare images linked to the execution of 200 prisoners at the Kaisariani firing range in Athens on May 1, 1944. For families, historians, and the wider public, the archive is more than a set of images. It preserves memory, may help identify victims, and documents a violent chapter of World War II with unusual visual evidence. The material is expected to be studied and digitised.

Grammar focus

Past simple: Greece acquired the archive in 2026.

Passive voice: The photographs were taken during the occupation.

FR: past simple pour les faits datés, passive pour mettre l’accent sur l’événement et non l’auteur.

Speaking angle

  • Why should painful history be preserved?
  • Can images change our understanding of the past?
  • Should schools use archives more often?

Climate / Europe • Reuters • March 5, 2026

EU countries approve a 2040 climate target

FR: Les pays de l’UE ont approuvé un objectif climatique pour 2040.

B1/B2

Headline → guess the story

Key question: Why can a climate target be both necessary and controversial?

  • emissions
  • policy
  • economic pressure

Short summary

EU countries gave final approval to a target to cut greenhouse gas emissions by 90% by 2040, although several states raised concerns about the economic impact.

Full summary

The European Union formally approved a legally binding 2040 climate target to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by 90% compared with 1990 levels. The agreement came after long negotiations and includes flexibility allowing a small share of the target to be met through international carbon credits. Supporters argue that the policy is needed for climate credibility and the EU’s net-zero pathway, while critics say the pace and cost could hurt industry and households.

Grammar focus

Reported information: Supporters say the target is necessary.

Opinion / advice: Governments should act faster on climate change.

FR: parfait pour exprimer un fait, puis un avis, un débat, ou une recommandation.

Speaking angle

  • Should climate action be faster?
  • How can governments protect both jobs and the environment?
  • What is a realistic target?

7) Write like a journalist

Mini article builder

Model structure

  1. Introduce the topic.
  2. Say what happened.
  3. Explain why it matters.
  4. Give your opinion.
  5. Suggest a consequence or next step.

According to the report, the tortoise reintroduction project is a hopeful sign for biodiversity. Conservation teams have released young tortoises on Floreana Island to help restore the ecosystem. This matters because species restoration can repair long-term environmental damage. In my opinion, similar projects should be supported in other places too. This could have important long-term consequences.

FR: idéal pour préparer l’oral, l’écrit, l’opinion, et le résumé journalistique.
