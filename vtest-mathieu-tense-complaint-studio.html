2) Tense lab

Which tense? Why that tense?

Present simple

Use it for facts, routines, neutral judgments, and general statements.

Example: The complaint is justified.

Here, is = present simple of to be, and justified works like an adjective.

Past simple / preterite

Use it for a finished action in the past.

Example: The company charged us 300 euros.

The action is finished and connected to a past moment.

Present perfect

Use it for a past action linked to the present result.

Example: We have not received a reply yet.

The situation started in the past but still matters now.

Would / could

Use these forms for polite requests, solutions, and suggestions.

Example: I would appreciate a refund.

This sounds professional and less aggressive.

Be + adjective or passive?

The complaint is justified.

This is best taught here as present simple with be + adjective.

The customer was charged unfairly.

This is a passive form in the past: someone charged the customer.

The company handled the situation badly.

This is past simple with a main verb + adverb.
