Grammar Masterclass

A / AN / THE / Ø — the travel logic

Important idea: articles are small words, but they change the meaning. We learn them with travel examples, not isolated rules.

Idée importante : les articles sont petits, mais ils changent le sens. On les apprend avec des situations de voyage.

Discovery first — try before the rule

1. I need ___ hotel.

2. I booked ___ apartment.

3. ___ hotel is near the station. (we know which hotel)

4. I speak ___ English.

A

Use A before one thing, first mention, consonant sound.

I need a hotel.

I would like a quiet room.

AN

Use AN before one thing, first mention, vowel sound.

I booked an apartment.

I need an umbrella.

THE

Use THE when we know which thing.

The hotel is near the station.

The room is quiet.

Ø Nothing

No article for languages, countries, activities in general, and general ideas.

I speak English.

I live in France.

I like travelling.

Step 1

A / AN — first mention

Use a or an when you mention one thing for the first time.

On utilise a/an quand on parle d'une chose pour la première fois.

UseExampleWhy?
aI need a hotel.hotel starts with a consonant sound
aI would like a room.one room, first mention
anI booked an apartment.apartment starts with a vowel sound
anI need an umbrella.umbrella starts with a vowel sound

Practice — A or AN

1. I would like ___ room.

2. I need ___ umbrella.

3. I am looking for ___ quiet hotel.

4. I booked ___ elegant apartment.

5. I would like ___ hotel near the station.

Step 2

THE — specific or known

Use the when you know exactly which thing you are talking about.
First mentionSecond mention
I booked a hotel.The hotel is near the station.
I have a room.The room is very quiet.

The station / the airport / the hotel: in travel situations, both people often know which place.

Practice — THE

1. I booked a hotel. ___ hotel is in York.

2. ___ station is five minutes away.

3. Is breakfast included in ___ price?

4. Could you call ___ hotel please?

Step 3

Ø — no article

This is often difficult for French speakers. In English, we often use no article with languages, countries, activities in general and general ideas.

C'est souvent difficile pour les francophones. En anglais, on ne met souvent pas d'article avec les langues, les pays et les activités en général.

Languages

I speak English.

Not: I speak the English.

Countries

I live in France.

Not: I live in the France.

Activities in general

I like travelling.

Not: I like the travelling.

Nature / general ideas

I love nature.

Not: I love the nature.

Practice — Ø nothing

1. I speak ___ English.

2. I live in ___ France.

3. I like ___ travelling.

4. I enjoy ___ African dance.

5. I love ___ nature.

Polite travel English

I would like...

I would like is more polite than I want. It is perfect for hotels, restaurants and travel agencies.
Iwould likea quiet room.
FormStructureExample
PositiveI would like + nounI would like a quiet room.
NegativeI would not like + nounI would not like a noisy room.
QuestionWould you like + noun?Would you like breakfast?

Practice — polite requests

1. I ___ a room with breakfast.

2. ___ you like a room with a sea view?

Listening challenge

Hotel booking dialogue

Task: Listen to the hotel conversation and answer the questions.

Receptionist: Good afternoon. How can I help you?

Nathalie: I would like a quiet room for two nights, please.

Receptionist: Of course. Would you like breakfast?

Nathalie: Yes, please. Is the hotel near the station?

Receptionist: Yes, the station is five minutes away.

1. What would Nathalie like?

2. What does the receptionist offer?

3. What is five minutes away?

CLOE oral mission

Speak for 60 seconds

Your task: Describe your destination, your hotel, your transport and your activities. Try to use a / an / the / Ø and I would like.

Model answer:

I am going to visit York because it is a historic city. I would like a quiet hotel near the station. The hotel should be comfortable and not too noisy. I usually travel by train, and I like visiting museums and walking around the city. I speak English when I travel, and I would like to feel more confident.

Travel journal writing

Write your hotel plan

Model paragraph

I am going to visit Bath. I would like a quiet hotel in the city centre. The hotel should be near the station because I usually travel by train. I like visiting historic places, and I enjoy walking around beautiful cities.
