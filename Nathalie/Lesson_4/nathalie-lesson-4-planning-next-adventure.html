🔢 Numbers & Ordinals for Travel

Use cardinal numbers for quantities and ordinal numbers for order, floors and dates.

Cardinal numbers

one, two, three, twenty-one

Use for prices, phone numbers, room numbers and platforms.

Ordinal numbers

first, second, third, fourth

Use for dates and floors: the 1st floor, the 21st of June.

Main rule

Most ordinal numbers end in -th: 4th, 5th, 6th.

Exceptions

1st first, 2nd second, 3rd third, 5th fifth, 8th eighth, 9th ninth, 12th twelfth.

✈️ Future Plans with GOING TO

Use going to for plans and intentions. Start with one verb: travel.

Iamgoing totravel

Affirmative

You are going to travel.

She is going to travel.

Negative

I am not going to travel.

She is not going to travel.

Question

Are you going to travel?

Is she going to travel?

Answer

Yes, I am.

No, I am not.

Going to — gradual practice

🚆 Travel Prepositions with Going To

travel by

by train, by plane, by ferry, by bus

I am going to travel by train.

travel to / go to

to Scotland, to Guadeloupe, to London

I am going to travel to Guadeloupe.

stay in

in a hotel, in Edinburgh, in London

I am going to stay in a hotel.

arrive at / in

at the station, at the airport, in Edinburgh

I am going to arrive at the station.
