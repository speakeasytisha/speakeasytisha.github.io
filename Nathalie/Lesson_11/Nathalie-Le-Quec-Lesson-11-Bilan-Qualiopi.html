Nathalie Lesson 11: Describing Places, Pictures & Everyday Situations Discussion: Original: They like to come to the house very often. Better: They like to come to the house quite often. OR They often come to the house. ("Very often" is correct, but "quite often" or "often" sounds more natural.) Original: My granddaughter is taking a nap. You could also say: My granddaughter is having a nap. (Very common in British English.) Vocabulary cobblestone = pavé (correct) pedestrian street = rue piétonne (correct) pavement (UK) = trottoir sidewalk (US) = trottoir bitumen/asphalt = bitume So "pavement" does not mean bitume in everyday English. In British English, it means the place where pedestrians walk. Edinburgh Castle A2: I can see a castle on a hill. It is old and beautiful. There are many tourists. B1: This is a historic castle in Scotland. It looks very impressive, and there are a lot of tourists visiting it. I think it is a good place to learn about Scottish history. Nathalie: In the background, I can see a castle. There is a garden around it/There is a garden at the bottom of the hill. There are a lot of beautiful flowers with different colours. In the foreground, two people are walking next to a fountain. It’s not crowded and it looks very peaceful. Corrected Version In the background, I can see a castle. There is a garden surrounding it, and there are many beautiful flowers of different colours. In the foreground, two people are walking next to a fountain. It isn't crowded, and it looks very peaceful. Notes "There is a garden around it" → Correct. "There is a garden at the bottom of the hill" → Excellent! "beautiful flowers with different colours" → Better: "beautiful flowers of different colours" "beautiful, colourful flowers" "It's not crowded" → Perfect. B1+ Version In the background, I can see a historic castle on a hill. At the bottom of the hill, there is a beautiful garden with colourful flowers. In the foreground, two people are walking near a fountain. The atmosphere seems very peaceful, and I think it would be a lovely place to visit. Lake District A2: I can see a lake and mountains. People are walking. It looks peaceful. B1: This place looks very peaceful. There is a lake in the foreground and mountains in the background. Some people are walking and enjoying nature. Nathalie: I can see a lake. In the background, there is a hill/mountain. In the foreground, there is a lot of vegetation/there are many trees. This place is worth visiting because it looks very peaceful. Corrected Version I can see a lake. In the background, there is a mountain. In the foreground, there is a lot of vegetation and many trees. This place is worth visiting because it looks very peaceful. Notes "there is a lot of vegetation" → Perfect. "there are many trees" → Perfect. "This place is worth visiting..." → Excellent CLOE expression. B1+ Version I can see a beautiful lake surrounded by mountains. In the foreground, there is a lot of vegetation and many trees. The landscape looks very peaceful and relaxing. I think this place is worth visiting because it would be a perfect destination for people who enjoy nature and hiking. Comments You are doing an excellent job with these exercises because you are naturally using: In the foreground... In the background... There is... There are... It looks... This place is worth visiting... because... These are exactly the structures that CLOE examiners expect to hear during picture description activities. The biggest improvement is that you are no longer giving one-sentence answers. You are now producing small paragraphs with connectors, opinions, and descriptive vocabulary, which is a major step forward.🏆
