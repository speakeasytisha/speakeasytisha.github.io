3) Greetings and addressing people

Learn how to greet someone in the street in a natural way and how to sound polite.

🌸 Polite / neutral

  • Hello
  • Good morning
  • Good afternoon
  • Good evening
  • Excuse me
  • Nice to meet you

Good for new people, shops, hotels, and polite street interactions.

☀️ Casual / friendly

  • Hi
  • Hi there
  • How are you?
  • See you

Good for relaxed conversation when the situation feels friendly.

Best advice: Start neutral or polite with a new person, then become more casual if the conversation feels relaxed.

💬 “Hello, how are you?” — useful answers

  • I’m fine, thank you. And you?
  • I’m good, thank you. And you?
  • I’m very well, thank you. How are you?
  • I’m doing well, thank you. How about you?
  • I’m fine, thank you. How about yourself?

🛍️ In a shop or store

Staff: How are you folks doing today?

You: Fine, thank you. How are you?

In the U.S., this is often polite small talk. With someone you do not know, people usually keep the answer short and positive.

Common light topics are the weather, traffic, weekend plans, or local life. In the U.K., this is similar, and the weather is especially common.

Note: You may hear Hey between friends or people who already know each other well, but it is better to teach Hello, Good morning, or Good evening as your main reference forms.
