Step 2

Sentence surgery: where to correct and why

Open each panel and read the three versions. Notice how the polished version becomes shorter, clearer and more useful to the recruiter.

Your draft

“I was drawn by its international real estate network and the broad range of services you can offer related to a high level client-focused approach.”

Clearer

I was drawn to your international real estate network, the broad range of services you offer and your high-level client-focused approach.

Polished

I was particularly drawn to your international real estate network, the breadth of your services and your high-quality, client-focused approach.

Why: We say drawn to. Use the services you offer, not “services you can offer.” Breadth of services is a concise professional phrase. In this context, high-quality sounds more natural than high level.
Your draft

“My-long standing interest in property led me to study law and later specialize in urban planning and construction law.”

Clearer

My long-standing interest in property led me to study law and later specialise in urban planning and construction law.

Polished

My long-standing interest in property led me to study law and later specialise in urban planning and construction law, with the aim of building a career in real estate.

Why: Long-standing needs a hyphen, but not after my. The polished version finishes the idea: your studies were part of a deliberate career choice, not simply a list of subjects.
Your draft

“My native French, conversational Spanish and continuing development of professional English and conversational Dutch support my integration into an international real estate environment.”

Clearer

As a native French speaker with conversational Spanish, I am actively developing my professional English and conversational Dutch.

Polished

As a native French speaker with conversational Spanish, I am actively strengthening my professional English and developing conversational Dutch as I prepare to integrate into an international real estate environment.

Why: The letter should be truthful and easy to understand. Actively strengthening shows commitment without claiming a level that you do not yet feel comfortable using in an interview.
Your draft

“My practical and client-focused approach to contract negotiation, and clients advisory would enable me to contribute effectively to Sotheby’s real estate and quality business objectives.”

Clearer

My practical, client-focused approach to contract negotiation and client advice would enable me to contribute effectively to your real estate and business objectives.

Polished

My practical, client-focused approach to contract negotiation and client advice would enable me to contribute effectively to your client-service and commercial objectives.

Why: The noun is client advice, not clients advisory. Remove the comma after negotiation: two noun phrases are joined with and. Client-service and commercial objectives is more meaningful than quality business objectives.
Your draft

“My property-development experience is also relevant to new-build consultancy.”

Clearer

My property-development experience would also be relevant to new-build consultancy.

Polished

My property-development experience would also allow me to support new-build consultancy and the coordination of complex property projects.

Why: Would be relevant sounds appropriately professional in a speculative application. The polished version explains how that experience could be useful.
Step 3

Grammar patterns for premium professional English

These short patterns will help you write clearly without trying to translate French sentence by sentence.

Dear + title + surname

Use Mr or Ms in a formal letter when you know the recipient.

Dear Ms Reerink, → closing: Yours sincerely,

Long-standing + noun

Use a hyphen before the noun.

My long-standing interest in property led me to study law.

Experience in + area / activity

Use experience in for a field or activity.

I have experience in contract negotiation and property development.

Would enable me to + verb

Use this to connect your skills to a potential contribution.

My client-focused approach would enable me to contribute effectively.
Step 4

Vocabulary bank — international client-facing real estate

Open each word to see the French translation, meaning and a model that fits a cover letter, networking conversation or interview.

Client-focused · orienté client

Definition: designed around understanding and responding to clients’ needs.
Example: I take a practical, client-focused approach to property projects.

Client advice · conseil client

Definition: professional guidance provided to clients.
Example: My experience combines contract negotiation and client advice.

International real estate network · réseau immobilier international

Definition: professional property connections across countries or markets.
Example: I am motivated by the opportunity to work in an international real estate network.

New-build consultancy · conseil en immobilier neuf

Definition: advice and support relating to newly built properties or developments.
Example: My property-development experience is relevant to new-build consultancy.

Property development · promotion immobilière

Definition: planning, funding, building or redeveloping real estate projects.
Example: I have worked in property development from both legal and commercial perspectives.

Discretion · discrétion / confidentialité

Definition: the ability to handle information and relationships carefully and confidentially.
Example: I understand the importance of discretion in client relationships.

Tailored advice · conseil sur mesure

Definition: advice adapted to a client’s specific needs.
Example: I listen carefully before offering tailored advice.

Stakeholder coordination · coordination des parties prenantes

Definition: organising communication and action between people involved in a project.
Example: I am experienced in stakeholder coordination across complex property projects.

Commercial objectives · objectifs commerciaux

Definition: goals related to business performance, sales or revenue.
Example: My legal advice is informed by practical commercial objectives.

Step 6

Final polished speculative application

This is a professional template based on your draft. Verify the recipient’s title and the exact name of the organisation before sending. The first contact remains focused on your experience; practical placement arrangements can be discussed later if appropriate.

Subject: Speculative application — Real Estate Legal & Commercial Professional

Dear Ms Reerink,

I am writing to express my interest in possible opportunities at Sotheby’s.

After reviewing your website, I was particularly drawn to your international real estate network, the breadth of your services and your high-quality, client-focused approach.

My long-standing interest in property led me to study law and later specialise in urban planning and construction law, with the aim of building a career in real estate. For the past 24 years, I have worked in the French real estate sector, combining sales management, property development, contract negotiation and legal advisory work.

In my previous roles, I drafted and negotiated real estate development contracts, supported operational teams and worked with external partners, including notaries, lawyers, investors and landlords. My practical, client-focused approach to contract negotiation and client advice would enable me to contribute effectively to your client-service and commercial objectives. My property-development experience would also allow me to support new-build consultancy and the coordination of complex property projects.

As a native French speaker with conversational Spanish, I am actively strengthening my professional English and developing conversational Dutch as I prepare to integrate into an international real estate environment.

I am now seeking a new professional opportunity in the Netherlands and would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my legal and commercial experience could support your team, either in an immediate role or in a future opportunity aligned with my profile.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Yours sincerely,
Isabelle Davion

Step 7

Application email — short, polished and easy to send

The email is not another cover letter. It is a clear professional message that tells the reader what is attached and why you are writing.

Subject: Speculative application — Real Estate Legal & Commercial Professional

Dear Ms Reerink,

Please find attached my speculative application and CV for possible opportunities at Sotheby’s in the Netherlands.

With more than 24 years of experience in the French real estate sector, combining legal advisory work, sales management, contract negotiation and property development, I would be pleased to explore how my profile could support your team.

Thank you for your time and consideration. I would welcome the opportunity to speak with you.

Yours sincerely,
Isabelle Davion

Step 8

Turn the letter into networking and interview English

Practise as a conversation, not as a written letter. Begin with the cue words; then reveal the model only after you have tried.

Why this type of real estate environment?

Cue: international · client relationships · property · quality service

I am interested in an international real estate environment because I enjoy combining a legal and commercial perspective with client relationships. I am particularly motivated by roles where professional advice, negotiation and understanding clients’ needs all contribute to a high-quality property service.

How do you work with clients?

Cue: listen · understand needs · risks · tailored advice

I listen carefully to clients before making recommendations. This helps me understand their needs, identify possible risks and offer tailored, practical advice. My legal background allows me to be precise, while my commercial experience helps me keep the client’s wider objectives in mind.

How will you integrate internationally?

Cue: French · Spanish · English development · Dutch · commitment

As a native French speaker with conversational Spanish, I am already used to working across languages and professional cultures. I am actively strengthening my professional English and developing my Dutch. I am committed to becoming fully effective in a Dutch professional environment.
