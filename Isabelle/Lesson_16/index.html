Step 2

Sentence surgery: correct it, understand it, reuse it

Open each panel. Read your starting idea, then compare it with the clearer and polished versions. The “why” box tells you exactly what to notice.

Your draft

“I was drawn by the sustainable, circular and reuse principes which guide the orientation of your company.”

Clearer

I was drawn to the sustainable, circular and reuse principles that guide your company’s approach.

Polished

I was particularly drawn to the principles of sustainability, circularity and reuse that guide your approach.

Why: We say drawn to something. In formal English, a list of related ideas is clearer as the principles of sustainability, circularity and reuse. Approach is more natural than the orientation of your company.
Your draft

“Your projects show the anticipated vision you have about environmental thematics.”

Clearer

Your projects show a forward-looking vision for environmental issues.

Polished

I was impressed by the forward-looking environmental vision reflected in your projects and by the positive impact thoughtful area development can have on communities and everyday life.

Why: Anticipated vision and environmental thematics sound translated. Use forward-looking vision and environmental issues. The polished version also adds a professional, human reason for your interest.
Your draft

“Your principes match with my own environmental values and actions.”

Clearer

Your principles align with my own environmental values and professional actions.

Polished

This approach aligns with my own values and with the sustainable solutions I have supported in area-development projects.

Why: In professional English, align with is the natural verb for values, goals and approaches. The polished sentence moves from a general value statement to evidence from your professional experience.
Your draft

“I worked on aera development projects including reusing rain waters for others uses such as gardens and toilets.”

Clearer

I worked on area-development projects that included the reuse of rainwater for gardens and toilet flushing.

Polished

As a specialist in urban planning and construction law, I worked on area-development projects that incorporated sustainable solutions, including rainwater reuse for irrigation and toilet flushing.

Why: Use area-development as a compound adjective. Rainwater is one word. Toilet flushing is accurate and professional; it is clearer than “for toilets.”
Your draft

“I contributed to setting up innovating concepts in building projects such as premises or car sharing for the residents.”

Clearer

I contributed to developing innovative concepts in building projects, such as shared facilities and car-sharing for residents.

Polished

I also contributed to the development of innovative, resident-focused concepts, including shared facilities and car-sharing initiatives with environmental and social benefits.

Why: Use innovative, not innovating, before a noun. Shared facilities is a more natural phrase than premises here. Resident-focused gives the idea a clearer professional value.
Step 3

Grammar patterns you can reuse in future applications

Do not memorise whole letters. Memorise reliable structures that let you write your own ideas accurately.

Drawn to + noun phrase

Use this to show genuine professional interest.

I was particularly drawn to the principles of sustainability and circularity.

Align with + values / objectives

Use this for values, approaches and priorities.

This approach aligns with my own values and experience.

Contribute to + noun

Use this to state your value without overclaiming.

I would be pleased to contribute to your environmental and social objectives.

Would enable me to + verb

Use this to connect your experience to the employer’s needs.

My background would enable me to support complex development projects.
Step 4

Vocabulary bank — sustainable area development

Open each item to see the French translation, a concise definition and a model sentence that belongs in an application or interview.

Sustainability · durabilité

Definition: meeting present needs while protecting resources and future needs.
Example: Sustainability is central to the way I approach long-term real estate projects.

Circularity · économie circulaire

Definition: designing systems that keep materials and resources in use for longer.
Example: I am interested in circularity and reuse in property development.

Reuse · réemploi / réutilisation

Definition: using something again rather than discarding it.
Example: The project included practical solutions for rainwater reuse.

Area development · aménagement urbain

Definition: planning and delivering an integrated urban area or neighbourhood.
Example: My experience includes legal support for area-development projects.

Rainwater harvesting · récupération des eaux de pluie

Definition: collecting rainwater for later use.
Example: Rainwater harvesting can support irrigation and toilet flushing.

Shared facilities · espaces / équipements partagés

Definition: amenities used by residents or users collectively.
Example: Shared facilities can create practical and social value for residents.

Car-sharing initiative · dispositif d’autopartage

Definition: a scheme in which several people share access to vehicles.
Example: I supported resident-focused concepts, including car-sharing initiatives.

Stakeholder · partie prenante

Definition: a person or organisation affected by or involved in a project.
Example: I am used to coordinating with stakeholders with different priorities.

Forward-looking · tourné vers l’avenir

Definition: focused on future needs and opportunities.
Example: I was impressed by your forward-looking approach to sustainable development.

Step 6

Final polished speculative application

This is a model you can adapt. Before sending, confirm the recipient’s preferred title and any company-specific details. The placement paragraph is deliberately omitted from the main letter: first, present your senior professional value.

Subject: Speculative application — Real Estate Legal & Commercial Professional

Dear Mr Roeven,

I am writing to express my interest in possible opportunities at AM.

After reviewing AM’s website, I was particularly drawn to the principles of sustainability, circularity and reuse that guide your approach. I was also impressed by the forward-looking environmental vision reflected in your projects and by the positive impact that thoughtful area development can have on communities and everyday life.

I am a real estate legal and commercial professional with over 24 years’ experience in the French property sector. My background combines legal advisory work for major property and area developers with hands-on commercial experience in sales management, contract negotiation and client relations. In my previous roles, I drafted and negotiated real estate development contracts, supported operational teams and liaised with external partners, including notaries, lawyers, investors and landlords.

As a specialist in urban planning and construction law, I worked on area-development projects that incorporated sustainable solutions, including rainwater reuse for irrigation and toilet flushing. I also contributed to the development of innovative, resident-focused concepts, including shared facilities and car-sharing initiatives with environmental and social benefits.

I would bring a practical, business-oriented approach to contract negotiation, risk assessment and coordination across internal teams and external stakeholders. My legal and commercial background would enable me to contribute effectively to AM’s real estate, environmental and social objectives.

I am now seeking a new professional opportunity in the Netherlands and would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my experience could support your team, either in an immediate role or in a future opportunity aligned with my profile.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Yours sincerely,
Isabelle Davion

Step 7

Turn the letter into interview answers

First answer from cue cards. Then reveal the model. Aim for 45–90 seconds; do not try to repeat the full letter.

Why AM?

Cue: sustainability · circularity · area development · community impact

I am interested in AM because I am drawn to the way sustainability, circularity and reuse can shape real estate projects. My own experience in urban planning and construction law has shown me that thoughtful area development can create practical environmental and social benefits for residents and communities.

What can you bring?

Cue: legal + commercial · contracts · risks · stakeholders

I bring a dual legal and commercial perspective developed over more than 24 years in real estate. I am comfortable with contract negotiation, risk assessment and coordination between internal teams and external partners. I also understand the importance of turning legal advice into practical support for a project.

Give one sustainable example.

Cue: rainwater reuse · shared facilities · car-sharing · residents

In area-development projects, I have supported practical sustainable solutions, including rainwater reuse for irrigation and toilet flushing. I have also contributed to resident-focused concepts such as shared facilities and car-sharing initiatives. These projects showed me how legal, practical and social considerations can work together.
