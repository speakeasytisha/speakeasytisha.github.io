Step 1

🧭 What is this type of application called?

For a letter sent to an agency when no specific vacancy has been advertised, use one of the following professional terms.

Best choice for Amsterdam

Speculative application

FR: candidature spontanée

Professional and common in British and European English.

Subject: Speculative application – Real Estate Legal & Commercial Professional

Unsolicited application

FR: candidature non sollicitée

Clear and correct in international English, especially in North American contexts.

I am writing to submit an unsolicited application for future opportunities.

Open application

FR: candidature ouverte

Often seen on Dutch company career pages. Use it when the company itself uses this wording.

Open application – Residential property and client relations.

Step 3

🧩 The four-paragraph blueprint

For an agency in the Netherlands, keep the letter to one page. Use four short paragraphs: agency connection → your experience → your value and availability → clear closing.

1

Why this agency?

Show one or two specific reasons why the agency interests you.

2

What is your evidence?

Choose your most relevant legal, sales and property-development experience.

3

What can you bring?

Explain how you listen, advise, coordinate and support clients or teams.

4

What is the next step?

Ask for a conversation or future consideration — confidently and politely.

Structure challenge

Give each paragraph a different number. Follow the same sequence as the blueprint above.

Recommended order: 1. Agency connection  →  2. Relevant experience  →  3. Value you bring  →  4. Clear next step

Step 4

🧠 Grammar studio: natural professional patterns

These patterns help you write naturally and explain your experience with confidence. They are not about “finding mistakes”; they are tools you can reuse in future applications.

1. Having explored… / After reviewing…

Use: Having + past participle or After + -ing to introduce research you completed before writing.

✅ Having explored your website, I was particularly interested in your new-build consultancy.

✅ After reviewing your services, I was impressed by the breadth of your residential and commercial activities.

2. Interest in + noun / -ing

Use: interested in + noun or interested in + verb-ing.

✅ I am interested in property development.

✅ I am interested in working in a client-focused real estate agency.

3. With over… years’ experience / I have worked for… years

Use: with over + number + years’ experience in for a powerful CV-style opening. Use for + duration with a verb.

✅ With over 24 years’ experience in real estate, I bring legal and commercial expertise.

✅ I have worked in the French property sector for 24 years.

4. Specialise in / experience in + noun or -ing

Use: specialise in + noun and experience in + noun / -ing.

✅ I later specialised in urban planning and construction law.

✅ I have experience in negotiating contracts and coordinating external partners.

5. Listen carefully to + person / offer + advice

Use: listen carefully to + person and offer / give advice. Advice is a noun; advise is the verb.

✅ I listen carefully to clients before making recommendations.

✅ I offer practical and appropriate advice.

✅ I advise clients on real estate matters.

6. Would welcome the opportunity to…

Use: would welcome the opportunity to + verb for a confident professional close.

✅ I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my experience could support your team.

✅ I would be pleased to provide any further information you may require.

Dear Ms Rikken - ’t Hart,

Having explored Broersma’s website, I was immediately drawn to its distinctive style, broad range of services and client-focused approach. Your work across residential property, lettings and new-build consultancy particularly resonates with my legal and commercial background in real estate.

For the past 24 years, I have worked in the French real estate sector, first as a Sales Manager and later as a Real Estate Legal Specialist. I studied law and later specialised in urban planning and construction law. Throughout my career, I have developed experience in property development, sales management, contract negotiation, risk analysis and coordination with clients, notaries, lawyers, investors and operational teams.

I listen carefully to clients and stakeholders in order to understand their needs, anticipate risks and offer appropriate advice. My legal and commercial background allows me to approach real estate projects from both a client-focused and a practical perspective. I am now seeking an opportunity to build the next stage of my career in the Amsterdam area, where I can contribute to an agency that values tailored advice, property expertise and long-term client relationships.

Please find my CV attached for further details of my experience. I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my profile could support Broersma’s team, either in an immediate role or in a future opportunity aligned with my experience.

Yours sincerely,

Isabelle Davion

Why this works: the letter gives evidence, shows genuine company research, keeps the relocation explanation brief and ends with a practical next step.

Step 8

🌱 Optional placement pathway: clear and careful language

For an experienced professional, internship can sound too student-focused. Use short professional immersion placement or short work-experience placement instead.

Best strategy

  • Do not lead with the placement in the first sentence.
  • First show your professional value and interest in the agency.
  • Mention a placement only as an optional first step.
  • Confirm the exact programme, duration and agreement with France Travail before sending details.

Safe optional wording

If relevant, I would also be open to a short professional immersion placement supported by a French employment programme, as an initial opportunity to gain first-hand experience of the Dutch real estate market. I would be pleased to provide practical details.

Important: do not write that the placement is free of charge, lasts one to four weeks, or creates no obligations for the agency until the relevant France Travail programme has confirmed those exact conditions in writing.
