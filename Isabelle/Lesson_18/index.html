Step 1

Networking: what it really means

Networking is not asking everyone for a job. It is making professional connections, sharing information and becoming known for your expertise. In the Netherlands, networking matters: EURES notes that more than 60% of vacancies are filled informally.

01 · Build

Professional relationships

Reconnect with former colleagues, managers, clients, lawyers, notaries, investors and sector contacts — always respecting confidentiality.

02 · Learn

Market intelligence

Ask what is changing in Dutch real estate, which skills are valued and how teams operate. A useful question is more powerful than a generic request.

03 · Be remembered

Your value in one sentence

Be ready to describe your dual profile: legal advisory work, commercial judgement, property development and stakeholder coordination.

Professional principle: start with curiosity and shared professional value. “I would appreciate your perspective on the Dutch property market” sounds more confident than “Do you know of any job for me?”

Step 2

French and Dutch professional culture

Think of these as useful tendencies, not fixed rules. The best strategy is to observe the company, listen carefully and adapt your level of formality. Dutch workplaces commonly value direct communication, planning, independence and relatively flat hierarchies.

Communication and feedback

In France, you may be used to: more formal wording, nuance and softer disagreement — particularly at the start of a relationship.

In the Netherlands, you may often hear: direct questions and frank feedback. It is usually intended to create clarity, not to be personal.

Hierarchy and first names

In France, you may be used to: titles, clearer hierarchy and a more formal professional distance.

In the Netherlands, you may often find: first names, easier access to managers and expectation that you share your view.

Time and preparation

In France, you may be used to: relationship-building and context before the central point.

In the Netherlands, you may often find: punctual meetings, an agenda and a quicker move to the key question or decision.

Decision-making

In France, you may be used to: a stronger link between responsibility, role and authority.

In the Netherlands, you may often find: discussion, consultation and consensus — while each person is expected to prepare and contribute independently.

Your Dutch-style professional formula: be warm, brief and specific. State your point, give a reason, ask one clear question, and confirm the next step.

Step 3

Build your networking map

Start with people who already know the quality of your work. A strong network is built in layers — not in one day.

Layer 1

Trusted former contacts

Former managers, colleagues and professional partners who can describe how you work.

Layer 2

Sector connections

Notaries, lawyers, architects, developers, investors, consultants and property specialists.

Layer 3

Dutch market contacts

Recruiters, agencies, professional associations and contacts in international property companies.

Layer 4

New connections

LinkedIn contacts, alumni, event conversations and informational interviews.

Reconnect with a trusted former contact

You are not asking them to find you a job. You are briefly sharing your direction and asking for insight or a conversation.

Hello [Name], I hope you are well. I am currently exploring opportunities in the Netherlands, where I would like to bring my legal and commercial real-estate experience to an international environment. I would be very happy to reconnect and would value your perspective on my project. Would you be available for a short call in the coming weeks? Kind regards, Isabelle

Ask for a 15-minute informational conversation

Be precise. Mention why this person is relevant and make the request easy to accept or decline.

Hello [Name], I am exploring real-estate opportunities in the Netherlands, particularly roles combining legal, commercial and transaction-related responsibilities. Given your experience in [company / sector], I would appreciate a short 15-minute conversation to understand how the market is evolving. I am not asking you to find me a role; I would simply value your perspective. Would that be possible next week? Best regards, Isabelle

Send a personalised LinkedIn request

Do not send an empty request to a recruiter or hiring manager. A short note creates context and shows professionalism.

Hello [Name], I am a French real estate legal and commercial professional exploring opportunities in the Netherlands. I was interested in your work in [company / property sector] and would be pleased to connect. Best regards, Isabelle
Step 4

Make LinkedIn work for you

LinkedIn is your digital professional introduction. Before reaching out, make sure the headline, profile summary and experience sections show the same professional story.

Headline formula

Professional identity | key expertise | market direction

Model: Real Estate Legal & Commercial Professional | Property Development, Contracts, Transactions & Stakeholder Coordination | Open to opportunities in the Netherlands

About-section opening

Experience + dual value + current direction

Model: I am a real estate legal and commercial professional with over 24 years’ experience in the French property sector. I combine legal advisory work, property-development expertise and sales-management experience.

Step 5

Networking conversation: speak without a script

Your goal is not to tell your entire career story. Give a short professional introduction, ask thoughtful questions and listen carefully.

1. Open the conversation

Thank them · one-sentence project · respectful purpose

Thank you for taking the time to speak with me. I am exploring real estate opportunities in the Netherlands, where I would like to use my legal and commercial experience in an international environment. I would really value your perspective on the market.

2. Explain your value

24 years · legal + commercial · contracts · stakeholders

I have over 24 years of experience in the French property sector. My background combines legal advisory work for developers, contract negotiation, risk analysis, sales management and coordination with a range of stakeholders.

3. Ask a useful question

market · company type · language · future opportunities

From your perspective, which types of company or role could benefit most from a profile combining legal real-estate knowledge and commercial experience? And how important is Dutch from the first day in those environments?

Step 6

Networking vocabulary you can reuse

Open each card. Say the example aloud, then adapt it to your own background.

Networking · réseau professionnel

Definition: building professional relationships over time.
Example: Networking is an important part of my job-search strategy.

Professional contact · contact professionnel

Definition: a person you know through work or a professional community.
Example: I am reconnecting with former professional contacts.

Referral · recommandation / mise en relation

Definition: a recommendation or introduction that may lead to an opportunity.
Example: Some roles are filled through referrals before they are advertised.

Informational interview · entretien informatif

Definition: a short conversation to learn about a market, role or company.
Example: I would appreciate an informational interview about the Dutch market.

Mutual connection · relation en commun

Definition: a person connected to both of you on LinkedIn or professionally.
Example: I noticed that we have a mutual connection in the property sector.

Open to opportunities · ouverte aux opportunités

Definition: interested in hearing about suitable roles, without sounding desperate.
Example: I am open to opportunities aligned with my experience.

Market insight · compréhension du marché

Definition: useful knowledge about a sector or market.
Example: I would value your market insight on Dutch real estate.

Introduce / introduction · présenter / mise en relation

Definition: connect someone professionally to another person.
Example: Would you feel comfortable introducing me to a recruiter in this sector?

Follow up · relancer / faire un suivi

Definition: contact someone again about a previous conversation or action.
Example: I am writing to follow up on our conversation last week.
