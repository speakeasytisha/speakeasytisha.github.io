CV title workshop

Choose a title that is clear and safe ⚖️

In English, a professional title must be accurate. Some titles are stronger because they avoid confusion and immediately show your double competence.

Recommended main title

Real Estate Legal & Commercial Professional

Why it works: it shows your legal background, your commercial experience and your real estate sector clearly.

Use with care

Real Estate Lawyer

Why we are careful: in English, “lawyer” can suggest a qualified solicitor, attorney or avocat. If that is not your exact status, a safer option is better.

Real Estate Legal Specialist

Useful for your legal expertise in property development.

Real Estate Legal Counsel

Professional, but use only if it matches the responsibilities and context.

Sales Manager – Property Development

Good for your commercial / sales management experience.

Property Development Professional

Broader and useful if applying to real estate companies.

Profile upgrade

Your CV profile: clear, concise, international ✨

The profile is the first thing a recruiter reads. It should quickly answer: Who are you? What is your value? What are you looking for?

Current French-style idea

With my dual expertise in both the legal and commercial aspects of the property sector during 25 years, I am determined to take on a new professional challenge in the Netherlands.

Clearer English

I have dual legal and commercial expertise in the property sector, with over 24 years of experience, and I am looking for a new professional challenge in the Netherlands.

Professional CV version

Real estate legal and commercial professional with over 24 years’ experience in the French property sector, combining legal advisory work, property development expertise and sales management experience.

Professional profile model

Real estate legal and commercial professional with over 24 years’ experience in the French property sector, including legal advisory work for major property developers and sales management responsibilities. Skilled in real estate contracts, property development operations, negotiation, risk prevention, client relations and coordination with notaries, lawyers, investors and operational teams. Now seeking a new professional opportunity in the Netherlands, ideally in a real estate agency or a property-related legal position.

Grammar that helps your CV

Natural English patterns for international CVs 🔍

Each rule is followed by practice, so you can use the pattern immediately.

Rule 1

Start CV bullet points with action verbs

English CVs often sound stronger when bullet points begin with verbs in the past simple.

French-style noun phrase: Drafting and negotiation of contracts

International CV English: Drafted and negotiated real estate development contracts.

Choose the best action verb

_____ operational teams on real estate law and risk prevention.

Rule 2

Use “for” or “with over” for experience

When you talk about a duration, use for + period of time. In a CV profile, with over + number + years’ experience sounds concise and professional.

I worked as a sales manager for 10 years.

Real estate professional with over 24 years’ experience.

Complete the sentence

I worked in legal departments 14 years.

Rule 3

Use the right professional word: advice / advise

Advice is the noun. Advise is the verb.

I give legal advice to clients.

I advise clients on real estate matters.

Choose the correct word

I would like to _____ clients professionally in English.

Rule 4

Use professional nouns and avoid false friends

Some words look similar to French but are used differently in English.

Convention→ agreement
Formation→ training / education
Validation→ review / approval
Particuliers→ private clients

Technical translation lab

VEFA, BEFA and legal real estate terms 🏗️

You do not need to translate everything word-for-word. The goal is to explain technical terms simply and professionally.

VEFA

Best English: off-plan sale / sale before completion

Simple explanation: a sale signed before the property is completed.

BEFA

Best English: off-plan lease / lease before completion

Simple explanation: a lease signed before the premises are completed.

Permis de construire

English: building permit / planning permission

Simple explanation: official permission to build or develop a property.

Étude d’impact

English: environmental impact assessment

Simple explanation: a study of how a project may affect the environment.
