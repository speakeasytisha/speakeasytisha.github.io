Step 3

🧠 Grammar for clear interview answers

These patterns help you connect past experience, current strengths and future motivation accurately.

1. Present perfect + for: professional experience until now

Use have / has + past participle with for + duration when the experience began in the past and remains relevant now.

✅ I have worked in real estate for over 24 years.

✅ I have developed strong experience in contract negotiation.

2. Past simple: a finished role or achievement

Use the past simple for a role that ended or for a completed achievement.

✅ I managed property sales across France.

✅ I drafted and negotiated real estate contracts.

3. Why this role? Use because + a clear reason

Use because + subject + verb to give a complete reason. Use because of + noun before a noun phrase.

✅ I am interested in this role because it combines legal expertise and business strategy.

✅ I am interested in the role because of its international scope.

Step 5

🇳🇱 Motivation, relocation and Dutch: speak with honesty and confidence

The role asks for fluent Dutch and English. You do not need to hide the fact that you are learning Dutch. You do need to show a realistic, professional plan and make your immediate value clear.

Professional principle

Lead with what you can contribute now. Then explain what you are actively developing.

“I can contribute immediately through my legal and commercial real estate experience, my English communication skills and my ability to work with international stakeholders.”

Honest Dutch answer

“I am currently learning Dutch and I am committed to developing my professional Dutch skills. I understand that this will be essential for long-term integration in the Dutch market.”

Choose the strongest answer

Which answer handles the Dutch-language requirement most professionally?

Step 7

☎️ First recruiter call: live practice

Imagine that a recruiter calls after reading your CV. Keep the answer short, friendly and structured. Use the cue cards first, then practise without them.

Recruiter

Hello Isabelle, thank you for your application. Could you briefly introduce yourself and tell me why this position interests you?

Your cue card
  • 24+ years in French real estate
  • legal advisory + sales management
  • contracts, transactions, stakeholders
  • international Netherlands project
Thank you for calling. I am a real estate legal and commercial professional with over 24 years of experience in the French property sector. My background combines legal advisory work for major property developers with sales management, contract negotiation and transaction support. I am particularly interested in this position because it brings together real estate transactions, business strategy and stakeholder coordination. I am now looking to develop my career in the Netherlands and contribute this dual perspective in an international environment.

Step 8

✍️ 8-minute writing sprint: your interview introduction

Write 110–150 words. Your aim is not perfection. Use the three-part structure: now → evidence → next step.

Prompt: Write a short professional introduction for a first interview with Holland Immo Group. Explain who you are, what evidence you bring, and why this role in the Netherlands interests you.
08:00

Model answer

I am a real estate legal and commercial professional with over 24 years of experience in the French property sector. My background includes legal advisory work for major property developers, contract drafting and negotiation, risk analysis, property development operations and sales management. I have worked with a wide range of stakeholders, including notaries, lawyers, investors, landlords and operational teams. I am interested in the Manager Legal & Business Affairs role because it combines transaction support, business strategy and stakeholder coordination in an international investment environment. I am now looking to continue my career in the Netherlands and bring my dual legal and commercial perspective to a dynamic organisation.

Why this works: It is clear, evidence-based and directly connected to the role. It does not try to explain every detail of your career.
