Step 1

Is a European CV the same as a US résumé?

Not exactly. For Amsterdam, you need a clear European / Dutch-style CV, not a French CV copied into English and not a purely American résumé.

🇫🇷 French CV habits

  • Often dense and detailed.
  • Can include many responsibilities.
  • May focus on status, diploma and job title.
  • Sometimes uses long noun phrases.

🇳🇱 European / Dutch target

  • Clear and direct.
  • Usually 1–2 pages.
  • Reverse chronological experience.
  • Strong profile + skills + targeted bullets.

🇺🇸 US résumé style

  • Often very achievement-focused.
  • Usually no photo or personal details.
  • Often one page, especially for many roles.
  • Uses strong measurable results.
Best choice for you: a modern international CV for the Netherlands: concise, professional, easy to scan, with your legal and commercial real estate expertise visible immediately.

Step 2

European / Dutch CV rules you need to know

📏 Rule 1 — Keep it short and relevant

A European CV should usually be clear and concise. Two pages is often a good maximum for an experienced profile. The goal is not to include everything you have ever done. The goal is to show the experience that matches the job you want.

Useful sentence: I selected the most relevant experience for the role.

Choose the best CV decision.

📍 Rule 2 — Personal details: be careful

For international applications, keep contact details simple: city/country, phone, email and LinkedIn. In the Netherlands, a photo can be common, but it is optional. A full street address, marital status, children or personal ID numbers are not needed for a modern international CV.

🧭 Rule 3 — Use reverse chronological order

Start with your most recent experience, then go backwards. This helps recruiters understand your current professional level quickly.

Structure: Current / most recent role → previous roles → education → languages → interests.

🎯 Rule 4 — Tailor your CV to the job

A Dutch or European recruiter should immediately see why your experience is useful for the role. For a real estate agency, highlight clients, property viewings, sales, negotiation and communication. For a legal property role, highlight contracts, risk, urban planning, developers and legal advice.

Step 4

Choose a safe professional title and write a strong profile

Your title should be accurate internationally. In English, “lawyer” can be sensitive because it may suggest a regulated professional status.

Less safe

Real Estate Lawyer

Possible issue: this may imply you are qualified as an avocat / solicitor / attorney.

Safer options

  • Real Estate Legal & Commercial Professional
  • Real Estate Legal Specialist
  • Real Estate Legal Counsel
  • Real Estate Legal & Sales Professional

Profile formula 🧩

Who you are + years of experience + sector + main expertise + target in the Netherlands

Model: Real estate legal and commercial professional with over 24 years’ experience in the French property sector, including legal advisory work for major property developers and sales management responsibilities. Skilled in real estate contracts, property development operations, negotiation, risk prevention, client relations and coordination with notaries, lawyers, investors and operational teams. Now seeking a new professional opportunity in the Netherlands, ideally in a real estate agency or a property-related legal position.

Step 5

Turn French-style tasks into international CV bullets

English CV bullets usually start with action verbs. They should be short, clear and relevant.

French-style idea

Advice to operational staff and assistance for monitoring operations.

Advised operational teams on real estate law, urban planning, construction and risk prevention.

French-style idea

Drafting and negotiation of contracts.

Drafted and negotiated real estate development contracts, including off-plan sale agreements and reservation agreements.

French-style idea

Relations with notaries, lawyers, investors and surveyors.

Liaised with notaries, lawyers, investors, surveyors and external partners.

French-style idea

Budget annuel : 1,000,000 euros excluding VAT.

Managed an annual fees budget of approximately €1 million excluding VAT.

Grammar pattern: action verb + object + context

Drafted contracts for real estate development projects.

Coordinated documents with notaries.

Managed sales operations across France.

Speaking practice

Turn your CV into interview answers

A good CV is not only a document. It is also the foundation for interviews, networking and CLOE speaking practice.

Question 1

Can you introduce yourself professionally?

I am a real estate legal and commercial professional with over 24 years’ experience in the French property sector. I have worked in legal departments for major property developers and I also have strong sales management experience.

Question 2

What makes your profile different?

My profile is different because I combine legal expertise with commercial experience. I understand both the legal risks and the client relationship side of real estate transactions.

Question 3

Why would your experience be useful in the Netherlands?

My experience could be useful because I can analyse real estate projects from both a legal and commercial point of view. I can advise clients, understand their needs and communicate with different professional partners.
