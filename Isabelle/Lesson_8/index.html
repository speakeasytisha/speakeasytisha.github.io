Step 2

🌍 French CV vs European/Dutch CV vs US résumé

For Amsterdam, your target is not a direct French translation and not a purely American résumé. You need a modern European CV in English, adapted to the Dutch job market.

🇫🇷 French CV habits

  • Often detailed and dense.
  • May include many responsibilities.
  • Can focus on diploma, status and job title.
  • Uses French legal abbreviations: VEFA, BEFA, CPI, HT.
  • Sometimes keeps long noun phrases.

🇳🇱 European / Dutch target

  • Clear, direct and easy to scan.
  • Usually 1–2 pages for an experienced profile.
  • Reverse chronological order.
  • Profile + core skills + targeted experience.
  • Achievements and figures are useful.

🇺🇸 US résumé style

  • Often very short and achievement-focused.
  • Usually no photo or personal information.
  • Often uses one page, depending on experience.
  • Very direct action verbs.
  • Less European diploma/context detail.
Best choice for your Amsterdam project: a concise European/Dutch-style CV in English, with your legal and commercial real estate expertise visible immediately.
📸 Photo, address and personal details

In the Netherlands, a professional photo can be optional. In the US, a photo is usually avoided. For your Amsterdam applications, choose a clean professional layout and only include details that help the recruiter contact and understand you.

  • Recommended: city/country, phone, email, LinkedIn.
  • Optional: professional photo, depending on the target company and style.
  • Avoid: too much personal information that is not relevant to the role.

Step 4

🔍 Grammar and style rules for your CV

These rules will help you avoid French structures and write clear professional CV English.

Rule 1 — Use action verbs for experience 🚀

English CV bullets usually start with action verbs. This is clearer and stronger than noun phrases.

Pattern: Action verb + object + context/result.

Example: Drafted and negotiated real estate development contracts.

Choose the strongest CV bullet.

Rule 2 — Use “for” with duration ⏳

Use for when you talk about a period of time.

Pattern: for + number + years/months.

Examples: for 24 years · for 10 years · for 14 years

Complete the sentence:

I have worked in real estate 24 years.

Rule 3 — Use present perfect for your overall experience 📌

Use have + past participle to connect your past experience to your present professional profile.

Pattern: I have worked / I have managed / I have developed.

Example: I have worked in real estate for over 24 years.

Choose the best profile sentence.

Rule 4 — Use past simple for previous responsibilities 🧾

Use the past simple for completed roles and responsibilities.

Pattern: I managed / I advised / I coordinated / I reviewed.

Example: Managed an annual fees budget of approximately €1 million excluding VAT.

Choose the best completed-job bullet.

Rule 5 — Use noun phrases for skills 🧩

In a skills section, short noun phrases are clearer than long sentences.

Pattern: Contract drafting · Risk analysis · Client relations · Sales management.

Which item belongs in a skills list?

Rule 6 — Avoid risky titles: lawyer vs legal professional ⚖️

In English, lawyer can suggest a formal professional qualification. If the role is “juriste immobilier”, safer titles may be more appropriate.

Recommended: Real Estate Legal & Commercial Professional · Real Estate Legal Specialist · Real Estate Legal Counsel.

Rule 7 — Explain French abbreviations clearly 🧠

For an international reader, avoid French abbreviations alone. Use the English term first, then the abbreviation if useful.

Pattern: off-plan sale agreement (VEFA) · off-plan lease agreement (BEFA) · excluding VAT.

Choose the clearest international wording.

Rule 8 — Use achievements and figures 📊

Figures help recruiters understand the scale of your experience. Use them when they are clear, relevant and easy to understand.

Examples: 24+ years’ experience · 10 years in sales management · €1 million annual fees budget.

Step 9

🗣️ Speak from your CV: interview and CLOE practice

Your CV is not only a document. It is the base for job interviews, networking conversations and the CLOE oral exam. Use the model answers, then practise speaking from the cues without reading the full text.

1. Introduce your profile

Question: Can you introduce yourself professionally?

Starter: I am a real estate legal and commercial professional with…

I am a real estate legal and commercial professional with over 24 years’ experience in the French property sector. I have worked both in legal departments and in sales management, mainly with property developers. My profile combines legal analysis, contract work, negotiation, client relations and a strong understanding of real estate development operations.

2. Explain your double expertise

Question: What makes your profile different?

Starter: I combine legal expertise with commercial experience…

What makes my profile different is that I combine legal expertise with commercial experience. I understand real estate transactions from both sides: the legal structure, contracts and risks, but also the client relationship, negotiation and sales process. This helps me communicate with different stakeholders and offer practical advice.

3. Talk about your target

Question: Why would you like to work in the Netherlands?

Starter: I would like to build a professional project in the Amsterdam area…

I would like to build a professional project in the Amsterdam area because I am planning to move to the Netherlands and integrate into the local business community. I am interested in working in real estate, either in an agency or in a property-related legal role, where I can use my French experience and develop my international professional network.

4. Explain your value to an agency

Question: What can you bring to a real estate agency?

Starter: I can bring experience in client relations, negotiation and legal analysis…

I can bring a strong combination of client relations, negotiation, legal analysis and real estate experience. I listen carefully to clients before speaking so that I can understand their needs and recommend the right solution. I can also support a team with legal vocabulary, contract understanding and professional communication with clients and partners.

5. Explain your sales budget

Question: What was your responsibility as a sales manager?

Starter: As a sales manager, I was responsible for…

As a sales manager, I was responsible for sales operations and an annual fees budget of approximately €1 million excluding VAT. I also coordinated a small team and worked with a wide range of clients, including private clients, property developers, institutional owners and professional partners.

6. Explain legal work simply

Question: How would you explain your legal experience to a non-specialist recruiter?

Starter: I worked on legal issues connected to…

I worked on legal issues connected to real estate development, contracts, urban planning, construction and risk prevention. My role was to advise operational teams, review and draft legal documents, monitor disputes and coordinate with notaries, lawyers, investors and other professional partners.

Speaking method: first read the model answer, then hide it and speak from only 3 cues: experience + example + value. The goal is not to memorise a text; the goal is to speak naturally from your CV.
