⭐ Grammar Review — Future and Professional Goals

Would like to

Use for polite professional goals.

I would like to work at Disney.

Going to

Use for plans and intentions.

I am going to improve my English.

Will

Use for promises, reactions and predictions.

I will do my best.

Hope to / Dream of

Use for ambitions and dreams.

I hope to work abroad one day.

Choose the best expression

🎤 Upgrade “Tell me about yourself”

🟢 Simple

My name is Angie. I live near Nantes. I love Disney and I would like to work there.

🟡 Stronger

My name is Angie and I live near Nantes. I would like to work at Disney because I enjoy helping people and communicating with guests.

🟣 Professional

My name is Angie. I am motivated, friendly and interested in customer service. I would love to work at Disney because I enjoy creating positive experiences for guests.

🔗 Fluency Connectors

Because

Use for reasons.

I want to work at Disney because I love helping people.

So

Use for results.

I am improving my English, so I can speak with guests.

Also

Use for adding ideas.

I am friendly. I am also patient.

In the future

Use for ambitions.

In the future, I would like to work abroad.
