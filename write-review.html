Hotel & Restaurant Review

Review your trip to San Francisco: talk about your hotel, your meals and your overall experience using the past simple and opinion adjectives.

Your score: 0 / 0

1. Vocabulary – Hotel Reviews

Key words for talking about your stay in a hotel: check-out, bill, stars, comments and more. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

BILL ICON HERE

bill

The total amount you must pay at the end of your stay.

RECEIPT ICON HERE

receipt

The printed document that confirms your payment.

STARS ICON HERE

star rating

How many stars (1–5) you give the hotel.

COMMENT ICON HERE

review

A short text where you describe your stay and give your opinion.

STAFF ICON HERE

staff

The people who work at the hotel (reception, restaurant, etc.).

ROOM ICON HERE

room

Where you sleep – you can describe the size, the view and if it was quiet or noisy.

Quick Vocabulary Quiz

1. Which word is the final amount you pay at the hotel?

2. You already paid by card and you get a printed document. What is it?

3. You write a short text online to say if the room was clean and quiet. What is it?

2. Grammar – Past Simple for Reviews

Use the past simple to talk about a finished stay: was / were / stayed / visited / loved / didn’t. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Examples:

  • We stayed at a modern hotel near Union Square.
  • The room was quiet and very comfortable.
  • The staff were friendly and helpful.
  • We didn’t use the pool because we were tired.

2. Which negative sentence is correct?

3. Which question is correct for the past?

4. Which sentence is the best for describing the staff in a review?

4. Reading – Restaurant of the Year

Read about a famous restaurant in California, then answer the questions. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

RESTAURANT IMAGE HERE

Burdell is a modern soul food restaurant in the Temescal neighbourhood of Oakland, California. It opened recently and quickly became famous in the United States. A major food magazine even named it “Restaurant of the Year”.

The chef-owner, Geoff Davis, grew up in California and worked in fine-dining restaurants before opening Burdell. The restaurant is named after his grandmother, and the décor feels like a warm family home from the 1960s–1970s, with vintage plates, old-school soul music and family photos.

The food is “California soul food”: traditional Black American comfort dishes made with fresh local ingredients and modern techniques — for example boiled peanuts with miso, barbecue shrimp in a rich sauce or chicken liver mousse served with a cornmeal waffle.

NEIGHBORHOOD ICON HERE

neighbourhood

A part of a city; an area where people live and go out.

SOUL FOOD ICON HERE

soul food

Traditional Black American comfort food, often linked to family recipes and history.

CHEF-OWNER ICON HERE

chef-owner

A person who cooks and also owns the restaurant.

VINTAGE DÉCOR ICON HERE

vintage décor

Decoration in an old-style design, inspired by the past.
