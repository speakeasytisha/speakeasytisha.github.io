1) Exam tips

What strong VTest writing usually looks like

⭐ What gives points

  • Clear purpose from the first lines
  • Useful connectors and logical sequencing
  • Specific details, dates, or examples
  • Polite, professional tone
  • Accurate tense choice

⚠️ What weakens the answer

  • Being too vague
  • Repeating very basic words
  • Missing greeting or closing in emails
  • Wrong tense for the situation
  • No explanation or no request

🧭 Quick plan

1. I’m writing to...
2. First of all...
3. Could you please...?
4. Thank you in advance...

2) Grammar boosters

Tenses, sequencing, comparatives, and transition words

⏱ Tenses and why

  • Present simple: facts, routines, neutral statements
  • Present continuous: current problem or action happening now
  • Past simple / preterite: what happened in a finished past situation
  • Present perfect: past experience linked to now
  • Would / could / will: requests, solutions, future action
