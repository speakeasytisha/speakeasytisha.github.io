1) What this part of the exam wants

You do not just answer. You must understand, clarify, reformulate, and write clearly.

Vous ne devez pas seulement répondre. Vous devez comprendre, demander une clarification, reformuler et écrire clairement.

2) Grammar workshop — calm or calmly?

This helps you sound professional in customer and passenger situations.

Cela vous aide à parler de façon professionnelle dans les situations avec des clients et des passagers.

Rule 1 — adjective vs adverb

calm = adjective → it describes a person or a situation.

calm = adjectif → décrit une personne ou une situation.

  • The passenger is calm.
  • You are calm and professional.
  • It is a calm situation.

calmly = adverb → it describes how you speak or act.

calmly = adverbe → décrit comment vous parlez ou agissez.

  • You speak calmly.
  • Please explain the situation calmly.
  • You answer the passenger calmly.
✅ You are calm.
✅ Please speak calmly.
❌ You are calmly.
❌ Please be calmly.

Rule 2 — other useful pairs

AdjectiveAdverbExample
clearclearlyYou give a clear explanation. / You explain clearly.
politepolitelyYou are polite. / You speak politely.
carefulcarefullyPlease be careful. / Please listen carefully.
quickquicklyIt is a quick check. / Please reply quickly.
Tip: adjective = noun / be. Adverb = verb.
adjectif = nom / be. adverbe = verbe.

3) Grammar workshop — polite clarification language

In the exam, you must not panic. You can ask for help professionally.

Dans l’examen, vous ne devez pas paniquer. Vous pouvez demander de l’aide de façon professionnelle.

Useful structures

Could you repeat that, please?
Pourriez-vous répéter, s’il vous plaît ?
Could you say that more slowly, please?
Pourriez-vous parler plus lentement, s’il vous plaît ?
Can I check something?
Puis-je vérifier quelque chose ?
If I understand correctly, ...
Si je comprends bien, ...
So, the problem is ...
Donc, le problème est ...
Let me check.
Laissez-moi vérifier.
I will explain the next steps.
Je vais expliquer les étapes suivantes.

Concrete examples

Passenger: I have a problem with my seat.

You: Could you explain the problem, please?

Passenger: I have a connection and I am worried.

You: If I understand correctly, you are worried about your connection.

Passenger: My cabin bag is too big?

You: Let me check. I will explain the next steps.
