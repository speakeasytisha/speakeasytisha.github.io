2) Grammar: Describe places + give location + talk about dates

Short rules, clear frames, then practice right away.

Describe a place (A1+/A2)

🧱
Useful frames
It is… It is + adjective + noun → “It is a lively city.”
There is/are… There is/are + noun → “There are many parks.”
You can… You can + base verb → “You can go skiing.”
💡
Adjectives (no slang)
relaxing, romantic, lively, quiet, scenic, cultural, touristy, modern, historic, affordable, expensive
🔗
Connectors
Because “It is great because…”
However “It is beautiful; however, it can be expensive.”
Also “Also, you can…”

Location + prepositions

🗺️
Where is it?
in “in Canada / in Quebec / in the city center”
on “on the west coast / on an island”
near “near the mountains / near the ocean”
between “between two cities”
🧭
Regions
west (Pacific), central (Ontario/Quebec), east (Atlantic), north (Yukon/NWT/Nunavut)
⏱️
Time & dates (simple)
in “in July / in summer / in 2026”
on “on July 12th / on Monday”
at “at 7:30 pm / at noon”
