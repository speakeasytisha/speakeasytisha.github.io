Canada Speaking Quest

Today you practise real oral scenarios for a trip to Canada: arriving at the airport, answering border questions, checking into a hotel, ordering in a restaurant, asking for help, and describing your travel plans.

Grammar toolbox — useful forms for Canada

Do not teach every tense today. Teach the forms he needs to survive a real conversation.

1. Polite requests

Use Could you…? when you ask another person to help you.

Could you + verb + …?
  • Could you help me, please?
  • Could you tell me where the bus stop is?
  • Could you repeat that more slowly, please?

2. Polite personal requests

Use Could I…? when you ask permission for yourself.

Could I + verb + …?
  • Could I check in now?
  • Could I have a map, please?
  • Could I change rooms?

3. Travel plans

Use be going to for plans that are already in your mind.

I am going to + verb
  • I’m going to visit Montreal.
  • We’re going to rent a car.
  • I’m not going to stay very long.

4. Travel preparation

Use have + past participle for things prepared before the trip.

I have booked / I have checked / I have prepared
  • I have booked my flight.
  • I haven’t checked in online yet.
  • Have you prepared the documents?

5. Explain a problem

Use because for the reason and so for the result.

Problem + because + reason. So + request.
  • My key card doesn’t work because the door does not open.
  • It is raining, so could you recommend an indoor activity?
  • I missed my bus, so I need another route.

6. Make answers longer

Use the four-step answer when the teacher asks a question.

Answer → detail → reason → closing sentence
  • I’m going to Canada for holidays.
  • I’d like to visit nature and cities.
  • Because I love beautiful landscapes.
  • I’m really looking forward to it.

      A2+/B1 model

      I’m really looking forward to my trip to Canada because I’d like to discover both the cities and the landscapes. I have already booked my flight and accommodation, but I still need to check the travel documents and the weather. During the trip, I’m going to ask for local advice, try Canadian food, and take photos. If something goes wrong, for example if I get lost or miss a bus, I’ll stay calm and ask, “Could you help me, please?”
