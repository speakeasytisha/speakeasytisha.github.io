1) Why this lesson matters for LILATE

In Part 1 and Part 2, you often need to identify the passenger, understand the situation, and reformulate correctly.

Dans la partie 1 et la partie 2, vous devez souvent identifier le passager, comprendre la situation et reformuler correctement.

What you need to say clearly

🧍
Identify the passenger
The passenger is Italian. He is travelling to Rome.

👪

His wife is travelling with him. Their child needs assistance.
Identifier la famille / les accompagnants.
❓

Are you travelling alone? Is this your cabin bag?

🔁

If I understand correctly, you are travelling with your daughter and you need help.

2) Grammar workshop — countries and nationalities

A country is the place. A nationality is the identity adjective.

Le pays est le lieu. La nationalité est l’adjectif d’identité.

Rule 1 — country ≠ nationality

Use the country after from.

  • She is from Spain.
  • He is from Italy.
  • They are from Portugal.

Use the nationality after be.

  • She is Spanish.
  • He is Italian.
  • They are Portuguese.
✅ She is from Spain. She is Spanish.
✅ He is from Germany. He is German.
❌ She is from Spanish.
❌ He is Germany.

3) Grammar workshop — pronouns and possessives

You need the right person word and the right possessive word.

Vous avez besoin du bon pronom et du bon possessif.

Rule 2 — pronoun

Use the pronoun to replace the person.

  • You are travelling to Madrid. (the passenger)
  • David is travelling to Rome. He is travelling to Rome.
  • Sofia is travelling to Lisbon. She is travelling to Lisbon.
  • The parents are travelling together. They are travelling together.
✅ Sofia is travelling. She is worried about the gate change.
❌ Sofia is travelling. You is worried about the gate change.

4) Mini grammar add-on — this / that / these / those

These words are useful at the airport and on board.

Ces mots sont utiles à l’aéroport et à bord de l’avion.

Simple rule

  • this = singular, near → Is this your passport?
  • that = singular, farther → Is that your seat?
  • these = plural, near → Are these your bags?
  • those = plural, farther → Are those your children?

Use the tools from the lesson: country, nationality, pronoun, possessive, family, and question.

Simple

She is Spanish. Her husband is with her. She has a question about her seat.

Better

The passenger is Spanish. She is travelling to Madrid with her husband. She has a question about her seat.

The passenger is Spanish, and she is travelling to Madrid with her husband. If I understand correctly, she has a question about her seat and she would like some help.
