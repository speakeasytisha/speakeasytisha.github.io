2) Grammar workshop A — this / that / these / those

Use these words to point to one thing or several things. This is essential when you check documents, bags, seats, and boarding passes.

The rule

this = one thing near you
ce/cette ici
Is this your boarding pass?
Is this your cabin bag?
that = one thing farther away / less immediate
ce/cette là
Is that your gate?
Is that your husband over there?
these = several things near you
ces ... ici
Are these your bags?
Are these your seats?
those = several things farther away
ces ... là-bas
Are those your children?
Are those the correct documents?
✅ Is this your passport?
❌ Is these your passport?
✅ Are these your bags?
❌ Are this your bags?

3) Grammar workshop B — whether

Use whether to sound more professional when you check, confirm, or verify information. It is very useful in the LILATE exam.

The rule

whether = if / si, in a more formal or professional way
I would like to check whether your special meal is confirmed.
Let me see whether your gate has changed.
Could you confirm whether you are travelling alone?
Important: after whether, use a normal sentence order.
✅ Let me check whether your gate has changed.
❌ Let me check whether has your gate changed.
✅ I need to know whether she is travelling with her husband.
❌ I need to know whether is she travelling with her husband.

Take notes
