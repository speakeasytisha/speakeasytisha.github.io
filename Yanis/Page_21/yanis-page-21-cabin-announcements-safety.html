Cabin Announcements & Safety English ✈️

This lesson helps you give clear announcements, safety instructions, and procedure information for the LILATE exam and for your future job. You will practise imperatives, must / have to / need to / can / can’t, be + past participle for status, and sequence markers.

Goal: sound calm, clear, and professional when you give instructions.

1) Rule workshop — grammar for announcements and procedures

Each block gives you the rule, the nuance, examples, and a note box.

A. Imperatives

Rule: use the base verb with no subject to give an instruction.
Fasten your seatbelt.
Remain seated.
Please switch off your electronic devices.
Nuance: please makes the instruction more polite. The verb does not change.
✅ Please fasten your seatbelt.
❌ You fasten your seatbelt.
Negative imperative: use Do not / Don’t + base verb.
Do not smoke.
Don’t stand up now.

B. Must / have to / need to / can / can’t

must = strong rule / safety instruction
Passengers must remain seated.
have to / need to = necessary action
You need to put your bag under the seat.
You have to wait here.
can / can’t = permission / possibility / rule
You can board now.
You can’t use your phone now.
Nuance: in airline safety language, must sounds stronger and more formal than have to.

C. Subject + be + past participle

Rule: use this structure to describe the situation now.
The flight is delayed.
The gate is confirmed.
Boarding is suspended.
The safety check is completed.
Simple learner explanation: these phrases describe the current status now.
✅ The flight is delayed.
✅ Boarding is cancelled.
❌ The gate is changed. Better: Your new gate is A7.

D. Sequence markers + because / so / but

Sequence: first, then, after that, finally.
First, show me your boarding pass.
Then, please go to gate A7.
After that, wait near the desk.
Finally, boarding will begin.
Linkers: use because for reason, so for result, and but for contrast.
Please remain seated because we are preparing for take-off.
The flight is delayed, so boarding will begin later.
You can board now, but please stay in your zone.
