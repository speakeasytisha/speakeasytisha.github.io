Clear grammar start

For your first lesson, you need two essentials: be and the present simple with main verbs.

1. Use be for identity and description

You use be to say who you are, how old you are, where you are, and what you are.

Type Example
Affirmative I am a receptionist. / I am twenty-five.
Negative I am not a steward yet.
Question Are you ready for boarding? / Am I clear?

2. Use the present simple for routines and job tasks

You use the present simple to talk about your job, your responsibilities, and your regular habits.

Type Example
Affirmative I welcome guests. / I answer emails.
Negative I do not work alone. / I do not use English every minute.
Question Do you answer phone calls? / Do you use English at work?

Useful job expressions

  • I work as a hotel receptionist.
  • I am responsible for guest reception.
  • I welcome guests and answer calls.
  • I handle check-in and check-out.
  • I deal with reservations and customer requests.
  • I use English mainly by email and on the phone.
  • I want to become a steward because I enjoy service and human contact.

Final mission — your introduction

Write your own short presentation first. Then reveal a model answer when you are ready.

Your checklist

  1. Introduce yourself.
  2. Say where you live.
  3. Describe your current job.
  4. Explain your main tasks.
  5. Say when you use English.
  6. Say what you do well.
  7. Explain your future goal and why.

Helpful words: motivated, professional, organized, helpful, flexible, customer service, international, welcoming, calm, teamwork.

Your writing space

Model answer:

Good afternoon. My name is Yanis, I am twenty-five years old and I live in Schiltigheim. I work as a receptionist in a three-star hotel. In my job, I welcome guests, handle check-in and check-out, answer emails and phone calls, and deal with reservations. During the weekend, I also help with the bar and breakfast service. I use English mainly on the phone and by email, especially with international guests. I think I am friendly, helpful, and comfortable with customer contact. In the future, I want to become a steward because I enjoy service, communication, and working with people.
