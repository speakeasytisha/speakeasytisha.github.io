Why this matters for CLOE

This is CLOE-style preparation: the goal is not to recite grammar rules, but to understand and use English clearly in everyday and professional communication.

Your tense passport

✓Present simplehabits, routines, facts
✓Present continuousnow, around now, arrangements
✓Past simplefinished past actions
✓Future: going toplans and intentions
✓Future: willoffers, quick decisions, predictions
✓Future arrangementpresent continuous + date/time

Important: can, could, should, must and have to are modal verbs, not verb tenses. You will review them briefly because they are useful in questions, answers and professional situations.

Not a core test today: the present perfect has appeared in a few useful phrases, but you have not studied it as a full system yet. It should be your next new tense after this lesson.

55–70 min · question detective

How to ask the right question

A good question uses the same tense as the answer. First find the time clue in the answer. Then choose the helper.

Present simple

Do / Does + subject + base verb...?

What do you usually do on Mondays?

Present continuous

Am / Is / Are + subject + verb-ing...?

What are you doing now?

Past simple

Did + subject + base verb...?

What did you do last weekend?

Future

Are + subject + going to...? / Will + subject...?

What are you going to do tomorrow?

Match the answer to the question

Look at the time clue in the answer.

70–80 min · answer in the correct tense

How to answer clearly

For a short answer, repeat the helper. For a fuller CLOE answer, add one detail and one reason or time expression.

Yes / No

Do you work on Mondays?
Yes, I do. / No, I don’t.

Past

Did you go out last weekend?
Yes, I did. I went to a restaurant.

Now

Are you studying now?
Yes, I am. I am reviewing my lesson.

Future

Are you going to travel this summer?
Yes, I am. I am going to visit...

After this lesson

Your next CLOE learning steps

1

Present perfect vs past simple

Use I have lived here for 30 years, I have been retired for three months, and compare them with finished past actions: I went to Nantes last week.

2

Listening + recovery language

Understand short everyday and professional messages, then ask someone to repeat, slow down, spell, confirm a time or clarify a detail.

3

Longer oral answers

Build answers with a reason, an example and a follow-up question so that you can continue an interaction naturally.

4

Final mixed mock

Timed written practice plus a realistic oral interview: introduction, practical situation and short discussion.
