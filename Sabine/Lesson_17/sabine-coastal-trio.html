03 · Grammar inside the trip

Use grammar as part of the journey

You do not need grammar alone. You need grammar + vocabulary + ideas + short useful travel sentences.

Grammar 1 — Prepositions of place and movement

Prépositions de lieu et de mouvement

Use in for a town, city or state. Use on for an island or a coast. Use near for something close. Use from to show the departure point and to for the destination.

Utilisez in pour une ville ou un État. Utilisez on pour une île ou une côte. Utilisez near pour quelque chose de proche. Utilisez from pour le départ et to pour la destination.
Nantucket is on an island. · Key West is in Florida. · Rainbow Row is in Charleston.
Location
Nantucket is on an island off Massachusetts.
Movement
You travel from Boston to Nantucket.
Travel recipe: place + preposition + detail.
“The hotel is near the harbour.” “The museum is in the historic district.”

Grammar 2 — Adjectives and adverbs

Adjectifs et adverbes

An adjective describes a noun: a beautiful street. An adverb describes a verb or the way you do something: walk slowly.

Un adjectif décrit un nom : a beautiful street. Un adverbe décrit un verbe ou la manière de faire quelque chose : walk slowly.
a beautiful island · a historic street · walk slowly · travel comfortably
AdjectiveAdverbExample
beautifulbeautifullyThe houses are beautiful. The sunset shines beautifully.
easyeasilyCharleston is easy to explore. You can move around easily.
quietquietlyNantucket feels quiet. You can walk quietly by the water.

Grammar 3 — Comparatives and superlatives

Comparatifs et superlatifs

Use -er / more to compare two places. Use the -est / the most when you choose one place in a group of three.

Utilisez -er / more pour comparer deux lieux. Utilisez the -est / the most quand vous choisissez un lieu parmi trois.
Key West is hotter than Nantucket. · Charleston is more historic than Key West. · Key West is the most tropical option.
Comparative
Nantucket is quieter than Key West.
Superlative
Charleston is the most elegant destination.
Important: short adjectives often take -er and -est. Longer adjectives often use more and the most.

Grammar 4 — Going to for a surprise plan

Going to pour un projet surprise

Use be going to when you already have a plan or an intention. It is perfect for a future travel project.

Utilisez be going to quand vous avez déjà un projet ou une intention. C’est parfait pour un projet de voyage futur.
Subject + am / is / are going to + base verb
FormExample
AffirmativeI am going to surprise my friend.
NegativeI am not going to choose a busy hotel.
QuestionAre you going to book the ferry?
Step by step: subject → be → going to → verb → place / detail.Étape par étape : sujet → be → going to → verbe → lieu / détail.

Grammar 5 — Travel irregular verbs

Verbes irréguliers utiles en voyage

Some very useful travel verbs are irregular in the past. You need to recognise them when you read and hear English, and use them when you talk about a past trip.

Certains verbes très utiles en voyage sont irréguliers au passé. Vous devez les reconnaître quand vous lisez et entendez l’anglais, et les utiliser quand vous parlez d’un voyage passé.
Base verbPast simpleExample
gowentLast summer, we went to Key West.
flyflewWe flew to Charleston.
taketookWe took the ferry to Nantucket.
seesawWe saw the lighthouse.
buyboughtWe bought the tickets online.
choosechoseWe chose a quiet hotel.
Small speaking frame: Last year, I went to… / I saw… / I took… / I bought…
