04 · Grammar lane

Grammar you can actually use during the trip

These grammar blocks help you speak in real situations: requests, plans, complaints, directions and useful irregular verbs.

Ces points de grammaire vous aident à parler dans de vraies situations : demandes, projets, plaintes, directions et verbes irréguliers utiles.

Grammar 1 — Polite requests

Demandes polies

Use Could I...? and Could you...? in hotels and restaurants.

Utilisez Could I...? et Could you...? à l’hôtel et au restaurant.
Could I + base verb ... ?
Could you + base verb ... ?
UseModel
room requestCould I have a quiet room, please?
helpCould you help me, please?
informationCould you recommend a restaurant?
serviceCould we have the bill, please?
Important: after could, use the base verb: help, recommend, bring, check.

Grammar 2 — Going to for plans

Going to pour les projets

Use going to when you already have a plan.

Utilisez going to quand vous avez déjà un projet.
Subject + am / is / are going to + base verb
Affirmative
I am going to check in at 3 p.m.
Negative
I am not going to eat lobster tonight.
Question
Are we going to take a taxi back?
Travel model
We are going to have dinner near the harbor.

Grammar 3 — Complaints with different subjects

Plaintes avec différents sujets

To complain clearly, choose the right subject first.

Pour vous plaindre clairement, choisissez d’abord le bon sujet.
SubjectModel complaint
There is...There is a problem with the air conditioning.
There are...There are no towels in the bathroom.
My room...My room is too noisy.
The shower...The shower does not work.
We...We ordered water, not wine.
I...I asked for a quiet room.
They...They gave us the wrong meal.
Useful correction phrases: I’m sorry, but... / Excuse me, there is a problem... / I think there is a mistake.

Hotel dialogue 1 · Before arrival

Avant l’arrivée
HotelHello, Seaport House Hotel. How can I help you?
YouHello, I would like to book a double room for two nights, please.
HotelOf course. What dates would you like?
YouFrom Friday to Sunday. Is breakfast included?
HotelYes, breakfast is included. Would you like a quiet room?
YouYes, please. I would also like parking if possible.

Hotel dialogue 2 · Arrival and check-in

Arrivée et check-in
ReceptionistGood afternoon. Welcome to Seaport House Hotel.
YouHello, I have a reservation under the name Sabine. Could I check in, please?
ReceptionistOf course. Could I see your passport, please?
YouYes, of course. Here you are.
ReceptionistThank you. You are in room 214. Here is your key card. Breakfast is from 7 to 10 a.m.
YouThank you. Is there a lift? And where is the restaurant?

Hotel dialogue 3 · Room requirements and complaints

Demandes pour la chambre et plaintes
YouExcuse me, there is a problem with my room.
ReceptionistI’m sorry to hear that. What seems to be the problem?
YouThere are no towels in the bathroom, and the shower does not work.
ReceptionistI’m very sorry. We are going to send someone immediately.
YouThank you. My room is also a little noisy. Could I change rooms if possible?
ReceptionistYes, let me check. We may have a quieter room on the third floor.

Hotel dialogue 4 · Recommendations, ATM and taxi

Recommandations, distributeur et taxi
YouCould you recommend a good restaurant near the waterfront?
ReceptionistYes, of course. Yes, of course. A restaurant on the waterfront is very popular and it is about ten minutes away on foot.
YouGreat. Is there an ATM near the hotel?
ReceptionistYes. Yes. There is one next to the pharmacy, across from the coffee shop.
YouThank you. And could you call a taxi for us for 7 p.m.?
ReceptionistOf course. I can arrange that for you.

Practice · restaurant quick check

Restaurant dialogue 1 · Before dinner

Avant le dîner
YouHello, I would like to book a table for two for tonight at 7:30, please.
RestaurantOf course. Do you have any seating preference?
YouYes, if possible, we would like a table by the window.
RestaurantThat should be fine. The reservation is under which name?
YouUnder the name Sabine, please.

Restaurant dialogue 2 · Arrival, drinks and meal

Arrivée, boissons et repas
HostGood evening. Do you have a reservation?
YouHello, yes. We have a reservation under the name Sabine.
HostPerfect. Your table is ready. Please follow me.
YouThank you. Could we have still water and two menus, please?
ServerOf course. Are you ready to order?
YouI would like the fish with salad, please, and my friend would like the chicken with fries.

Restaurant dialogue 3 · Sides, extras and a wrong meal

Accompagnements, extras et mauvais plat
YouCould I have extra sauce, please? And could I have fries instead of salad?
ServerYes, of course.
YouExcuse me, I think there is a mistake. I ordered the fish with salad, not the chicken with fries.
ServerI’m very sorry about that. I am going to change it immediately.
YouThank you. Also, this dish is a little cold.
ServerI apologise. We are going to bring you a new plate.

Restaurant dialogue 4 · Dessert, bill, paying, directions back

Dessert, addition, paiement, retour à l’hôtel
YouCould we see the dessert menu, please?
ServerOf course. We have blueberry pie and vanilla ice cream.
YouThat sounds lovely. We would like one blueberry pie to share.
ServerCertainly.
YouCould we have the bill, please?
ServerYes, of course. You can pay by card here.
YouThank you. One more question: how do we get back to Seaport House Hotel?
ServerGo straight ahead, turn left at the corner, and the hotel is near the waterfront.

Restaurant role-play prompts

  • Ask for fries instead of salad.
  • Say the dish is wrong or cold.
  • Ask for dessert, the bill, and if you can pay by card.
  • Ask how to get back to the hotel.
