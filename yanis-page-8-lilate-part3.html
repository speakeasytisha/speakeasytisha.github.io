Yanis — Give Instructions & Solve Problems ✈️

In this lesson, you practise the next LILATE step: giving instructions, explaining procedures, passing on information clearly, and helping in a real passenger situation.

Dans cette leçon, vous travaillez l’étape suivante du LILATE : donner des consignes, expliquer une procédure, transmettre des informations clairement et aider dans une situation réelle avec un passager.

Transmettre des consignes, des procédures ou des informations.

1) What this part of the exam wants

You must explain what to do, give the next steps, and stay clear and professional.

Vous devez expliquer quoi faire, donner les étapes suivantes et rester clair et professionnel.

LILATE target

💬
Give instructions
Tell the person what to do next.
Dire à la personne ce qu’elle doit faire ensuite.
📋
Explain a procedure
Use clear order: first, then, after that, finally.
Utiliser un ordre clair : d’abord, puis, après cela, enfin.
📨
Pass on information
Repeat important details from a message or from a colleague.
Répéter les informations importantes d’un message ou d’un collègue.
🛟
Help with a problem
Stay calm, explain the next steps, and offer a clear solution.
Rester calme, expliquer les étapes suivantes et proposer une solution claire.

Take notes

2) Grammar workshop — imperatives for instructions

An imperative is the simplest way to give an instruction.

L’impératif est la façon la plus simple de donner une consigne.

Rule 1 — imperative = base verb

Use the base verb to tell a person what to do.

On utilise la base du verbe pour dire à une personne ce qu’elle doit faire.

  • Show your boarding pass.
  • Please take your seat.
  • Place your bag in the overhead bin.
  • Remain calm.

To be more polite, you can add please.

Pour être plus poli, vous pouvez ajouter please.

  • Please wait here.
  • Please follow me.
  • Please listen carefully.
✅ Show your passport.
✅ Please take your seat.
❌ You show your passport.
❌ Please to take your seat.

More concrete examples

At the gate: Please have your boarding pass ready.

On board: Please fasten your seat belt.

With luggage: Please place your bag under the seat.

During a delay: Please wait near the gate for the next announcement.

Short pattern to remember:

Please + base verb + complement

Please + base verb + complément

3) Grammar workshop — can, can’t, must, have to

These words help you explain permission, possibility, and obligation.

Ces mots vous aident à expliquer la permission, la possibilité et l’obligation.

Rule 2 — what each word means

FormMeaningExample
canpermission / possibilityYou can take one small bag.
can’tnot allowed / not possibleYou can’t keep the bag here.
muststrong obligationYou must fasten your seat belt.
have toobligation / procedureYou have to go to the gate now.
Important: after can / can’t / must, use the base verb.
Important : après can / can’t / must, on utilise la base du verbe.
✅ You must wait.
❌ You must to wait.

Concrete steward-style examples

You can keep your passport in your hand.

You can’t take this large bag into the cabin.

You must remain seated during take-off.

You have to follow the crew instructions.

Question forms:

Can I help you?

Do I have to print my boarding pass?

4) Grammar workshop — sequence markers for procedures

Sequence words help you explain steps in a clear order.

Les mots de séquence vous aident à expliquer les étapes dans un ordre clair.

Useful sequence markers

First, ...
D’abord, ...
Then, ...
Puis, ...
After that, ...
Après cela, ...
Finally, ...
Enfin, ...

Model procedure

First, show your boarding pass. Then go to the gate. After that, place your bag in the overhead bin. Finally, take your seat and fasten your seat belt.

8) Writing ladder — compare and improve

You can compare levels together and explain how to improve step by step.

Vous pouvez comparer les niveaux ensemble et expliquer comment améliorer pas à pas.

Level 1 — simple

Go to gate C4. You need your passport. Be fast.
Très simple, compréhensible, mais peu naturel.

Level 2 — better

Please go to gate C4 now. You need to keep your passport and boarding pass ready.
Plus clair, plus poli, plus précis.

Level 3 — stronger

Please go to gate C4 immediately. You have a short connection, so please keep your passport and boarding pass ready. If you need help, I can assist you.
Plus professionnel, mieux structuré, plus utile pour la situation.
Add a polite opening: Please...
Add a clear instruction: go to / keep / show / wait
Add an important detail: gate / time / passport / boarding pass
Add support: If you need help, I can assist you.
