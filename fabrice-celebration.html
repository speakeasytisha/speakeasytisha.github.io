Practice studio

Things you can do to keep moving forward

Short, useful activities designed to build confidence without overwhelm.

🗣️

Speaking routine

10 minutes — calm, simple, repeatable

Useful speaking prompts
  • What do you usually do in the morning?
  • What do you like doing at the weekend?
  • Describe a good meal or a good day.
  • Talk about a place you would like to visit.
✍️

Writing builder

10–15 minutes — short messages that feel useful

Easy writing prompts
  • Ask for information about a hotel or a class.
  • Write to confirm a meeting or a restaurant booking.
  • Describe your daily routine in 5 short sentences.
  • Write a short positive review of a place you like.
🎧

Listening & reading

10 minutes — understand first, then reuse

Good materials to use
  • Very short dialogues with clear pronunciation.
  • Simple professional emails or booking confirmations.
  • Beginner news or travel texts with everyday vocabulary.
  • Short videos with subtitles, then without subtitles.
🧩

Grammar booster

5–10 minutes — focus on the most useful essentials

Best priorities
  • Present simple and word order.
  • Question forms with do / does.
  • Articles: a / an / the.
  • Very common verbs: be, have, go, like, work, need.

Weekly plan

A simple routine that can really work

Consistency matters more than long sessions.

Monday

Speak + repeat

3 personal questions + 5 model repetitions.

Tuesday

Write short

A mini email or message in 4–5 lines.

Wednesday

Listen smart

One short audio twice + note 3 useful words.

Thursday

Grammar focus

Present simple, questions, or there is / there are.

Friday

Speak again

Retell the week or describe a photo for 45 seconds.

Weekend

Enjoy English

Watch something short with subtitles and repeat one useful phrase.

Confidence toolkit

Useful phrases to keep nearby

Could you repeat, please? Let me think for a moment. I understand the question. In my opinion… Usually, I… For example… First… then… To sum up…

Self-check after practice

Three good questions to ask yourself

  1. Did I speak or write clearly enough to communicate?
  2. Did I use one or two useful structures correctly?
  3. Did I keep going, even if I was not perfect?

If the answer is yes, the practice was a success.
