1) Warm-up — what do these little words do?

The key idea

In French, students often try to translate word by word. In English, that can be confusing. With verbs like go out, find out, get on with, come in, or walk through, the small word is part of the meaning.

A good strategy: learn the whole expression and group expressions by the small word.

Quick examples

  • 🚪 come in = enter / entrer
  • 🌦️ go out = go outside / sortir
  • 🧠 find out = discover / découvrir
  • 🤝 get on with = have a good relationship / bien s'entendre avec
  • 🚶 walk through = go across / traverser
  • 📴 take off = remove / enlever

3) Verb + particle lesson — organised by the little word

This organisation is often easier for students because it gives a simple idea first, then examples, then practice.

OUT

Main idea: outside, completion, discovery

go out = sortir

We went out after breakfast.

find out = découvrir

I found out where the harbour was.

work out = comprendre / arranger

We worked out a plan for the afternoon.

IN

Main idea: entering, being inside, including

come in = entrer

Please come in and make yourself at home.

check in = s'enregistrer

We checked in at the hotel.

join in = se joindre à

I joined in the conversation.

UP

Main idea: upward movement, completion, contact

pick up = aller chercher

My son picked me up at the station.

set up = organiser / installer

They set up a lovely lunch outside.

meet up with = retrouver

We met up with family friends.

ON

Main idea: contact, continuation, dependence

go on = continuer

The conversation went on for hours.

try on = essayer (un vêtement)

I tried on a warm coat.

carry on = continuer

We carried on walking despite the wind.

THROUGH

Main idea: crossing, passing from one side to another, process

walk through = traverser à pied

We walked through the town centre.

go through = passer par / examiner

We went through the plan together.

look through = parcourir / feuilleter

I looked through the brochure.

WITH

Main idea: connection, company, relationship

stay with = loger chez

I stayed with my family in Denmark.

get on with = bien s'entendre avec

I get on very well with my daughter-in-law.

catch up with = prendre des nouvelles

We caught up with each other over dinner.

OFF

Main idea: separation, removal, departure

take off = enlever

I took off my coat when I came in.

set off = partir

We set off early for the coast.

drop off = déposer

They dropped me off near the station.

4) Syntax lesson — easy comparison with French

The simple formula

Subject + Verb + Object + Place + Time

French can move elements more freely. English usually needs a clearer order.

Compare

  • 🇫🇷 Demain, avec eux, je vais visiter la côte.
  • 🇬🇧 I am going to visit the coast with them tomorrow.
  • 🇫🇷 Avec ma belle-fille, j'ai parlé du voyage.
  • 🇬🇧 I talked about the trip with my daughter-in-law.

In English, put the main verb early. Do not stack too many extras at the beginning.

Adjective position

In French, adjectives often come after the noun. In English, they usually come before the noun.

  • 🇫🇷 une petite ville danoise
  • 🇬🇧 a small Danish town
  • 🇫🇷 un beau paysage côtier
  • 🇬🇧 a beautiful coastal landscape

5) Practical dialogue — a real family-visit conversation

Daughter-in-law: What would you like to do today?

You: I would love to go out for a walk along the coast. We could also look through the town centre if you like.

Daughter-in-law: That sounds nice. We could set off after breakfast.

You: Perfect. I also wanted to ask about a good café near the harbour.

Daughter-in-law: Of course. We can go there and catch up properly.

Useful speaking chunks

  • ☕ We could go out for coffee.
  • 🌊 I would love to walk along the coast.
  • 🗺️ Could you tell me about a nice place to visit?
  • 😊 I really get on well with you.
  • 📍 What time should we set off?

Model response

Hello! Thank you again for inviting me. I had a wonderful time in Denmark with you.

I really enjoyed staying near the coast because it was calm, beautiful, and very relaxing.

I loved spending time with you, talking together, and discovering your daily life here.

The walks by the sea were lovely, and I also enjoyed the atmosphere, the houses, and the peaceful surroundings.

Everything was very warm and welcoming. Thank you for your kindness and for making me feel at home.

6) Guided writing — tell the story naturally

Writing frame

Write 6 to 8 lines about your stay in Denmark. Try to use at least:

  • one expression with out
  • one expression with with
  • one expression with through or along
  • a natural English word order

Possible model text

Last week, I stayed with my son and daughter-in-law in Denmark.

We went out several times and walked along the coast because the scenery was beautiful and peaceful.

We also looked through the town centre and spent quality time together.

I got on very well with my daughter-in-law, and we had lovely conversations over coffee and meals.

I really enjoyed the atmosphere, and I came back with wonderful memories from the trip.
