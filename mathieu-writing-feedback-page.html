Model answers

Ugraded versions

The first stays close to your original ideas. The second pushes the answer toward a stronger B1/B1+ level.

Improved version close to the original

In my opinion, films and documentaries can be a good way to improve listening and comprehension in English. For example, when you listen to native speakers in films, you can hear natural pronunciation, common expressions, and everyday idioms. This can help you communicate more naturally with other people. In addition, documentaries are useful because they often explain real topics with clear and informative vocabulary. If you regularly practise the words and expressions you hear, you can become more confident when speaking English. In conclusion, films and documentaries are a useful tool for learning English.

Stronger B1/B1+ version

I believe that films and documentaries can be an effective way to learn English, especially for improving listening skills. First, they expose learners to natural pronunciation, rhythm, and authentic expressions. For example, by watching films in English, learners can become more familiar with idioms and everyday language. In addition, documentaries are very useful because they focus on real-life topics and often use clear, informative vocabulary. However, watching alone is not enough: learners also need to repeat useful expressions and practise them actively. In conclusion, films and documentaries can be a very valuable learning tool when used regularly.
