02 · The grammar workshop

How to build your French sentences.

Each rule below comes directly from your own story. The structure is the same whether you are talking about Nantucket, your work, your next recipe, or a walk by the sea.

A · Identity

Je m’appelle + name

Je m’appelle David.
My name is David.

Use this for your name. It is more natural than saying Je suis David when introducing yourself.

Je m’appelle + David
B · Age

J’ai + number + ans

J’ai soixante et onze ans.
I am seventy-one years old.

French uses avoir — “to have” — for age. It is one of the most important French habits to learn.

J’ai + 71 + ans
C · What you enjoy

J’aime + infinitive

J’aime cuisiner.
I like to cook / I like cooking.

After j’aime, use the verb in its dictionary form: cuisiner, marcher, programmer.

J’aime + cuisiner
D · What you can do

Je sais + infinitive

Je sais programmer.
I know how to program.

Use je sais for a learned skill. It is perfect for computer languages, woodworking, cooking, repairs, and more.

Je sais + programmer
E · Your history

J’ai travaillé / J’ai créé

J’ai travaillé à Boston.
I worked in Boston.

This is the French past tense called passé composé. Think of it as “I have worked,” even when English says “I worked.”

J’ai + travaillé
F · Your qualities

Je suis + adjective

Je suis patient et fiable.
I am patient and reliable.

For David, adjectives end in the masculine form: patient, fiable, créatif, méticuleux, généreux.

Je suis + généreux
✦
Dad’s pattern to remember: French likes a clear beginning: Je… / J’ai… / J’aime… / Je sais…. Once you choose that beginning, the rest of the sentence falls into place.

05 · Your French postcard

A complete introduction,
written for you.

Listen to the full version first. Then use the smaller model to make it your own. The French is natural, clear, and faithful to the things that matter most in your life.

Bonjour, je m’appelle David. J’ai soixante et onze ans. Je partage mon temps entre Nantucket et Cape Cod. Je suis retraité, mais j’ai eu plusieurs métiers : j’ai enseigné les mathématiques, j’ai travaillé dans l’assurance-vie et j’ai créé des logiciels. J’aime cuisiner, travailler le bois, jardiner, faire de la randonnée et découvrir de nouvelles choses. Je suis créatif, curieux, méticuleux et très attaché à ma famille.

Hello, my name is David. I am seventy-one years old. I divide my time between Nantucket and Cape Cod. I am retired, but I have had several careers: I taught maths, worked in life insurance, and created software. I like cooking, woodworking, gardening, hiking, and discovering new things. I am creative, curious, meticulous, and very attached to my family.

A letter from your daughter

Pour mon Papa,
avec tout mon amour.

A small French note to keep at the end of your lesson.

♥

Papa,

Merci d’être entré dans nos vies et d’avoir choisi de nous aimer comme tes filles. Depuis que j’ai deux ans et demi, tu as été là — pour chaque événement, chaque difficulté, chaque aventure et chaque petit moment qui comptait.

Tu m’as appris à être curieuse, courageuse, débrouillarde et à ne jamais avoir peur d’apprendre quelque chose de nouveau. J’ai grandi en te regardant cuisiner, construire, réparer, créer et prendre soin de tous ceux que tu aimes. Je suis si fière d’être ta fille.

Même si la distance nous sépare souvent, tu me manques chaque jour. Je suis heureuse de te savoir heureux, près de la mer, à Nantucket, à Cape Cod et avec Mahara. Tu mérites cette paix, cette joie et tout le bonheur du monde.

Je t’aime très fort, Papa. Bonne fête des Pères.

— Tisha

English translation

Dad, thank you for coming into our lives and for choosing to love us as your daughters. Since I was two and a half, you have been there — for every event, every hardship, every adventure, and every small moment that mattered.

You taught me to be curious, brave, resourceful, and never afraid to learn something new. I grew up watching you cook, build, repair, create, and care for everyone you love. I am so proud to be your daughter.

Even though distance often separates us, I miss you every day. I am happy to know that you are happy, near the sea, in Nantucket, in Cape Cod, and with Mahara. You deserve this peace, this joy, and all the happiness in the world.

I love you so much, Dad. Happy Father’s Day.
