Task 1

Do team sports help people develop useful skills for work?

You have strong ideas here. The main improvement is to make your phrasing more natural and more precise.

Your original answer

I would say that team sports is an efficient apprenticeship to learn how to collaborate with teammates and develop the sense of sociability. For example, knowing how to interact with different people or how to carry out a project from the beginning to the end as a team. Even though human relationship can sometimes be difficult to stand on, team sports can be a good way to learn how to manage some type of conflicts that can occur in society. In conclusion, doing team sports is not only good to keep your body healthy but it is also an effective way to improve the connection skills with people.

Corrected version

I would say that team sports are an effective way to learn how to collaborate with teammates and develop social skills. For example, they teach you how to interact with different kinds of people and how to work toward a common goal as part of a team. Even though human relationships can sometimes be difficult to manage, team sports can be a good way to learn how to deal with certain types of conflict that can occur in society. In conclusion, doing team sports is not only good for keeping your body healthy, but it is also an effective way to improve your teamwork and communication skills.

You already do this well

You make a clear link between team sports, teamwork, and professional life.

Main language point

Team sports are, not team sports is. You also need more natural expressions.

Useful upgrade

Use social skills, teamwork, communication skills, and deal with conflict.

How to make this answer stronger

  1. Start with a direct opinion: I believe team sports help people develop useful skills for work.
  2. Use more natural work vocabulary: teamwork, cooperation, communication, conflict management.
  3. Make the example more concrete: work toward a common goal sounds more natural than carry out a project from beginning to end.
  4. Finish with a smoother conclusion: valuable professional skills sounds stronger than connection skills with people.

Stronger model answer

I believe that team sports help people develop many useful skills for work. First of all, they teach teamwork, communication, and cooperation. In a team sport, players need to listen to each other, share responsibilities, and work toward a common goal. These are all important skills in the workplace. In addition, team sports can help you learn how to manage conflict and deal with different personalities. This is useful because professional relationships are not always easy, and employees often need to stay calm and solve problems together. In conclusion, team sports are not only good for physical health, but they are also an excellent way to develop valuable professional skills.
Task 2

How should a company respond when a customer complains about poor service?

This answer already sounds thoughtful and practical. The main improvement is to make the phrasing more fluent and more professional.

Your original answer

In my view, it is in the interest of the company to keep going on professionalism that means providing the necessary information to the customer about the complaint and then ensure that the customer is satisfied with the information provided. If the customer complains about the delay, the company can show genuine empathy when responding, for example, "I understand how frustrating it is to wait that long." The company must offer a concrete solution, for example they might offer discounts for the next collaboration to apologize. Overall, the company has to learn from complaints to fix root causes and rebuild trust with customers.

Corrected version

In my view, it is in a company’s best interest to respond professionally. This means providing the customer with the necessary information about the complaint and making sure that the customer is satisfied with the response. If the customer complains about a delay, the company can show genuine empathy when replying, for example: “I understand how frustrating it is to wait that long.” The company should also offer a concrete solution. For example, it might offer a discount on a future purchase to apologise. Overall, a company has to learn from complaints in order to fix the root causes of problems and rebuild trust with customers.

You already do this well

You think like a professional: empathy, explanation, solution, and trust.

Main language point

Respond professionally is much more natural than keep going on professionalism.

Useful upgrade

Use future purchase, refund, replacement, resolve the issue, and rebuild trust.

How to make this answer stronger

  1. Begin simply and directly: A company should respond quickly, politely, and professionally.
  2. Use a clear structure: listen → apologise → explain → offer a solution → prevent the problem from happening again.
  3. Use customer-service vocabulary: show empathy, offer a solution, resolve the issue, improve customer satisfaction.
  4. Keep the conclusion focused on trust and improvement.

Stronger model answer

A company should respond to a customer complaint quickly, politely, and professionally. First, it should listen carefully to the customer and acknowledge the problem. Then, it should apologise sincerely and show empathy, especially if the poor service caused frustration or inconvenience. After that, the company should explain what happened clearly and offer a practical solution, such as a refund, a replacement, or a discount on a future purchase. It is also important to make sure the same problem does not happen again. In conclusion, a good response to a complaint should solve the problem, satisfy the customer, and help rebuild trust in the company.
Task 3

What makes someone professional at work?

You have a strong central idea here. The mechanic example is a very good idea and makes the answer realistic.

Your original answer

I would say that being professional at work is about the way people act, to be a good professional you have to be respectful, reliable and easy to work with others. In addition to that, I would say that the way you communicate with people shows if you’re a professional at work or not. For example, if you’re good as a mechanic but none of your coworkers can trust you or you’re not respectful of customers, no one would want to work with you because you’re not a professional at work. Overall, professionalism is when people know that you’re reliable, they can work with you without worrying about the quality of your work.

Corrected version

I would say that being professional at work is about the way people behave. To be a good professional, you have to be respectful, reliable, and easy to work with. In addition, the way you communicate with people shows whether you are professional or not. For example, even if you are a skilled mechanic, if your coworkers cannot trust you or if you are not respectful toward customers, people will not want to work with you because you are not behaving professionally. Overall, professionalism means that people know you are reliable and that they can work with you without worrying about the quality of your work.

You already do this well

You clearly show that professionalism is about attitude and behaviour, not only technical skill.

Main language point

Behave, skilled mechanic, and behaving professionally sound much more natural.

Useful upgrade

Use reliable, respectful, responsible, good communicator, and take responsibilities seriously.

How to make this answer stronger

  1. Define professionalism clearly: Being professional means being reliable, respectful, and responsible.
  2. Add 2–3 concrete qualities: punctual, honest, organised, clear communicator.
  3. Keep your mechanic example because it is strong and realistic.
  4. End with trust and consistency: people can depend on you.

Stronger model answer

In my opinion, someone is professional at work when they are reliable, respectful, and responsible. Professionalism is not only about technical skills; it is also about attitude and behaviour. A professional person communicates clearly, works well with others, and takes their responsibilities seriously. For example, a mechanic may be very skilled, but if coworkers cannot trust him and customers feel disrespected, he will not be seen as truly professional. Good work quality is important, but so are teamwork, honesty, and respect. Overall, professionalism means that people can trust you, depend on you, and feel comfortable working with you.

Your writing upgrade toolkit

Use this simple structure in future VTest opinion tasks.

Easy structure to remember

  1. Give your opinion.
  2. Give reason 1.
  3. Give an example.
  4. Give reason 2.
  5. Finish with a clear conclusion.
Opinion + reasons + example + conclusion = a stronger and clearer answer.
