1) Why questions matter

Simple idea

To speak well, you need to:

  1. recognise the question word,
  2. recognise the tense keyword,
  3. understand what information is needed,
  4. reuse part of the question in your answer.

Mini example

Question: Where did you go last weekend?

Keyword: did → past simple

Answer: You went to a dance performance last weekend.

The question already helps you build the answer.

2) WH question words — what information do you need?

These words tell you what kind of answer to give.

What...?Quoi ? / Que ?What do you do for work?
Where...?Où ?Where are you going this summer?
When...?Quand ?When did you go there?
Who...?Qui ?Who did you go with?
Why...?Pourquoi ?Why do you prefer the coast?
How...?Comment ?How was your trip?
How often...?À quelle fréquence ?How often do you run?
How long...?Combien de temps ?How long are you going to stay?

3) Question keywords by tense

🟢 Present simple — habits, routines, facts

Keywords

do / does

Use them for routine, habits, regular truth.

Examples

What do you do for work?

Where does your son live?

How often do you run?

🔵 Past simple — finished past events

Keyword

did

Use it for a finished action in the past.

Examples

Where did you go?

What did you do last Friday?

How did the performance go?

🟣 Present continuous — now / around now / arrangement

Keywords

am / is / are

Use them for what is happening now or a current arrangement.

Examples

What are you doing this weekend?

Who are you seeing tomorrow?

Why is she waiting?

🟠 Be going to — future plan or intention

Keywords

am going to / is going to / are going to

Use them for future plans and intentions.

Examples

Where are you going to stay?

What are you going to do in Denmark?

How long are you going to stay?

🟤 Present perfect — life experience / result / recent past

Keywords

have / has

Use them for experiences or a link between past and present.

Examples

What have you done so far?

How long have you worked as an assessor?

Why has this become important for you?

⚪ Would / would like / would rather — preference, polite question

Keywords

would

Use it for polite questions, preferences, wishes.

Examples

What would you like to do?

Where would you rather stay?

Why would that be better for you?

🟡 Can — ability, possibility, request

Keyword

can

Use it for ability, possibility, or asking someone to do something.

Examples

How can you explain that?

What can you do in this situation?

Where can you go for help?

4) How to build the question

Basic pattern

Question word + keyword + subject + main verb + rest

Where + did + you + go + last weekend?

What + do + you + do + for work?

What + are + you + going to + do + this summer?

Present simple

What do you do for work?

Past simple

What did you do last Friday?

Be going to

What are you going to do this summer?

5) How to build the answer

Reuse the question to create a strong answer.

Question

What did you do last Friday?

Answer pattern

Last Friday, you went to court as part of your work as an assessor.

Question

Where are you going to stay?

Answer pattern

You are going to stay near the coast with your family.

Easy answer strategy

  1. reuse the question word or time phrase,
  2. change the verb to the answer form,
  3. add one detail,
  4. add one reason, opinion, or feeling if possible.

7) Sentence order and dialogue practice

Sentence order: did / where / you / go ?

Sentence order: what / do / for work / you / do ?

Sentence order: what / going to / are / do / this summer / you ?

Dialogue trainer

Question

What are you going to do in Denmark this summer?

Useful answer

You are going to visit your family and spend time near the coast.

Question

Why do you prefer the coast?

Useful answer

You prefer the coast because it is calmer and more relaxing.

8) Final speaking challenge

Ask the question correctly, then answer it correctly.

Ask

  • What do you do for work?
  • What did you do last Friday?
  • Where are you going to go this summer?
  • How long have you worked as an assessor?
  • What would you like to do in Denmark?

Answer strategy

  • reuse the question words,
  • use the same tense in the answer,
  • add one detail,
  • finish with a reason, feeling, or opinion.
