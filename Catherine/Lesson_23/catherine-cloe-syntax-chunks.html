1) Lesson plan — what you will practise today

By the end of the lesson, you can…

  • build more natural English sentences
  • use useful chunks like talk to, talk about, wait for, look at, go out
  • ask and answer questions more easily
  • react naturally in conversation
  • write a short message or story more clearly

Simple idea to remember

English is often more fixed than French. In English, it is often easier to learn a group of words together than to translate word by word.

Good strategy: learn small useful chunks and practise them immediately.

2) Vocabulary studio — open by category

Open the category you need. Learn the whole expression, not just one word.

Family & catching up
  • 👪 stay with = loger chez • I stayed with my son and daughter-in-law.
  • 💬 talk to = parler à • I talked to my daughter-in-law.
  • 🗣️ talk about = parler de • We talked about our plans.
  • ❤️ get on with = bien s'entendre avec • I get on well with her.
  • ☕ catch up with = prendre des nouvelles • We caught up over coffee.
Travel & everyday actions
  • 🚆 arrive in = arriver en • We arrived in Denmark on Friday.
  • 🚗 pick up = aller chercher • My son picked me up at the station.
  • ⏳ wait for = attendre • We waited for the bus.
  • 👀 look at = regarder • We looked at the map together.
  • 🌊 go out = sortir • We went out for a walk by the sea.
Conversation reactions
  • ✨ Really? = Vraiment ?
  • 😊 That sounds lovely. = Ça a l'air charmant.

  • 🤔 What did you do then? = Qu'est-ce que vous avez fait ensuite ?

3) Verb + little-word lesson — organised by the particle

This is easier to understand when you group the expressions by the little word itself.

WITH

Main idea: company, relationship, doing something together

stay with = loger chez

I stayed with my family in Denmark.

go with = aller avec / convenir

This jacket goes with everything.

get on with = bien s'entendre avec

I get on well with my daughter-in-law.

ABOUT

Main idea: topic, subject

talk about = parler de

We talked about the trip.

think about = penser à

I am thinking about the next lesson.

learn about = apprendre sur

I learned about Danish culture.

TO

Main idea: direction, destination, person who receives the action

go to = aller à

We went to a small harbour.

talk to = parler à

I talked to my son.

listen to = écouter

I listened to her story.

Very important: after a movement verb, English often uses to.

I am in Denmark = position / location.

I am going to Denmark = movement / destination.

FOR

Main idea: target, goal, waiting, reason

wait for = attendre

We waited for the bus.

look for = chercher

I looked for a café.

thank someone for = remercier pour

I thanked them for their kindness.

AT

Main idea: point, target, place in a specific way

look at = regarder

We looked at old photos.

arrive at = arriver à

We arrived at the station at 6 p.m.

be good at = être bon en

She is good at languages.

OUT / UP / IN

Main idea: very common travel and daily-life particles

go out = sortir

We went out in the evening.

find out = découvrir

pick up = aller chercher

My son picked me up.

meet up with = retrouver

come in = entrer

Please come in.

check in = s'enregistrer

4) Syntax lesson — English order vs French order

Main pattern

English: Subject + Verb + Object

French: sometimes more flexible, especially with adverbs or adjectives.

🇫🇷 J'ai visité une très belle ville danoise.

🇬🇧 I visited a very beautiful Danish town.

Adjectives

In English, adjectives usually come before the noun.

🇫🇷 une petite ville calme

🇬🇧 a small quiet town

Adverbs

Adverbs often come after the verb, or before the main adjective/adverb group, but not always in the same place as in French.

🇫🇷 Nous avons parlé calmement.

🇬🇧 We spoke calmly.

Time & place

English often puts place and time later in the sentence.

🇬🇧 We walked by the sea in the afternoon.

place + time often come at the end

6) Practical dialogue — a real family-visit conversation

A: Hello! It is lovely to see you again. How was your trip?

B: It was very good, thank you. The journey was long, but everything went well.

A: Did you enjoy your stay in Denmark?

B: Yes, I did. I had a wonderful time with my son and daughter-in-law.

A: What did you do there?

B: We walked by the sea, talked a lot, and spent time together as a family.

A: Did you get on well with your daughter-in-law?

B: Yes, very well. We talked about many things, and I felt very welcome.

Model response

Hello! Thank you again for inviting me. I had a wonderful time in Denmark with you. I really enjoyed staying near the coast because it was calm, beautiful, and very relaxing. I loved spending time with you, talking together, and discovering your daily life there. The walks by the sea were lovely, and I also enjoyed the peaceful atmosphere. Everything was very warm and welcoming. I would love to come back one day.

7) Guided writing — tell the story naturally

Useful writing steps

  1. Say when and where
  2. Say who you were with
  3. Say what you did
  4. Add feelings/opinion
  5. Finish with a positive ending

Useful connectors

first, then, after that, because, so, finally

Possible model text

Last week, I went to Denmark to visit my son and my daughter-in-law. They live near the coast, so the scenery was very beautiful and peaceful. During my stay, we spent time together, talked a lot, and enjoyed lovely moments as a family. We went for walks, discovered the area, and enjoyed the sea and the fresh air. I really liked the atmosphere because it was quiet and relaxing. I feel very grateful because I had a fabulous time with them.

Include: when, where, who, what you did, one feeling, and one final sentence.
