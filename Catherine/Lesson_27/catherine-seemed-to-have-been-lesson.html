Catherine — perfect passive infinitive Studio

Use it when you want to say that something appeared to be true in the past, and that the action had already happened before that past moment. A full lesson to help you understand, build, and use this structure naturally when you describe complex situations, past impressions, and sensitive cases.

Target structure

They seemed to have been recruited and manipulated.

  • meaning
  • formation
  • affirmatives, negatives, questions
  • work, running, animals, zoo vocabulary

1) Meaning

They seemed to have been recruited and manipulated means:

It looked as if this had already happened before.

It is useful when you want to speak carefully and professionally, without saying that you know every detail with 100% certainty.

She seemed to have been frightened.

The animals seemed to have been well cared for.

The decision seemed to have been made earlier.

2) How to make it

subject + seemed + to have been + past participle

They seemed to have been recruited.

She seemed to have been supported.

The animals seemed to have been rescued.

Step by step

  1. Choose the subject
  2. Add seemed
  3. Add to have been
  4. Add a past participle

Useful participles

recruited, manipulated, protected, supported, placed, helped, guided, observed, cared for, rescued, treated, frightened, relieved

3) Affirmative, negative, questions

Affirmative

They seemed to have been recruited.

She seemed to have been supported.

The animals seemed to have been rescued.

Negative

They did not seem to have been protected.

She did not seem to have been informed.

The animals did not seem to have been stressed.

Questions

Did they seem to have been recruited?

Did she seem to have been supported?

Did the animals seem to have been well cared for?
