1) Preference phrases — say what you prefer

Useful preference structures

I prefer A to B.

I prefer peaceful places to noisy places.

I would rather + verb.

I would rather go for a walk.

I like... because...

I like nature because it helps me relax.

For me,... is better because...

For me, a small town is better because it is calmer.

Mini model

I prefer calm and meaningful activities to noisy or stressful situations. For example, I would rather spend time in nature than in a crowded place. For me, peaceful surroundings are better because they help me feel balanced and relaxed.

3) Comparatives — compare two things or situations

How to form comparatives

Short adjective + er + than

calm → calmer than

small → smaller than

More + long adjective + than

more interesting than

more emotional than

Irregular forms

good → better than

bad → worse than

Useful comparison sentences

A peaceful place is more relaxing than a crowded place.

A small event can be more moving than a very big event.

For me, meaningful work is better than repetitive work.

4) Superlatives — say which one is the most / the best

How to form superlatives

The + short adjective + est

the calmest, the smallest

The most + long adjective

the most difficult, the most emotional

Irregular forms

good → the best

bad → the worst

Useful superlative sentences

It was the most emotional part of the hearing.

The costumes were the most amazing part of the performance.

For me, the coast is the best place to relax.

5) Adjectives and adverbs — describe better

Adjectives describe nouns. Adverbs describe verbs, adjectives, or the whole sentence.

Adjectives

Use adjectives after be or before a noun.

Adverbs

Use adverbs to describe how you do something.

6) Guided speaking — compare and explain

Speaking topic A — compare two places

Useful frame

For me, __________ is better than __________ because __________.
It is __________ and __________.
I prefer __________ because __________.

Model

For me, the coast is better than a crowded city centre because it is more peaceful and more relaxing. It is also more beautiful for me. I prefer the coast because I feel calmer there.

Speaking topic B — compare two situations

Useful frame

This situation was __________ than __________.
It was also __________ because __________.
However, the outcome was __________.

Model

This case was more difficult than a simple hearing because the young women were very vulnerable. It was also more emotional for me. However, the final outcome was more positive and more supportive than I had expected.

Speaking topic C — talk about your preferences

Useful frame

I prefer __________ to __________.
I would rather __________ than __________.
For me, __________ is the best because __________.

Model

I prefer meaningful work to repetitive work. I would rather spend time in nature than in a noisy place. For me, peaceful surroundings are the best because they help me feel balanced and calm.

7) Guided writing — build a comparison paragraph

Writing recipe

Sentence 1: say your preference

Sentence 2: compare two things

Sentence 3: add one or two adjectives

Sentence 4: finish with a reason or conclusion

Model paragraph

I prefer peaceful places to crowded places. For me, the coast is calmer and more relaxing than a busy town centre. The atmosphere is quieter, and the surroundings are more beautiful. That is why it is the best place for me to relax.

Guided writing frame — preferences and comparison

I prefer __________ to __________.
For me, __________ is __________ than __________.
It is also __________ and __________.
That is why __________ is the __________ for me.

8) Final CLOE challenge — describe, compare, and justify

Use the grammar and vocabulary from the lesson. Keep your ideas simple and clear.

Oral prompts

  • Do you prefer a peaceful or a lively place? Why?
  • Compare a court situation and a family event.
  • What is the most memorable part of a performance?
  • What kind of work do you find more meaningful?

Checklist

  • Use at least 1 preference phrase
  • Use at least 1 comparative
  • Use at least 1 superlative
  • Use adjectives and adverbs correctly
  • Finish with a reason or a conclusion
