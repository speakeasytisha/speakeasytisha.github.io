3) Grammar lesson — which / that / where / why / when

Why this matters

These little words help you connect ideas. They make your English sound more fluid, more elegant, and less like a list of short sentences.

Simple guide

where = for a place

The spa where I stayed was peaceful.

when = for a time / moment

It was the moment when I finally relaxed.

why = for a reason

That is why I want to go back.

that = for a thing / idea in everyday English

It was a place that I really liked.

which = for a thing; often sounds a little more descriptive or written

The massage, which lasted an hour, was wonderful.

4) Grammar lesson — this / that / these / those

These words help you point to things clearly in speaking and writing.

this = singular, near

This spa is beautiful.

that = singular, far / not near / not immediate

That place looks lovely.

these = plural, near

These treatments are very relaxing.

those = plural, far / not near

Those photos remind me of the trip.

5) Spa story lab — tell the experience naturally

Story checklist

  • Where was it?
  • Who were you with?
  • What type of place was it?
  • What treatments did you have?
  • How many days were you there?
  • How did you feel before and after?

Model

Last week, I went to a spa with my sister. It was a peaceful and elegant place near the coast. We stayed there for two days. I had a massage, a facial, and some time in the thermal bath. Before the stay, I felt tired and tense. Afterward, I felt calmer, lighter, and much more relaxed.

7) Guided writing — from simple sentences to fluid English

Writing recipe

Start with when + where + who.

Add what the place was like.

Add what you did.

Finish with before / after feelings.

Use one or two linking words like where, that, which, why.

Possible model text

Last week, I went to a spa with a friend. It was a beautiful place where everything felt calm and elegant. The treatment that I enjoyed most was the massage, which was deeply relaxing. Before the stay, I felt rather tired and tense. Afterward, I felt lighter, calmer, and much more positive.
