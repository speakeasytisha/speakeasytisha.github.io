1) Past simple — complete grammar toolkit 🧩

Form • rules • questions • negatives • short answers • time markers • pronunciation.

A) When do we use past simple?

  • Finished past: yesterday / last week / in 2024
  • A story sequence: First → Then → After that → Finally
  • Facts in the past: I lived in… / We travelled…
French tip: passé simple here = prétérit (past finished).

B) Form (affirmative)

Regular: verb + -ed → visited, treated, called
Irregular: special form → go→went, see→saw, write→wrote
  • I visited Denmark.
  • We went on safari.

C) Questions & negatives

Question: Did + subject + base verb?
Negative: subject + didn’t + base verb
  • Did you visit Denmark?
  • I didn’t go to court.
  • Yes, I did / No, I didn’t.

D) Spelling rules (regular -ed) ✍️

+ed

work → worked
Most verbs

+d

arrive → arrived
Verb ends in -e

y → ied

study → studied
Consonant + y

Double consonant: stop → stopped (CVC pattern).

E) Pronunciation of -ed 🔊

/t/

watched, worked
after /k/ /p/ /f/ /s/ /ʃ/ /tʃ/

/d/

called, cleaned
after vowels /b/ /g/ /v/ /m/…

/ɪd/

visited, needed
after /t/ or /d/

Goal: in speaking, say the ending clearly (especially /ɪd/).
