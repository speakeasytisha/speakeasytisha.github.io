2) Grammar booster

Review the grammar you already know, but use it better and more automatically.

Present Simple

facts • habits • preferences

Use it for: what is generally true.

  • You prefer the beach.
  • You travel in May.
  • You like quiet hotels.

Present Continuous

now • plan now

Use it for: what is happening now or your current plan.

  • You are travelling to Hossegor.
  • You are booking a hotel.
  • You are looking for a restaurant.

Would like

polite request

Use it for: sounding polite in hotels and restaurants.

  • I would like to book a room.
  • I would like a table for two.
  • I would like to know if parking is included.

See the difference

You prefer the beach.general preference
You are travelling to the beach.current plan
I want a room.simple / direct
I would like a room.more polite
