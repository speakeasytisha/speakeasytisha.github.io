📖 Section E — Reading Comprehension

Text 1 — Theme Park Team Notice

Tomorrow, the park will be busier than usual because a special evening event is taking place. Team members should arrive fifteen minutes early, check their uniforms and review the safety instructions. If an attraction closes temporarily, guests should be informed calmly and offered another recommendation. Lost-child situations must be reported to a supervisor immediately.

Text 2 — Travel Email

Hello Angie, your train to London leaves at 8:40 a.m. from platform 6. Please arrive at least twenty minutes early. You can bring one large suitcase and one small bag. If the train is delayed, check the information screens or speak to a member of staff. Your hotel reservation begins on Friday afternoon.

Text 3 — Customer Complaint

Dear Customer Service, I bought a sweatshirt from your shop yesterday, but I noticed a tear when I arrived home. I still have the receipt and the original packaging. I would prefer an exchange, but I would accept a refund if the same size is no longer available.

🎧 Section F — Listening Comprehension

In the official exam, recordings may be heard only once. For realistic practice, try listening only once before answering.

1. Airport announcement

Attention passengers. Flight 238 to London has been delayed by 45 minutes because of bad weather. Please remain near gate 12 and check the information screens for updates.

2. Restaurant conversation

Good evening. Before you order, could you tell me whether anyone at the table has a food allergy? We can check the ingredients with the kitchen team.

3. Lost property

Excuse me, I think I left my wallet near the gift shop about ten minutes ago. It is small, black and has a silver zip. Could you help me look for it?

4. Manager instructions

First, welcome the guests and check their tickets. Then, explain the safety instructions clearly. Finally, make sure everyone is seated before the attraction starts.

📸 Section G — Realistic Photo Description

Choose one or complete both. Describe the general scene, foreground, background, people, actions, feelings and your opinion.

Photo 1 — Tourists Looking at a Map

Useful clues: tourists, map, directions, foreground, city, confused, planning

A2: This picture shows some tourists. They are looking at a map. They are outside in a city.

B1: This picture shows a group of tourists looking at a map. In the foreground, they are standing together and trying to find their way. They seem interested but a little confused.

B1+: This picture shows several tourists exploring a city. In the foreground, they are studying a large map and discussing the route they should take. They may be looking for a famous attraction. The atmosphere seems relaxed, and I would help them by giving clear directions.

Photo 2 — Restaurant Service Environment

Useful clues: restaurant, tables, customers, staff, service, atmosphere, welcome

A2: This picture shows a restaurant. There are tables and chairs. The restaurant looks clean.

B1: This picture shows a modern restaurant. In the foreground, there are several tables ready for customers. The atmosphere looks calm and welcoming.

B1+: This picture shows a modern restaurant prepared to welcome customers. The tables are neatly arranged, and the atmosphere appears comfortable and professional. Staff working here would need to greet guests, answer questions and provide efficient service.

✍ Section H — Writing Tasks

Task 1 — Professional Self-Introduction

Write 70–100 words about your experience, qualities, progress and future goals.

A2: My name is Angie. I am motivated and friendly. I have worked with customers. I am improving my English because I would like to work at Disney.

B1: My name is Angie, and I live near Nantes. I have worked with customers before, especially at Carrefour Market. I am patient, motivated and friendly. I am currently improving my English because I would like to work at Disney in the future.

B1+: My name is Angie. I am motivated, patient and passionate about customer service. My past experiences have helped me become more confident with customers, and I am currently improving my English step by step. In the future, I would love to work at Disney and create memorable experiences for international guests.

Task 2 — Reply to a Complaint

A guest waited for an attraction, but it closed before their turn. Apologise, explain and offer a solution.

A2: Dear guest, I am sorry. The attraction was closed. You can try another attraction.

B1: Dear guest, I am sorry for the inconvenience. The attraction was temporarily closed for safety reasons. You could try another attraction nearby.

B1+: Dear guest, thank you for your message. I understand your frustration, and I am sorry for the inconvenience. The attraction was temporarily closed for safety reasons. On your next visit, you could check the app for wait times or ask a cast member for another recommendation nearby.

Task 3 — Incident Report

A guest reported a lost wallet near a shop. Write a short factual report.

A guest reported a lost black wallet near the gift shop at approximately 3 p.m. I asked for a description, checked where the guest had last seen it and contacted my supervisor. The guest was advised to visit the lost property office.
