⏳ Chapter 1 — When do we use the Past Simple?

Use the Past Simple for finished actions in the past. The action is finished and we often know when it happened.

Finished action

I worked at Carrefour.

Past experience

I visited Disneyland Paris.

Something learned

I learned new vocabulary.

Completed event

I had an interview.

Past Simple signal words: yesterday • last week • last month • last year • two years ago • in 2024 • when I was younger • during my last job

⚙️ Chapter 3 — Regular Verbs Academy

Affirmative structure

Subject + verb + ED

I worked. She helped. They visited.

Pronouns

I worked / You worked / He worked / She worked / We worked / They worked

With regular verbs, the past form is the same for every pronoun.

Most verbs

Add -ed

work → worked

Ending in -e

Add -d

love → loved

Consonant + y

y → ied

study → studied

Short CVC verb

Double consonant + ed

stop → stopped

Easy → transform the verb

Medium → fill in the blank

Hard → write a Disney sentence

❌ Chapter 5 — Negative Magic

Negative structure

Subject + didn’t + base verb

I didn’t work yesterday.

She didn’t visit Disney.

Important rule

After didn’t, use the base verb.

❌ I didn’t worked.

✅ I didn’t work.

Easy → choose the negative form

Medium → rewrite positive to negative

Hard → correct the mistake

❓ Chapter 6 — Question Kingdom

Yes / No question

Did + subject + base verb?

Did you work at Carrefour?

WH question

WH + did + subject + base verb?

What did you learn?

Easy → choose the correct question

Medium → word order builder

Hard → write an interview question

🎤 Chapter 10 — Disney Interview Training

Question 1

What was your last job?

A2: My last job was at Carrefour Market. I sold vegetables.
B1: My last work experience was at Carrefour Market. I helped customers and learned about different products.
B1+: One of my most useful experiences was working at Carrefour Market because it helped me understand customer service and become more confident.

Question 2

What did you learn?

A2: I learned new vocabulary.
B1: I learned how to speak more clearly with customers.
B1+: This experience taught me how to stay calm, communicate clearly and work more efficiently.
