⏳ Chapter 1 — What time period does the Present Perfect represent?

The Present Perfect connects the past to now. It often answers: “What experience or progress do you have now?”

Past Simple

Finished past moment.

Last year, I worked at Carrefour.

Present Perfect

Past experience with a connection to now.

I have worked with customers.

Now

The result matters now.

Now, I feel more confident.

Present Perfect = experience / progress / result until now.
I have improved my English. I have learned new vocabulary. I have visited Disneyland Paris.

⭐ Chapter 2 — How to form the Present Perfect

Affirmative

Subject + have / has + past participle

I have improved my English.

She has learned new words.

Pronouns

I / you / we / they → have

he / she / it → has

I have worked. She has worked.

Regular participle

Verb + ed

work → worked

Irregular participle

3rd column

see → seen

Contractions

I have = I’ve
She has = She’s

I’ve learned a lot.

Do not use did

Present Perfect uses have/has.

I have visited Disney.

Easy → choose have or has

Medium → choose the past participle

Hard → build a Present Perfect sentence

❌ Chapter 3 — Negative form

Structure

Subject + haven’t / hasn’t + past participle

I haven’t worked at Disney yet.

She hasn’t taken the VTest yet.

Important

Use the past participle after haven’t / hasn’t.

❌ I haven’t saw Disney.

✅ I haven’t seen Disney.

❓ Chapter 4 — Questions

Yes / No question

Have / Has + subject + past participle?

WH question

WH + have / has + subject + past participle?

What have you learned?

🪄 Chapter 5 — Key Words Magic

ever

Questions about life experience.

Have you ever worked in customer service?

never

Negative life experience.

I have never worked at Disney.

already

Done before now.

I have already visited Disneyland Paris.

yet

Questions / negatives. Often at the end.

Have you taken the VTest yet?

for

Duration.

I have studied English for several months.

since

Starting point.

I have studied English since January.

Easy → choose the keyword

Medium → for or since?

Hard → fill in the blank

🎯 New VTest Grammar Rule — Present Perfect vs Past Simple

This is the logical new grammar rule now: experience until now vs. finished past moment.

Present Perfect

No exact finished time, or experience until now.

I have worked with customers.

ever, never, already, yet, for, since

Past Simple

Finished action at a clear past time.

Last year, I worked at Carrefour.

yesterday, last year, ago, in 2024

🏰 Disney Interview Builder

🎤 Oral Practice — Disney Interview Questions

Question 1

Have you ever worked with customers?

A2: Yes, I have. I worked at Carrefour.
B1: Yes, I have worked with customers at Carrefour Market. It helped me understand customer service.
B1+: Yes, I have already worked with customers, especially at Carrefour Market, where I learned how to communicate more clearly and stay professional.

Question 2

What have you learned during your English training?

A2: I have learned new vocabulary.
B1: I have learned how to speak about my experience, my qualities and my Disney goals.
B1+: I have learned how to structure my answers, use past and present tenses more clearly, and speak more confidently about my professional goals.

✍️ Written Practice — Upgrade your answer

Write a short paragraph using Present Perfect + Past Simple + a result.

Model answers by level

A2: I have worked with customers. Last year, I worked at Carrefour. It helped me become more confident.

B1: I have worked with customers before. Last year, I worked at Carrefour Market, where I sold fruit and vegetables. This has helped me understand customer service better.

B1+: I have already gained some customer-service experience through my work at Carrefour Market. Last year, I sold products, answered questions and learned how to communicate more clearly. This has helped me become more confident and better prepared for a future role at Disney.
