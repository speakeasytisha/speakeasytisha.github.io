2) Grammar toolkit — complete (form • rule • questions • negatives) 🧩

Each section includes: rule • how to use • examples • micro exercises with instant feedback.

A) Present perfect — updates & experience ✅

Form have/has + past participle (I’ve / She’s)
Affirmative I’ve met her. • She’s moved.
Negative I haven’t met her yet. • She hasn’t called.
Question Have you met her yet? • Has she moved?
Short answers Yes, I have. / No, I haven’t.
Mini conjugation
I / you / we / they have (’ve) + pp
he / she has (’s) + pp
Negative haven’t / hasn’t + pp
Question Have/Has + subject + pp?

B) Present perfect — how to use 🎯

Experience Have you ever…? • I’ve never…
Recent I’ve just finished. • I’ve already sent it.
Unfinished time today / this week / recently / lately
Result now I’ve lost my keys (now I can’t find them).

C) Present perfect vs past simple — finished time ⏳

Past simple finished time: yesterday / last week / in 2024
Present perfect no finished time (catching up): I’ve met her.
Past Q Did you call her yesterday? (did + base)
Past Neg I didn’t call yesterday. (didn’t + base)

D) for / since — duration 🧷

for duration: for 2 years / for a few months
since starting point: since 2023 / since Monday
Question How long have you…?
