01 · TIME CLUES + MEANING

Use signal words as clues. Then choose the future form according to meaning.

Signal words help you notice time, but they do not always choose the tense. First identify the meaning: routine, plan, arranged appointment, promise, prediction or official schedule.

Des mots comme tomorrow, next week et in August indiquent le futur, mais ils ne choisissent pas le temps à eux seuls. Il faut d’abord se demander : intention, rendez-vous organisé, promesse, prédiction ou horaire officiel ?

🎯

By the end of this page, you can:
talk about the move, housing plans, family arrangements and future travel with more accurate grammar.

🧭
One reliable method

1. Find the time clue. 2. Ask “What do I mean?” 3. Choose the tense.

Strong clues

every day, usually, often, never often point to the present simple. now, right now, at the moment, look!, listen! often point to the present continuous.

Flexible clues

today, this week, tomorrow, next week, in August, later, currently tell you when. They do not choose the tense alone.

Today can use different tenses: Today, we are meeting the agent at 10:00. / Today, we are going to check the house documents. / I will call the bank today.

02 · THE FUTURE MAP

Four ways to talk about the future.

English offers several future forms. Each one carries a slightly different message.

PLAN / INTENTION

be going to

Use it for an intention or a plan already decided before you speak.

subject + am/is/are going to + base verb

We are going to move to Lisbon in August.

Helpful clues: this summer, next year, in August, my plan is…

going to : intention ou plan déjà décidé.

FIXED ARRANGEMENT

present continuous

Use it for a planned arrangement, often with another person, a time or a place.

subject + am/is/are + verb-ing

We are meeting an estate agent on Thursday.

Helpful clues: at 10, on Thursday, next week, with…

Le présent continu : rendez-vous ou arrangement concret, souvent avec une heure, un jour ou une autre personne.

PROMISE / PREDICTION

will

Use it for a promise, an offer, a spontaneous decision, or an opinion-based prediction.

subject + will + base verb

I will call you this evening. I think Lisbon will be a good place for us.

Helpful clues: I think, probably, maybe, don’t worry, I promise

will : promesse, offre, décision spontanée ou prédiction fondée sur une opinion.

OFFICIAL SCHEDULE

present simple

Use it for official timetables, transport schedules and fixed programmes.

subject + present simple verb

The flight leaves at 09:40. The appointment starts at 11:00.

Helpful clues: flight timetable, programme, official schedule

Le présent simple : horaires officiels, vols, programmes et calendriers fixes.

🔑 Future clues: meaning first

These words are useful clues, but they do not choose the tense alone. First decide what you mean: plan, arrangement, promise/prediction or timetable.

Ces mots sont des indices utiles, mais ils ne choisissent pas le temps à eux seuls. Il faut d’abord décider du sens : plan, arrangement, promesse/prédiction ou horaire.

be going to

plan, intend, decided, look!
Use for an intention already decided or evidence you can see.

present continuous

appointment, booked, arranged, meeting, with…, on Thursday at…
Use for a fixed arrangement.

will

I think, probably, perhaps, maybe, I promise, don’t worry, I’ll…
Use for an opinion, promise, offer or decision now.

present simple

timetable, schedule, flight, train, starts, leaves, arrives
Use for an official timetable.

Flexible time words: today, this week, tomorrow, next week, in August, later tell you when. The meaning tells you which form.

💡
Important:

Tomorrow does not automatically mean will. Compare: “We are meeting the agent tomorrow” (arrangement) / “I think it will rain tomorrow” (prediction).

03 · STAGE 1 — AFFIRMATIVE SENTENCES

Start with one pronoun: I.

First, practise the form without changing the subject. This makes the grammar pattern easier to see.

Choose the correct affirmative form.

Every sentence starts with I.

1. I ___ for a family home in Lisbon.

2. I ___ the estate agent on Thursday afternoon.

3. Don’t worry, I ___ the bank this afternoon.

4. I ___ good opportunities for my family.

04 · STAGE 2 — NEGATIVE SENTENCES

Keep the same pronoun: I.

Now focus on the negative forms. Notice where not goes.

Future meaningAffirmativeNegativeQuestion
IntentionI am going to move.I am not going to move.Am I going to move?
ArrangementI am meeting the agent.I am not meeting the agent.Am I meeting the agent?
Promise / predictionI will call.I will not call. / I won’t call.Will I call?

Choose the correct negative form.

Every sentence starts with I.

1. I ___ the house before we receive the funds.

2. I ___ the lawyer today; the meeting is tomorrow.

3. I ___ to send the documents. I promise.

4. I ___ easy at first, but it will be worthwhile.

05 · STAGE 3 — QUESTIONS

Use one question pronoun: you.

Questions change the word order. Start with the helper verb: Are or Will.

Choose the correct question.

Every question uses you.

1. ___ to Lisbon in August?

2. ___ the estate agent next week?

3. ___ the bank before Friday?

4. ___ a good place for your daughters?

Stage 4 · Pronouns change

Now switch subjects.

Read the whole sentence carefully before choosing.

1. My wife and I ___ a new life in Portugal.

2. My eldest daughter ___ her apprenticeship adviser on Monday.

3. I think our new neighbourhood ___ safer for the children.

4. The flight to Lisbon ___ at 09:40.

06 · TIME GRAMMAR

Future plans need accurate time words.

Use these prepositions to make your arrangements and schedules clear. They tell us when something happens; they do not choose the tense.

AT

Exact time

Use at with a precise clock time.

  • at 10:00
  • at half past six
  • at the weekend
  • at home / at work

We are meeting at 10:00.

ON

Days and dates

Use on with days and specific dates.

  • on Thursday
  • on 15 August
  • on Monday morning
  • on board

We are travelling on Saturday.

IN

Months, years, periods

Use in with months, years and longer periods.

  • in August
  • in 2026
  • in the morning
  • in Lisbon / in Portugal

We are going to move in August.

🚫 No preposition: say next week, this Friday, last year and every day — not in next week or on this Friday.

Choose the correct time word.

1. We are going to move ___ August.

2. I am meeting the agent ___ 14:30.

3. My daughter is meeting her adviser ___ Tuesday.

4. We are going to visit Lisbon ___ next week.

08 · LISTENING: A FAMILY PLAN

Listen for the future meaning.

Play the message twice. Then choose the answers. Focus on the reason, the arrangement and the timetable.

My family and I are going to move to Lisbon in August. We are meeting an estate agent on Thursday at ten o’clock. I think the move will be challenging at first, but it will be a good opportunity for our daughters. Our flight leaves at 09:40 in the morning.

Quick check

1. The family ___ an estate agent on Thursday.

2. The flight ___ at 09:40.

09 · FINISH

Keep the full decision process in mind.

1. Find the time clue. 2. Identify the meaning. 3. Choose the form: decided plan → going to; arranged appointment → present continuous; promise/opinion → will; official schedule → present simple.
