01 · TIME CLUES + MEANING AT WORK

Use time clues as evidence. Then say exactly what you mean.

In airline catering, words such as tomorrow, Friday and at 07:45 are essential — but they do not select the verb form alone. Decide whether you mean a plan, a confirmed arrangement, a promise, a prediction or an official schedule.

Dans la restauration aérienne, le futur n’est pas une règle abstraite. Il permet de préciser ce que l’équipe prévoit de préparer, ce qui est déjà organisé, ce que vous promettez de faire et ce qu’un horaire officiel impose.

🎯

By the end of this page, you can:
confirm an order, describe the next steps, ask clear future questions and use time prepositions in a professional context.

✈️
Professional decision check

Time word + meaning = clear message for the client, driver and kitchen team.

Time expressions that are flexible

today, tomorrow, this afternoon, next week, in August, at 07:45, on Friday tell you when the action happens. They do not automatically choose the tense.

Future meaning decides the form

planned production → going to. confirmed handover / appointment → present continuous. commitment to a client → will. flight slot / timetable → present simple.

Same time word, different message: Today, the team is preparing a special order. / Today, we are meeting the driver at 07:45. / I will send the confirmation today. / The flight leaves today at 09:40.

02 · FUTURE MAP FOR WORK

Same future. Different professional message.

Choose the form that tells the client exactly what type of future action you mean.

DECIDED PLAN

be going to

Use it for a production or service plan that has already been decided.

We are going to prepare the revised order.

We are going to use oven-proof containers.

Useful with: planned changes, intended actions, decided solutions.

Plan déjà décidé : We are going to prepare…

FIXED ARRANGEMENT

present continuous

Use it for a confirmed operational arrangement, often with a person, a date, a time or a location.

We are delivering the order at 07:45.

I am speaking with the client at 16:00.

Useful with: at 07:45, on Friday, with the driver, at the airport.

Arrangement fixé : We are delivering…

PROMISE / QUICK RESPONSE

will

Use it for a promise, offer, quick decision or prediction based on your opinion.

I will send the confirmation by 16:00.

Don’t worry, I will check the labels.

Useful with: I promise, don’t worry, I think, probably, I’ll…

Promesse ou réaction immédiate : I will send…

OFFICIAL SCHEDULE

present simple

Use it for a flight timetable, official deadline, transport schedule or planned programme.

The aircraft arrives at 07:30.

The delivery window opens at 07:45.

Useful with: flight schedule, official order details, delivery slot.

Horaire officiel : The aircraft arrives…

🔑 Future clues for professional communication

These words are useful clues, but they do not choose the tense alone. First decide what you mean: plan, arrangement, promise/prediction or timetable.

Ces mots sont des indices utiles, mais ils ne choisissent pas le temps à eux seuls. Il faut d’abord décider du sens : plan, arrangement, promesse/prédiction ou horaire.

be going to

plan, intend, decided, this summer, in August
Use for a plan already decided or evidence you can see.

present continuous

appointment, booked, arranged, meeting, with…, on Thursday at…
Use for a fixed arrangement.

will

I think, probably, perhaps, maybe, I promise, don’t worry, I’ll…
Use for an opinion, promise, offer or decision now.

present simple

timetable, schedule, programme, flight, leaves, starts, opens, arrives
Use for an official timetable.

Important: tomorrow, on Friday, at 07:45 and by 16:00 show time or deadline. First decide if you are describing a plan, arrangement, promise or timetable.

💡
Key point:

Tomorrow is not a tense. Compare: “We are delivering the order tomorrow at 07:45” (fixed arrangement) / “I think the new process will save time tomorrow” (prediction).

03 · STAGE 1 — AFFIRMATIVE SENTENCES

Start with one pronoun: I.

Keep the subject stable. Focus only on the future form and the base verb.

Choose the correct affirmative form.

Every sentence starts with I.

1. I ___ the special dietary requirements before production.

2. I ___ the driver at the airport at 07:30.

3. I ___ the updated quote before 16:00. I promise.

4. I ___ delivery delays in the future.

04 · STAGE 2 — NEGATIVE SENTENCES

Still one pronoun: I.

Use negative sentences to reassure a client, rule out an option or clarify what is not scheduled.

4. I ___ any further changes, but I will stay available.

05 · STAGE 3 — QUESTIONS

One question pronoun: you.

Ask a clear question before you make assumptions about an order or a requirement.

Choose the correct question.

Every question uses you.

1. ___ the final quantity today?

2. ___ the order to the aircraft at 07:45?

3. ___ the revised order by email?

4. ___ any vegetarian meals?

Stage 4 · Pronouns change

Now change the subject.

Use the form that matches the person or thing.

1. The team ___ three different cold breakfast meals.

2. The driver ___ at the airport at 07:30.

3. I think the new direct-delivery system ___ more efficient.

4. The delivery window ___ at 07:45 local time.

07 · TIME & DEADLINE GRAMMAR

At, on and in — plus one essential deadline word: by.

Use these prepositions to make operational details precise. They tell us when or by when something happens; they do not choose the future form.

AT

Exact times

at 07:45 · at the airport · at the gate

We are delivering the order at 07:45.

ON

Days & dates

on Friday · on 25 June · on board

The team is preparing the order on Thursday.

IN

Periods & places

in the morning · in August · in the kitchen

We are checking the labels in the morning.

⏳
Use by for a deadline:

“I will send the confirmation by 16:00” means no later than 16:00. “I will work until 16:00” means the activity continues up to 16:00.

Choose the correct time word.

1. The driver is arriving ___ 07:30.

2. We are preparing the revised order ___ Friday morning.

3. The order will be ready ___ the morning.

4. I will send the confirmation ___ 16:00.

10 · FINISH

Use the same decision process in every client update.

1. Find the time expression. 2. Identify the operational meaning. 3. Choose: plan → going to; confirmed arrangement → present continuous; commitment / quick response → will; official flight or delivery timetable → present simple.
