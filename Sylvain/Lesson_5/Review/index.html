01 · TIME CLUES + MEANING = THE RIGHT TENSE

First, read the time clue. Then ask: what do I mean?

Signal words are useful. Some are strong clues; others are flexible. A flexible time word tells you when something happens, but the meaning of the whole sentence chooses the tense.

Les expressions de futur comme tomorrow, next week et in August sont utiles, mais elles ne choisissent pas le temps. C’est le sens qui choisit le temps.

🎯

After this review, you can:
choose and conjugate the future form in positive sentences, negatives and questions.

1

Strong signal words

every day, usually, often, never often suggest the present simple. now, right now, at the moment, look!, listen! often suggest the present continuous.

2

Flexible time words

today, this week, tomorrow, next week, in August, later, currently tell you when. They do not choose the tense on their own.

3

Meaning chooses the future form

Plan / evidence → going to. Arrangement → present continuous. Promise / opinion → will. Official schedule → present simple.

🔍 One word, five possible tenses: today

Arrangement

Today, we are meeting the estate agent at 10:00.

Current activity

Today, the team is preparing a special order.

Plan

Today, we are going to check the house documents.

Promise

I will send the confirmation today.

Schedule

The flight leaves today at 09:40.

Important correction: today is a flexible time word. It never automatically means the present continuous.

🔑 Key words and signal phrases — use them carefully

These words are useful clues, but they do not choose the tense alone. First decide what you mean: plan, arrangement, promise/prediction or timetable.

Ces mots sont des indices utiles, mais ils ne choisissent pas le temps à eux seuls. Il faut d’abord décider du sens : plan, arrangement, promesse/prédiction ou horaire.

be going to

plan, intend, decided, look!
Use for a decided plan or evidence you can see.

present continuous

appointment, booked, arranged, meeting, with…, on Thursday at…
Use for a fixed arrangement.

will

I think, probably, perhaps, maybe, I promise, don’t worry, I’ll…
Use for a promise, offer, decision now or opinion prediction.

present simple

timetable, schedule, flight, train, starts, leaves, arrives
Use for an official timetable.

Flexible time words: today, this week, tomorrow, next week, in August, later and currently tell you when. The whole message tells you which tense.

🔎
A quick comparison:

We are meeting the driver at 07:30 = our arrangement. The flight leaves at 09:40 = official flight timetable. I will call the driver = promise / decision now. We are going to change the packaging = already-decided plan.

Two essential rules:

will + base verb: I will send, not will sends or will to send. going to + base verb: We are going to move, not going to moving.

07 · TIME PREPOSITIONS + DEADLINES

At, on, in — and no preposition.

At, on, in and by make schedules, appointments, travel and deadlines precise. They tell you when or by when; they do not select the tense.

AT

Precise time

at 07:45 · at 10:00 · at the airport · at home

The delivery starts at 07:45.

ON

Day / date

on Monday · on 15 August · on Friday morning · on board

We are meeting on Thursday.

IN

Month / year / period

in August · in 2026 · in the morning · in Lisbon

We are going to move in August.

BY

Deadline: no later than

by 16:00 · by Friday · by the end of the day

I will send the confirmation by 16:00.

🚫 No preposition before: this afternoon · next week · last year · every Friday.

Choose the correct option.
