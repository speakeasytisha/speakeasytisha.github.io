03 · LANGUAGE FOUNDATION

Build stronger sentences: subject + verb + detail.

For your daily professional communication, simple present-tense sentences are extremely useful. They describe routines, services, responsibilities and facts.

Pour votre communication professionnelle quotidienne, les phrases simples au présent sont très utiles. Elles décrivent les habitudes, les services, les responsabilités et les faits.

Think in blocks

We + prepare + fresh meals for several airlines.

The logistics company + delivers + the orders to the airport.

1

I / we / they

Use the base verb.

We prepare meals.
They deliver the orders.

Avec I / we / they, on utilise le verbe sans -s.

2

He / she / it

Add -s to most verbs.

The company prepares meals.
The driver delivers the order.

Avec he / she / it, le verbe prend généralement -s.

3

Negative

Use do not / does not + base verb.

We do not deliver directly.
The company does not manage the final step.

Après do not / does not, le verbe reste à la base : no -s.

4

Question

Use Do / Does + subject + base verb?

Do you prepare special meals?
Does the driver collect the order?

Dans une question avec Do / Does, le verbe reste à la base.

🧩

Quick check — choose the correct sentence

Read carefully: what changes after we, the company and does?

Look first at the subject: we = base verb; the company = verb + -s; after does = base verb.

05 · SPEAKING LAB

Explain your workflow step by step.

Clear sequencing helps people understand you. Use first, then, after that and finally to describe a routine professionally.

Une chronologie claire aide les autres à vous comprendre. Utilisez first, then, after that et finally pour décrire une routine de façon professionnelle.

1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → 5

Useful question: “Could you explain your usual process?”

Put the service steps in order.

Drag the cards, or use the arrow buttons. Then press Check my order.

Think about the real journey: the customer asks → the kitchen works → the order is prepared → it leaves the kitchen → the airline receives it.

  2. ⠿
    We receive and check the client’s order.menu, quantity, time and special requirements
  3. ⠿
    The airline receives the order.the service is complete
  4. ⠿
    Our team prepares the meals.fresh food, quality checks and timing
  5. ⠿

Build your professional sentence

Choose options, then personalise the result.

SPEAKING CHALLENGE

Explain the service in 45 seconds.

  1. Start with: “I run…”
  2. Use four sequence words: first, then, after that, finally
  3. Include one detail about quality, timing or special meals.
  4. Finish with: “Our goal is…”
Open a B1 model

“I run an airline-catering business with my wife. First, we receive the client’s order and check the menu, quantities and special dietary requirements. Then, our team prepares the meals and carries out quality checks. After that, we pack and label every order carefully. At the moment, a third-party logistics company handles the final delivery. Finally, the airline receives the food for its service. Our goal is to provide reliable, high-quality meals on time.”

🔎

Professional accuracy — correct the sentence

These are realistic mistakes French speakers often make. Choose the best correction.

Check: subject + verb agreement, word order, and whether the verb follows do / does.

06 · LISTENING LAB

Listen for the details that matter.

For both work and VTEST-style listening, do not try to understand every word. Listen for the task, time, quantity, location and action.

Au travail comme dans les exercices d’écoute de type VTEST, n’essayez pas de comprendre chaque mot. Écoutez la tâche, l’heure, la quantité, le lieu et l’action.

🎧

Before you listen:
Read the question first. Decide what detail you need to hear.

🎙️

Call: change to an airline order

Listen once at normal speed. Then listen again more slowly if useful.

Airline contact: Good morning, this is Emma from Nordic Air. I’m calling about tomorrow’s delivery for flight NA214.

Sylvain: Good morning, Emma. How can I help?

Airline contact: We need to change the order. Please add twelve vegetarian meals and reduce the standard meals from eighty to seventy-two.

Sylvain: Certainly. What time do you need the order?

Airline contact: The collection time is now 10:45, not 11:30. Could you also confirm that every box is labelled in English?

Sylvain: Yes, of course. I will update the order and send you a confirmation email before 4 pm today.

Key-detail checklist

  • Who? Nordic Air / Emma
  • Which flight? NA214
  • What changes? vegetarian / standard meals
  • When? 10:45 / before 4 pm
  • What action? update and confirm

Repérez les noms, numéros, quantités, heures et actions demandées.

🎧

Check what you understood

Choose one answer. You will see the correction immediately.

Do not guess from one word. Match the number, time or action to the full question.

La préparation au VTEST n’est pas séparée de votre anglais réel. Une grammaire claire, du vocabulaire utile, l’écoute d’informations précises et des réponses pratiques soutiennent tous vos progrès.

Today’s VTEST skills

1

Read the task first

2

Find the evidence

Listen for numbers, dates, times, people, quantities and actions.

3

Use grammar as a clue

Ask: who is doing the action? Is it routine, past or future?

4

Stay calm

One difficult word does not mean you have lost the meaning.
