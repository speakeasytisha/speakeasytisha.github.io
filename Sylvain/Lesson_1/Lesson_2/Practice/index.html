02 · THE ENGLISH SENTENCE

Sentence DNA: who + verb + information.

In English, the subject is usually visible. Do not begin with only an action or only a place. Start with the person or thing doing the action.

En anglais, le sujet est généralement visible. Ne commencez pas seulement par une action ou un lieu. Commencez par la personne ou la chose qui fait l’action.

Build in blocks

We + prepare + meals for several airlines.

My wife + works + with me.

We + are going to move + to Lisbon in August.

Verb to be — identity, family, place and description

I am · you / we / they are · he / she / it is

On utilise be pour l’identité, l’âge, la famille, le lieu, la profession et la description.

I amI am a caterer.
You areYou are ready.
He / She isMy wife is Portuguese.
We areWe are a family business.
They areMy daughters are at school.
It isIt is a niche market.

Verb to have — family, possessions and responsibilities

I / you / we / they have · he / she / it has

On utilise have pour parler de la famille, des choses que l’on possède, d’un rendez-vous ou d’une responsabilité.

I haveI have two daughters.
You haveYou have a new order.
He / She hasMy daughter has an apprenticeship.
We haveWe have airline clients.
They haveThey have dietary requirements.
It hasThe flight has a delay.

Do / does — questions and negatives with action verbs

Do I / you / we / they work? · Does he / she / it work?

Avec un verbe d’action au présent simple, on utilise do / does pour les questions et do not / does not pour les négations.

Do you work…?Do you work with airlines?
Does she work…?Does your wife work with you?
I do not work…I do not work alone.
He does not work…He does not deliver directly.
Do they live…?Do they live in Lisbon?
Does it arrive…?Does it arrive on time?

Present simple action verbs

I / you / we / they prepare · he / she / it prepares

Au présent simple, on ajoute généralement -s avec he / she / it.

I runI run a catering business.
We prepareWe prepare airline meals.
She worksMy wife works with me.
It handlesThe logistics company handles delivery.
He wantsHe wants to work directly.
The flight leavesThe flight leaves at 6.30.

Quick check — core grammar

Choose an answer. You receive feedback immediately.

First, identify the subject. Is it I / we / they or he / she / it? Then choose the verb form.

03 · PRESENT FORMS: FACTS, ROUTINES & NOW

Present simple or present continuous?

Use the present simple for regular work, habits and facts. Use the present continuous for something happening now or around now.

Utilisez le présent simple pour les habitudes, le travail régulier et les faits. Utilisez le présent continu pour une action en cours maintenant ou autour de maintenant.

PRESENT SIMPLE

Routine, fact, regular work

We prepare meals for several airlines.

I live in Savoie.

My wife works with me.

often · usually · every day · on Mondays
PRESENT CONTINUOUS

Now, temporary situation, current project

We are preparing a large order today.

We are looking for a home in Lisbon.

I am selling my restaurant.

now · at the moment · today · currently
Present simpleSubject + base verb (+ s with he / she / it)
Present continuousSubject + am / are / is + verb-ing

Choose the right present form

Look for the time signal: every week, usually, today, at the moment…

“At the moment / currently / today” often calls for am / are / is + -ing. A regular fact usually uses the present simple.

04 · FUTURE PLANS: LISBON & NEXT STEPS

Use going to for a plan or intention.

You already have a real project: moving to Lisbon, finding a home and developing your business. This is the perfect context for be going to + base verb.

Vous avez déjà un projet réel : déménager à Lisbonne, trouver une maison et développer votre entreprise. C’est le contexte idéal pour be going to + verbe à l’infinitif.

The complete pattern

We are going to move to Lisbon.

I am going to manage the deliveries.

My daughter is going to become a veterinarian.

Affirmative

We are going to move in August.

Negative

We are not going to stay in Savoie.

Question

Are you going to work directly with airlines?

🔗

Give a reason, result or sequence

because = reason · so = result · then = next step · however = contrast

We are moving to Lisbon because my wife is Portuguese. We still need to find a home, so we are waiting for the funds. Then, we are going to organise the next business steps.

Future plans — quick check

Choose the complete and natural sentence.

After going to, always use the base verb: move, find, manage, work — never “to move” or “moving.”

Precise details

Choose the missing word.

Use in for months and cities, on for days, and at for times and precise places.

Memory picture

IN = inside a large space or period
in Lisbon · in August

ON = touching a day/date
on Tuesday · on 30 June

AT = a point
at the airport · at 6.30
