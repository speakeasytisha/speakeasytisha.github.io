02 · WARM-UP: YOUR LIFE NOW

Read, repeat, then personalise.

These are short, useful sentences. They are not “too basic”: they are the building blocks for longer and more natural English.

03 · GRAMMAR RESET: THE ESSENTIALS

English sentence DNA: who + verb + information.

Before verb tenses, start with a clear sentence order. In English, the subject is usually essential: I / you / he / she / we / they.

Avant les temps verbaux, on commence par un ordre clair. En anglais, le sujet est presque toujours indispensable : I / you / he / she / we / they.

Think in blocks

We + live + in Savoie.

My daughter + wants + to become a vet.

We + are going to move + to Lisbon in August.

1

Verb to be — identity, place and description

Use am / are / is to say who you are, where you are, how you feel or what something is.

I amI am 38.
You areYou are welcome.
He / She isMy wife is Portuguese.
We areWe are ready to move.
They areMy daughters are at school.
PositiveWe are in Savoie.NegativeWe are not in Lisbon yet.QuestionAre you ready?
2

Verb to have — family, possessions and responsibilities

Use have / has to talk about your family, your home, your business and what you own.

I haveI have two daughters.
You haveYou have a question.
He / She hasMy daughter has a project.
We haveWe have a catering business.
They haveThey have lessons.
PositiveWe have two daughters.NegativeWe do not have a home in Lisbon yet.QuestionDo you have children?
3

Present simple — routines, facts and regular work

Use the present simple for what is generally true and what you do regularly.

I / We workWe work with airlines.
You workYou work every day.
He / She worksMy wife works with me.
My daughter wantsShe wants to become a vet.
They liveThey live with us.
PositiveWe prepare food.NegativeWe do not work on Sundays.QuestionDo you work with airlines?
4

Going to — plans already decided

Use am / is / are going to + verb for future plans and intentions.

I am going toI am going to learn Portuguese.
You are going toYou are going to travel.
She is going toShe is going to study.
We are going toWe are going to move to Lisbon.
They are going toThey are going to start school.
PositiveWe are going to move in August.NegativeWe are not going to stay in Savoie.QuestionAre you going to find a house soon?
🧩

Quick check — choose the correct sentence

Focus on the subject, the verb form and the time marker.

Look for the clue: every day = present simple; in August = future plan; my wife = she, so add -s in the present simple.

  • How old are your daughters?

A SCHOOL CONTACT

Ask useful questions

  • What school does she go to?
  • Does she speak English?
  • Is the school close to your home?
  • What subjects does she like?
Question patterns

Does + subject + base verb?
Does she speak English?
Does she like science?
Do you live near the school?

✍️

Family sentences — choose the natural option

Focus on have / has, do / does and word order.

My daughter = she → has / wants / lives. After does, use the base verb: Does she want…?

05 · LISBON, TRAVEL & FUTURE PROJECTS

From Savoie to Lisbon: describe the change clearly.

The future is not only grammar. It is your family plan, your move, your new home and the next step for your business.

🧳

Core future pattern:
We are going to + base verb
We are going to move / find / work / travel / organise.

NOW

Life in Savoie

We live in Savoie. We run our catering business. We are selling the restaurant.

BEFORE AUGUST

Prepare the move

We are going to find a home, organise the paperwork and plan our journey.

AUGUST

Move to Lisbon

We are going to move to Lisbon with our family.

NEXT STEP

Grow the business

I am going to work more directly with airline clients and manage deliveries.

Connect your ideas

Connectors make your English sound organised. Start with these four.

Les connecteurs rendent votre anglais plus structuré. Commencez avec ces quatre mots.

andadd information

We are moving to Lisbon and we are looking for a home.

butcontrast

We want to move soon, but we have not found a house yet.

becausegive a reason

We are moving because it is a family project.

soshow a result

We are preparing now, so the move is easier.

🧳

Future plans — choose the best sentence

Use going to for a plan that is already decided.

The form is always: subject + am/is/are going to + base verb. Example: We are going to move.

06 · YOUR PERSONAL STORY BUILDER

Make your English sound like you.

Build a real introduction in layers. Begin with solid A2 sentences. Then add connectors and richer details when you feel ready.

Speaking tip

Do not try to say everything at once. Pause after each idea. Clear English is strong English.

A2

Simple and correct

One idea per sentence. Repeat I / we when necessary.

A2+/B1

Connected

Group ideas and use and, but, because, so.

B1

More natural

Vary the openings: At home, Professionally, Looking ahead, Overall.

07 · LISTENING LAB: A FAMILY MOVE

Listen for key details — not every word.

For VTEST and real life, listen for names, dates, places, family information and future plans.

Listening strategy

First listen: general idea.
Second listen: numbers, dates, places and actions.

🎧

Voice message from a new contact in Lisbon

Listen once at normal speed. Then use slow speed or the transcript only if you need it.

Hello Sylvain. It is great to hear about your family’s move to Lisbon in August. You said that you are coming from Savoie with your wife and your two daughters. Your eldest daughter is seventeen and is on an apprenticeship programme, while your youngest daughter is twelve and is still in middle school. I understand that you are looking for a home near Lisbon, but you have not found one yet. You are also going to develop your catering business and work more directly with airline clients. Please let me know when you know your travel dates.

🎯

Check the details

Answer after listening. You can replay the message as often as you need.

Listen for: August, Savoie, 17, 12, home and airline clients.
