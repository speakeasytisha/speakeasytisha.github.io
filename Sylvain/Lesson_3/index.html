01 · TODAY’S ROUTE

One word. Several possible verb forms.

You asked an excellent grammar question: does today always mean the present continuous? The answer is no. Today tells us when. The situation tells us which tense to use.

Vous avez posé une excellente question de grammaire : est-ce que today signifie toujours le présent continu ? La réponse est non. Today indique quand. La situation indique quel temps utiliser.

02 · THE KEY IDEA

Today is a time expression. It is not a tense signal.

Do not choose a tense because you see the word today. First ask: Is it a routine, a fact, a schedule, a temporary situation or an arrangement?

Ne choisissez pas un temps simplement parce que vous voyez le mot today. Demandez-vous d’abord : Est-ce une habitude, un fait, un horaire, une situation temporaire ou un rendez-vous ?

🧭

Your decision tool

  1. Read the whole idea. What is happening?
  2. Find the meaning. Routine? Temporary situation? Schedule? Arrangement? State?
  3. Choose the form. Present simple or present continuous.
🔁ROUTINE

Every day, we prepare meals.

Regular activity → present simple.

Activité régulière → présent simple.

⏳TEMPORARY

Today, we are preparing a special order.

Special situation around now → present continuous.

Situation particulière temporaire → présent continu.

🗓️SCHEDULE

Today, the flight leaves at 10:20.

Timetable or fixed schedule → present simple.

Horaire officiel ou fixe → présent simple.

🤝ARRANGEMENT

Today, I am meeting the client at 3 p.m.

Personal planned arrangement → present continuous.

Rendez-vous personnel prévu → présent continu.

💡STATE / FACT

Today, I need to confirm the order.

Need, know, want, have = usually present simple.

Need, know, want, have = généralement présent simple.

💬
Talk together

What are you doing today? What do you do every day? Do you have a schedule or a meeting today?

Que faites-vous aujourd’hui ? Que faites-vous tous les jours ? Avez-vous un horaire ou un rendez-vous aujourd’hui ?

03 · PRESENT SIMPLE

Use it for routines, facts, schedules and states.

The present simple is not only for “every day.” It is also the right form for timetables, facts and many verbs that describe a state rather than an action.

Le présent simple ne sert pas seulement pour « tous les jours ». C’est aussi la bonne forme pour les horaires, les faits et de nombreux verbes qui décrivent un état plutôt qu’une action.

Formula

subject + base verb

I / we prepare.
He / she / it prepares.

🔁

Routine

Every day, we prepare meals for several airlines.

Regular business activity.

✈️

Schedule

Today, the aircraft leaves at 10:20.

Fixed timetable or official schedule.

💡

State / fact

Today, I need to confirm the order.

Need = state; use simple, not continuous.

🏠

Permanent situation

We live in Savoie at the moment.

Current home situation; a fact.

⚠️
Some verbs are usually not continuous.

know · need · want · like · love · understand · believe · have (possession)

✅ I need the final quantity today.    ❌ I am needing the final quantity today.

✅ We have two daughters.    ❌ We are having two daughters.

PRACTICE A

Choose the meaning, then the form.

Ask: routine? schedule? state? If yes, choose the present simple.

04 · PRESENT CONTINUOUS

Use it for now, temporary situations and personal arrangements.

The present continuous shows that an action is in progress, temporary or organised as a personal plan.

Le présent continu montre qu’une action est en cours, temporaire ou organisée comme un rendez-vous personnel.

Formula

subject + am / is / are + verb-ing

We are preparing.
I am meeting.

⏳

Temporary today

Today, we are preparing a special crew breakfast.

Not the normal routine; special today.

🔧

Current project

At the moment, I am selling my restaurant.

A project in progress, not necessarily this minute.

🤝

Arrangement

Today, I am meeting a new airline contact.

A personal planned appointment.

🇵🇹

Changing situation

We are looking for a home in Lisbon.

A temporary search project happening now.

PRESENT SIMPLE

Today, I have a meeting at 3 p.m.

The meeting is on my schedule.

↔
PRESENT CONTINUOUS

Today, I am having a meeting with a new client.

I focus on the arranged event / activity.

Both can be possible. The choice changes the focus slightly. In daily English, both are natural when the context is clear.

PRACTICE B

Spot the “temporary / arranged” clues.

Look for: at the moment, this week, today (special event), currently, now, an arranged appointment.

07 · LISTENING LAB

Listen for the meaning, not only one time word.

In a short professional message, listen for the real situation: routine, timetable, current action or planned appointment.

Dans un court message professionnel, écoutez la situation réelle : routine, horaire, action en cours ou rendez-vous prévu.

🎧

Listening strategy:
First identify the action. Then listen for time, quantity, place and next step.

MESSAGE 1 · AIRLINE CATERING

Today’s special order

Listen once for the main idea. Listen again for the details.

Good morning, Sylvain. Today, we are preparing a special breakfast order for flight FJ 482. The flight leaves at 10:20, so the driver collects the meals at 8:45. We usually prepare standard crew breakfasts, but today we are making three different meals because of dietary requirements. Please check the labels and send the final confirmation before 7:30.

MESSAGE 2 · FAMILY & LISBON

Today’s plan

Listen for the present simple and the present continuous.

Today, Sylvain has a call with a bank in Lisbon at 2 p.m. He and his wife are looking for a family home, but they do not have a house yet. They live in Savoie at the moment, and they are going to move to Lisbon in August. Their daughters usually finish school in the afternoon, but today the family is having dinner with Sylvain’s parents.

PRACTICE D

Listening for form and meaning.

Listen for different clues: special order = temporary; leaves at = schedule; usually = routine; has a call = schedule / state.

08 · VTEST CONNECTION

For VTEST, context is your strongest clue.

VTEST-style grammar questions often include a time word, but the best answer depends on the complete sentence and the real-world context — not one word alone.

Dans les questions de grammaire de type VTEST, il y a souvent un marqueur de temps, mais la meilleure réponse dépend de la phrase complète et du contexte réel — pas d’un seul mot.

🔎

Your VTEST strategy:
1. Find the subject. 2. Find the verb. 3. Read the time clue. 4. Decide the meaning. 5. Choose the form.

Subject→Meaning→Time clue→Correct form
VTEST MINI CHECK
