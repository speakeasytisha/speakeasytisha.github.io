01 · QUESTION BLUEPRINT

Questions are built, not guessed.

Before asking a question, identify the verb family. Then apply the right question key.

Avant de poser une question, identifier la famille du verbe. Ensuite, appliquer la bonne clé de question.

Main question rhythm

Question word + auxiliary + subject + verb

Where do you live? / When are you moving?

Family 1 · BE

Are you ready? Is the order ready?

Family 2 · DO / DOES

Do you work? Does the order include fruit?

Family 3 · FUTURE

Are you going to move? Will you send it?

03 · BE questions · Am / Is / Are

BE questions · Am / Is / Are

Use BE questions when the main verb is am, is or are. Move BE before the subject. Do not use do/does.

On pratique d’abord uniquement cette famille de questions avant de mélanger avec les autres.

Pattern

Am / Is / Are + subject + information?

🏡 Personal examples

  • Are you living in Savoie now?
  • Is your wife Portuguese?
  • Are your daughters ready for Lisbon?
  • Is Lisbon a good place for your family?

✈ Professional examples

  • Is the order ready?
  • Are the labels in English?
  • Is the container oven-safe?
  • Are the meals chilled?

04 · HAVE questions · Do / Does + have

HAVE questions · Do / Does + have

Use do/does for questions with have when have means possession, family, equipment or details. After does, use have, not has.

On pratique d’abord uniquement cette famille de questions avant de mélanger avec les autres.

Pattern

Do / Does + subject + have + information?

🏡 Personal examples

  • Do you have a house in Lisbon yet?
  • Does your wife have family in Portugal?
  • Do your daughters have school plans?
  • Does your eldest daughter have an apprenticeship programme?

✈ Professional examples

  • Does the aircraft have a microwave?
  • Does the order have any allergies?
  • Do you have the flight number?
  • Does the client have special requirements?
Controlled question practice

1. Choose the correct question for: “The aircraft has an oven.”

2. Choose the correct question for: “You have two daughters.”

3. Choose the correct question.

05 · Present simple questions · Do / Does + base verb

Present simple questions · Do / Does + base verb

Use present simple questions for routines, facts, regular work and official schedules. Use do/does, then the base verb.

On pratique d’abord uniquement cette famille de questions avant de mélanger avec les autres.

Pattern

Do / Does + subject + base verb + information?

  • What do you usually do at weekends?
  • Where does your daughter go to school?

✈ Professional examples

  • Does the order include fruit?
  • What time does the flight leave?
  • Which company handles the delivery?

07 · GOING TO questions · plans and intentions

GOING TO questions · plans and intentions

Use going to questions to ask about plans and intentions. Move am/is/are before the subject. Keep the base verb after going to.

On pratique d’abord uniquement cette famille de questions avant de mélanger avec les autres.

Am / Is / Are + subject + going to + base verb?

08 · WILL questions · promises, decisions, predictions

WILL questions · promises, decisions, predictions

Use will questions to ask about promises, decisions, predictions and offers. Will is the same for every subject.

On pratique d’abord uniquement cette famille de questions avant de mélanger avec les autres.

Pattern

Will + subject + base verb?

🏡 Personal examples

  • Will life be different in Lisbon?
  • Will your family settle in quickly?
  • Will you visit Savoie often?
  • What will change for your daughters?

✈ Professional examples

  • Will you send the confirmation today?
  • Will the delivery be on time?
  • Will the order be ready at 07:45?
  • Who will call the client?

09 · WH QUESTIONS

Ask for the missing information.

Use WH words to ask for a place, time, reason, person, choice, quantity or method.

Les mots interrogatifs permettent de demander un lieu, un moment, une raison, une personne, un choix, une quantité ou une manière.

Pattern

WH word + auxiliary + subject + verb?

WordUseExample
Whatthing / informationWhat do you prepare?
WhereplaceWhere do you live?
WhentimeWhen are you moving?
WhyreasonWhy are you moving?
HowmannerHow do you organise deliveries?
How manycountable quantityHow many meals do you need?
How muchuncountable quantity / priceHow much food do you need?
WhichchoiceWhich flight is this order for?
WhopersonWho should we contact?

11 · SPEAKING ROUNDS

Ask, answer, improve.

Use the models when needed, but try first without looking. Each round can last 3–5 minutes.

Utiliser les modèles si besoin, mais essayer d’abord sans regarder. Chaque round peut durer 3 à 5 minutes.

Speaking rule

One short question. One clear answer. One follow-up question.

Round 1 · Personal

Ask about Lisbon, family, home and plans.

Model:

When are you moving to Lisbon? Why are you moving there? What kind of home are you looking for?

Round 2 · Professional

Ask about orders, flight, delivery and labels.

Model:

Which flight is this order for? How many meals do you need? Are there any dietary restrictions?

Round 3 · Future

Ask about plans, arrangements and promises.

Model:

Are you going to work directly with airlines? Are you meeting a client this week? Will you send the confirmation today?
