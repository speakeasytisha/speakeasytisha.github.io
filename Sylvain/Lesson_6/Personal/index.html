02 · THE PAST SIMPLE — AFFIRMATIVE SENTENCES

Use the past simple for a finished time.

Use it when the action started and finished before now: yesterday, last week, in 2024, two years ago, or when you were younger.

Regular verbs

Most regular verbs add -ed.

work → worked

I worked with my wife last week.

Irregular verbs

Some verbs change completely. Learn them as a pair.

learn → learned / go → went

I learned English after middle school.

Verb “be”

Use was / were, not did.

I / he / she / it was · you / we / they were

We were in France at the time.

Personal past: a useful mini-story

Read the model slowly. Notice the time marker, the past verbs and the sequence words.

START

“I learned English after middle school.”

CHANGE

“Then, I did not use English for about twenty years.”

NOW

“Later, I started using English again because I worked with airline clients.”

Important: The word then tells us the order of events. It does not create the past tense by itself. The finished time and the meaning tell you that the past simple is needed.

Stage 1 — One pronoun only: I

Choose the correct affirmative form. Keep your eyes on the verb.

1. I ___ English after middle school.

2. I ___ to Portugal for a family visit.

3. I ___ with my wife yesterday.

4. I ___ very busy last week.

For a finished past action, do not use am / is / are. Choose one past form: learned, went, worked, was.

03 · FAMILY MEMORIES & YOUR LISBON JOURNEY

Make a story easy to follow with time markers and connectors.

Time markers answer when? Connectors show the order or reason: first, then, after that, because, so and finally.

A clear story pattern

When? Last year…
What happened? we discussed the move.
Why? because Portugal is important to our family.

1 · TIMEAt first

At first, we talked about the idea.

2 · ACTIONThen

Then, we looked at different possibilities.

3 · REASONBecause

We wanted a new family project.

4 · RESULTFinally

We decided to prepare the move.

Put the family story in order.

Choose the best connector. The grammar stays in the past simple.

1. ___, we talked about our future in Portugal.

2. ___, we looked at houses and schools.

3. We considered Lisbon ___ my wife has family in Portugal.

4. ___, we started to organise the move.

04 · NEGATIVE SENTENCES

For action verbs, use did not + base verb.

After did not / didn’t, the main verb returns to its original form. Do not put the verb in the past twice.

One rule — two parts

AffirmativeI visited Portugal.
NegativeI didn’t visit Portugal.
AffirmativeWe made a decision.
NegativeWe didn’t make a decision.
Never say: I didn’t visited.
Say: I didn’t visit. The past is already carried by didn’t.

Stage 2 — One pronoun only: I

Choose the correct negative form.

1. I ___ English for about twenty years.

2. I ___ abroad last month.

3. I ___ ready to move at that time.

4. I ___ the right information immediately.

05 · QUESTIONS ABOUT THE PAST

Start with Did. Then use the base verb.

Questions change the word order. The helper did goes first; the main verb stays in its base form.

Question formula

Action verbDid you learn English at school?
Action verbDid they visit Portugal?
Verb beWere you ready?
Verb beWas she at home?
Different rule for be: do not use did with was/were. Ask: Were you in Savoie? — not Did you be in Savoie?

Stage 3 — One pronoun only: you

Choose the correct question.

1. ___ English after middle school?

2. ___ Portugal last year?

3. ___ interested in English when you were younger?

4. ___ to move to Lisbon together?

06 · PAST TIME WORDS & PREPOSITIONS

Show exactly when and where something happened.

Time words help your listener understand the past. They tell us when; the past form tells us what happened.

At

Use at for a precise time.

at 7:45 · at midnight

We left home at 7:45.

On

Use on for days and dates.

on Monday · on 25 June

We travelled on Saturday.

In

Use in for months, years and places.

in August · in 2024 · in Savoie

I lived in Savoie in 2024.

No preposition: say yesterday, last week, two years ago and this morning — not in last week.

Time check

1. I learned English ___ 2007.

2. We visited the family ___ Sunday.

3. The meeting started ___ 10:30.

4. We talked about Lisbon ___ last week.
