01 · THE GRAMMAR MAP

Find the time. Choose the pattern. Add the right detail.

The past simple answers one main question: What happened before now? It is used for finished actions, completed events and past states.

Le prétérit répond à une question principale : Qu’est-ce qui s’est passé avant maintenant ? Il est utilisé pour les actions terminées, les événements achevés et les états passés.

🎯

By the end of the review, you can:
form affirmatives, negatives and questions; choose subject and object pronouns; use time and place details; and recognise common past forms in a short VTEST-style task.

1. Completed action

Use a past verb.

We prepared the meals.

The action finished before now.

2. Past state

Use was / were with be.

The trays were ready.

A description or condition in the past.

3. Exact detail

Add time or place.

at 07:45 · on Monday · in June

Details make your story clear.

Past clues: yesterday · last week · last year · ago · in 2024 · when I was younger · on Monday at 07:45.
Remember: the time clue helps you locate the event; the verb form shows that it is past.

02 · STAGE 1 — AFFIRMATIVE SENTENCES

For most actions: subject + past verb + detail.

Start with who did the action. Then use one past form. Finally, add the object, place, time or reason.

Two types of past verbs

TypeRuleBase verbPast simpleExample
RegularAdd -ed or -d.work / liveworked / livedWe worked with the client.
IrregularLearn the past form individually.go / make / send / findwent / made / sent / foundThe company sent the details.
BeUse was / were.bewas / wereThe order was ready.
Do not add did to an affirmative sentence. Say We prepared the meals. — not We did prepared the meals.

Stage 1A — Keep the same subject: I

Choose the correct affirmative past form.

1. I ___ using English again for work.

2. I ___ the airline representative at the airport.

3. I ___ very busy last week.

4. I ___ an important document yesterday.

Stage 1B · Change the subject

Now read the subject first.

Choose the correct form for each subject.

1. My wife and I ___ in Savoie at the time.

2. The airline ___ us about a new order.

3. My daughters ___ cards for the family.

4. The delivery ___ on time.

03 · STAGE 2 — NEGATIVE SENTENCES

For action verbs: did not + base verb.

When you add didn’t, do not keep the past ending on the main verb. Didn’t already shows the past.

Negative patterns

Action verbI didn’t use English.
Action verbWe didn’t receive the email.
Be — singularThe order wasn’t ready.
Be — pluralThe trays weren’t ready.
Two common mistakes:
We didn’t prepared the meals. → We didn’t prepare the meals.
The trays didn’t be ready. → The trays weren’t ready.

Stage 2A — Keep the same subject: We

Choose the correct negative.

1. We ___ the final order yesterday.

2. We ___ to Lisbon last month.

3. We ___ ready for the original delivery time.

4. We ___ the correct address immediately.

Stage 2B · Change the subject

Now switch the pronoun.

1. The client ___ us before 6 p.m.

2. My wife ___ at the office yesterday afternoon.

3. My daughters ___ the meal yesterday.

4. The delivery ___ late.

04 · STAGE 3 — QUESTIONS

For action verbs: Did + subject + base verb?

In a past question, the helper did comes first. The main verb stays in the base form.

Question patterns

Action verbDid you send the email?
Action verbDid they visit Lisbon?
Be — singularWas the flight on time?
Be — pluralWere the meals ready?
Remember: do not use did with was/were. Ask Was the order ready?, not Did the order be ready?

Stage 3A — Keep the same pronoun: you

Choose the correct question.

1. ___ with the client yesterday?

2. ___ the final details?

3. ___ ready to leave at 07:30?

4. ___ a decision about Lisbon?

Stage 3B · Change the subject

Read the subject before choosing.

1. ___ you before the delivery?

2. ___ complete at 07:45?

3. ___ the trip?

4. ___ chilled before delivery?

05 · SUBJECT PRONOUNS, OBJECT PRONOUNS & POSSESSIVES

Know who did it, who received it and who owns it.

These small words make sentences more natural and avoid repeating the same noun again and again.

Subject
before the verb		Object
after a verb / preposition		Possessive adjective
before a noun		Example
ImemyThe client contacted me. My order was ready.
youyouyourI called you. Your delivery arrived.
hehimhisWe met him. His flight left at 8.
sheherherI spoke to her. Her order changed.
itititsThe driver delivered it. Its label was clear.
weusourThe airline emailed us. Our team replied.
theythemtheirWe prepared meals for them. Their flight was delayed.
Simple rule: subject = the doer (They contacted us.) · object = the receiver (We replied to them.) · possessive = ownership before a noun (Their order changed.).

Choose the right small word.

1. The client contacted ___ after the flight changed.

2. We checked ___ special meal request.

3. I spoke to my wife, and I explained the plan to ___.

4. The airline sent an email. We read ___ immediately.

06 · REGULAR -ED: SPELLING & PRONUNCIATION

Regular verbs have rules too.

Understanding the patterns helps you write and recognise past forms more confidently.

RuleBase verbPast formExample
Most verbs: add -ed.workworkedWe worked with several airlines.
Verb ends in -e: add only -d.live / preparelived / preparedWe prepared the meals.
Consonant + y: change y to i + ed.carrycarriedThe driver carried the trays.
Short vowel + consonant: often double the consonant.planplannedWe planned the delivery.
British English: often double l.label / travellabelled / travelledThe team labelled each item.

/t/ sound

After unvoiced sounds: worked, packed, washed.

/d/ sound

After voiced sounds: planned, delivered, labelled.

/ɪd/ sound

After t or d: needed, wanted, started.

Speaking note: Do not automatically add an extra syllable to every -ed verb. We say worked (one syllable), but needed (two syllables).

07 · TIME, PLACE & THE SMALL WORDS

Make past events precise with at, on, in, to, from and with.

These words do not change the tense. They add the exact information that a listener needs.

At

Exact time or a point.

at 07:45 · at the airport

The driver arrived at 07:45.

On

Days and dates.

on Monday · on 25 June

The order arrived on Monday.

In

Months, years, cities, countries.

in August · in 2026 · in Lisbon

We lived in Savoie in 2026.

To

Destination.

to the aircraft · to Lisbon

We delivered the meals to the aircraft.

From

Origin.

from the client · from France

We received an update from the client.

With

Company / relationship / tool.

with my wife · with the team

I worked with my wife.

No preposition: yesterday · last week · last year · next week · this morning · every day.
Say: We met last week. Not: We met in last week.

Small words: mixed review

1. Sylvain began using English again ___ 2026.

2. The order arrived ___ 07:45.

3. The driver delivered the food ___ the aircraft.

4. The client contacted us ___ last week.

08 · VTEST-STYLE CHECKPOINT

Read for the past verbs, names, times and specific details.

On VTEST-style tasks, do not try to translate every word. First find: who, what, when, where and what changed.

Mini professional report

Read it silently first. Then listen. Finally, answer the questions.

Yesterday, the airline sent an updated breakfast order. The client requested three different meals for the crew because of allergy and dietary requirements. Our team prepared the meals, labelled each tray in English and checked the packaging. The driver collected the order at 07:15 and delivered it to the aircraft at 07:45.

Strategy: underline the past verbs: sent, requested, prepared, labelled, checked, collected, delivered. Circle the exact times: 07:15 and 07:45.

Answer the details.

1. What did the client request?

2. Why did the client request different meals?

3. When did the driver collect the order?

4. Where did the driver deliver the order?
