02 · HOW THE BUSINESS BEGAN

Use the past simple to explain a finished professional story.

The key is to choose one past verb for each finished action: contacted, needed, prepared, delivered, started and became.

CLEAR A2

“A company needed a catering service in France. They contacted me, and I prepared the food.”

A2+ / B1

“The opportunity began when a friend could not deliver for an airline. The company contacted me because they needed a catering service in France, so I prepared the order and organised the delivery.”

Professional past verbs to keep

These verbs are useful because they tell the story of an order from request to delivery.

need → neededThe client needed a service.
contact → contactedThe client contacted us.
prepare → preparedWe prepared the meals.
deliver → deliveredThe team delivered the order.
send → sentThe client sent the details.
make → madeWe made three different meals.
meet → metWe met the handling team.
become → becameThe client became regular.
Useful difference: needed, contacted, prepared, delivered are regular. sent, made, met, became are irregular, so the past form must be learned.

03 · SUBJECTS & OBJECTS — ME, US, THEM, IT

Put the person doing the action before the verb.

Put the person or thing receiving the action after the verb or after a preposition such as to, for or with.

Placez la personne qui fait l’action avant le verbe. Placez la personne ou la chose qui reçoit l’action après le verbe ou après une préposition comme to, for ou with.

Compare

They contacted us.
We prepared the meals for them.

04 · FROM A CLIENT ORDER TO A COMPLETED DELIVERY

Read real-work details and report what happened.

Orders often include exact times, special meal requirements, packaging instructions and aircraft limitations. In the past, you can report each completed step clearly.

Food presentation

Use precise words: prepared, labelled, packed, chilled, delivered.

Quality language

Professional reporting is clear and factual: what was ordered, what was prepared and how it was packed.

📦 Packaging actions

labelled
added clear English labels
packed
put food in containers or bags
separated
kept sauces or delicate items apart
blast chilled
cooled hot food quickly for safety

🥗 Order details

crew meal
a meal for crew members
dietary requirement
a food or medical restriction
allergy note
information about an allergen
half-atlas tray
a smaller tray used on board

✈️ Delivery details

local time
the time at the delivery location
aircraft
the plane
handling team
airport team that manages the aircraft on the ground
oven-proof
safe to use in an oven

Example: reporting a completed order

Read the report aloud. The key is to make each action clear.

On 25 June, the client requested three different cold breakfast meals for the crew. The team prepared the meals, labelled the allergy and ketogenic requirements, and packed the order in half-atlas trays. The delivery arrived at the aircraft at 07:45 local time.

Le 25 juin, le client a demandé trois petits-déjeuners froids différents pour l’équipage. L’équipe a préparé les repas, a étiqueté les contraintes liées à l’allergie et au régime cétogène, et a emballé la commande dans des demi-plateaux Atlas. La livraison est arrivée à l’avion à 7h45, heure locale.

Which action happened?

1. The team ___ the special dietary requirements.

2. The order ___ at the aircraft at 07:45.

3. The caterer ___ oven-proof equipment for the order.

4. We ___ three different meals for security reasons.

05 · REPORTING A PROBLEM: NEGATIVES

Say what did not happen with didn’t + base verb.

This is useful when you explain a delay, a missing detail or an issue that the team corrected.

Action verbs versus be

Action verbThe client didn’t confirm the time.
Action verbWe didn’t receive the final list.
BeThe trays weren’t ready.
BeThe driver wasn’t at the airport.
Important: The client didn’t confirmed. → incorrect.
The client didn’t confirm. → correct.

07 · TIME, PLACE & DELIVERY DETAILS

Use small words to make the report precise.

Prepositions tell the client exactly when, where and to whom something happened.

At = exact time

at 07:45 · at the airport

The delivery arrived at 07:45.

On = day / date

on Monday · on 25 June

We received the order on Monday.

In = month / place

in June · in France · in the kitchen

The company started in France.

To = destination

deliver to the aircraft

The driver delivered it to the aircraft.

From = origin

an order from the client

We received an order from the airline.

With = company / tool

with the handling team

We worked with the handling team.
