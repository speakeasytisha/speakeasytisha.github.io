1) What to practise now

Two useful writing habits

This continuation focuses on two types of writing that help you progress: a short opinion essay and a short polite message.

🧠

Short opinion essay

Answer the question clearly, give your opinion, support it with reasons, and finish with a short conclusion.

Example: In my opinion, flexible working hours can improve work-life balance and reduce stress.

✉️

Short practical message

Write a subject line, explain the situation, ask your question politely, and end with a warm closing.

Example: Could you please confirm which documents are required?

2) Essay map

A simple essay structure you can trust

When you do not know how to start, use this 5-step plan.

1

Answer the question

Show that you understand the topic.

Example: Many companies are thinking about remote work today.

2

Give your opinion

Be direct and simple.

Example: I believe it can be a positive solution when it is well organized.

3

Reason 1

Explain your first main idea.

Example: First, it can save commuting time and reduce stress.

4

Reason 2 + example

Add another idea and one concrete example.

Example: It can also help parents organize family life more easily.

5

Mini conclusion

Finish with one clear sentence.

Example: Overall, I think remote work is useful if teams communicate clearly.

Mini prompt 1

          
You want information about a training course: dates, price, and online access.

          

            
Useful start: I am writing to ask for some information about your training course.

            
Useful question: Could you please tell me the dates, the price, and whether online access is included?

          

        


        

          
Mini prompt 2

          
You need to confirm an appointment and ask which documents to bring.

          

            
Useful start: I am writing to confirm my appointment next Tuesday.

            
Useful question: Could you also please confirm which documents I should bring with me?
