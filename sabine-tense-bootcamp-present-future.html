1) Present Simple
Routine / job / facts — Sabine’s daily work.
When & how
Use
✅ routines / jobs / facts
✅ repeated actions (every day, usually)
✅ habitudes / travail / faits
✅ actions répétées (every day, usually)
Form
I/you/we/they + base verb: I call
he/she/it + -s: She calls
Attention: en anglais, le -s est seulement à la 3e personne.
Key words
French vs English
EN: Present Simple = routine / facts.
FR: Souvent… on utilise aussi le présent.

2) Present Continuous
Now / around now — what Sabine is doing today.
When & how
Use
✅ action happening now / around now
✅ temporary actions (this week)
✅ action en cours (maintenant / en ce moment)
✅ action temporaire (cette semaine)
Form
am/is/are + verb-ing: I am calling
Spelling: make → making (drop “e”), plan → planning (double consonant)
FR: souvent = “être en train de…” (mais pas toujours obligatoire).
Key words
French vs English
EN: “I am calling” is very normal for “now”.
FR: “Je suis en train d’appeler” vs “J’appelle”.
Ex: “J’appelle maintenant” → “I’m calling now.”

3) Future: going to / will
Plans vs promises — simple and practical.
When & how
Going to = plan
am/is/are going to + base verb
I am going to send the schedule tomorrow.
FR: “Je vais…” (intention / plan)
Will = promise / decision
will + base verb
I will call you this afternoon. (promise)
FR: “Je vais le faire” / “Je le ferai” (promesse)
Key words
French vs English (common confusion)
FR often uses “je vais…” for many futures.
EN uses “going to” for plans, and “will” for promises/decisions.
Objectif : écouter la logique (plan vs promesse).
