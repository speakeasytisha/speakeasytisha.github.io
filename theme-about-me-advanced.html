About me! – Advanced

This page is for more confident learners who can go beyond basic information. You describe your role, responsibilities, experience, reasons you like your job and even future plans, using connectors like because, so, however, in addition.

Going further: richer self-introductions

At this level, “About me” is more than “My name is [your name], I live in [town, city].” You can:

  • Describe your role and responsibilities
  • Mention your experience (how long, where, with whom)
  • Add reasons and opinions about your job or studies
  • Talk about future projects or goals

Useful structures:

  • Present simple – daily routine and general facts I coordinate…, I organize…, I work with…
  • Present continuous – current projects I am working on a new train project this year.
  • Present perfect – experience I have been working in tourism for six years.
  • Past simple – previous experience Before that, I worked in a hotel.
  • Future – plans In the future, I would like to…

Connectors – making the paragraph fluent

Connectors help you sound more natural and organized:

  • because – to give a reason
  • so – to show a result
  • however – to contrast
  • in addition / also – to add information

1. Choose the best connector:

I love my job, ______ I sometimes feel tired after long days.

2. Choose the best connector:

I have a lot of responsibilities, ______ I need to stay organised.

3. Choose the best connector:

I coordinate the project on the train, ______ I visit different sites to prepare the passenger experience.

Example – Advanced “About me”

Hello, my name is Tisha and I work as an independent English and French trainer in France. I have been living here for more than twenty years, and I have been teaching languages to adults and young adults in different professional sectors. I currently design personalised training programmes and digital resources to help learners feel more confident in real-life situations.

I really enjoy my job because every learner is different and I can be creative with my lessons. I also love combining my American background with my French experience to explain cultural differences and everyday language. In the future, I would like to continue developing my online courses, collaborate with more training centres, and reach learners in different countries.
