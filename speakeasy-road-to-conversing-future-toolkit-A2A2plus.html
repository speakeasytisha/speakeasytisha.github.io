2) Future tenses — complete toolkit 🧠

Each tool has: Rule • How to use • Keywords • Examples • Mini conjugation • Micro check.

A) 🎯 Plan be going to

Rule am / is / are + going to + base verb
Use plans / intentions (already decided)
Keywords plan, intention, next week, next month, soon, this summer
Examples I’m going to visit my son next month. • I’m not going to stay long.
Mini conjugation
I am going to / am not going to
you are going to / aren’t going to
he/she/it is going to / isn’t going to
we are going to / aren’t going to
they are going to / aren’t going to
Question Are you going to…? Is he going to…?

B) ⚡ Now will

Rule will + base verb (no “to”)
Use decision now • offer • promise • prediction
Keywords OK! / Don’t worry / I think / probably
Examples OK, I’ll book the tickets tonight. • It will be cold in Denmark.
Mini conjugation
I will / won’t
you will / won’t
he/she/it will / won’t
we will / won’t
they will / won’t
Question Will you…? Will she…?

C) 📅 Arranged present continuous

Rule am / is / are + verb‑ing
Use scheduled/arranged future (date/time)
Keywords on Monday, at 3 pm, tomorrow, next week
Examples I’m meeting them on Saturday. • I’m volunteering at the vet clinic on Tuesday.
Mini conjugation
I am meeting / am not meeting
you are meeting / aren’t meeting
he/she/it is meeting / isn’t meeting
we are meeting / aren’t meeting
they are meeting / aren’t meeting
Question What time are you leaving?

D) ⏳ Rule time clauses

Rule After when / after / before / until → use present (NOT will)
Use connect two actions in the future
Examples When I arrive, I’ll call you. • I’ll stay until the meeting ends.
Mini patterns
When I arrive, I’ll call.
After I finish, I’ll send it.
Before I go, I read the file.
Until it ends, I stay.
As soon as I get home, I’ll write.
Once we agree, we’ll decide.

E) 🌤️ Maybe may / might (B1 stretch)

Rule may / might + base verb (might not + verb)
Use possibility (not 100%)
Examples I might have a meeting at court next week. • I may write the report tonight.
Mini patterns
I might go. / I might not go.
She may call later.
It might be difficult.
We may meet at 3 pm.
