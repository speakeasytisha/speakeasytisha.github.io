French learner note 🇫🇷→🇬🇧
In English, prepositions are often fixed with certain verbs/adjectives: depend on, interested in, good at, listen to, wait for. You can’t always translate “à / de / en / dans / chez” word-for-word.

Quick cheat sheet 📌

Fast rules
🗺️ Place
  • at + point: at the station, at the door
  • in + area: in London, in a room, in a box
  • on + surface/line: on the table, on the wall, on the left
🕒 Time
  • at 6:30, at noon, at the weekend (UK)
  • on Monday, on 12 July, on Monday morning
  • in 2026, in April, in the morning (general)
🚶 Movement
  • to (destination): go to London
  • into (enter): walk into the room
  • onto (move to a surface): step onto the bus
✅ Collocations
  • arrive in a city / arrive at a place
  • interested in · good at · afraid of
  • depend on · wait for · listen to

Prepositions of place 🗺️

in · on · at · next to · between…

Think of scale: at = point 📍, in = inside/area 🧊, on = surface/line 📄.

📍
atat the entrance · at the bus stop · at reception
🧊
inin London · in the room · in a taxi
📄
onon the table · on the wall · on the third floor
↔️
betweenbetween the bank and the museum
➡️
next tonext to the lift / elevator
🪟
in front of / behindin front of the station · behind the shop
Common tricky ones
  • on the bus / on the train / on a plane (public transport)
  • in a car / in a taxi (private vehicle)
  • at home / at work / at school (fixed expressions)
French trap ⚠️
  • ✅ in London (not “at London”)
  • ✅ on the bus (not “in the bus”, usually)
  • ✅ at the door (point) vs ✅ in the doorway (inside the opening)

Prepositions of time 🕒

at · on · in · by · until · for · since

at / on / in

at 7:15 · at noon · at midnight · at the weekend (UK)
on Monday · on 12 July · on Monday morning
in April · in 2026 · in the morning (general)

Tip: on = calendar “surface” (days/dates). in = inside a period (month/year).

by / until

I’ll send the report by 5 p.m. (deadline ✅)
I’ll work until 5 p.m. (continuation → end ✅)

for / since / during

I’ve lived here for 10 years (duration)
I’ve lived here since 2015 (start point)
We stayed quiet during the presentation (inside an event)

Difference vs French 🇫🇷
“Depuis” → usually for (duration) or since (start point). “À” can become at, to, in, or on depending on context.

Prepositions of movement 🚶

to · into · onto · through · across · along
🎯
todestination: go to the station
🚪
intoenter: walk into the room
🧱
ontomove to a surface: step onto the platform
🕳️
throughinside then out: go through the tunnel
🌉
acrossfrom one side to the other: walk across the bridge
🏞️
alongfollow a line: walk along the river
Mini examples
  • We drove to the hotel, then went into the lobby.
  • She stepped onto the train and sat by the window.
  • They walked across the square and along the main street.
French trap ⚠️
  • ✅ arrive in Paris / arrive at the hotel
  • ✅ go to London (movement) vs be in London (location)

Common collocations ✅

verbs + adjectives + prepositions

These are the ones advanced learners get tested on. You’ll see them in business emails, meetings, and everyday speech.

🎧 Verbs
  • listen to
  • wait for
  • depend on
  • belong to
  • apologise for
  • apply for
🎯 Adjectives
  • good at
  • interested in
  • responsible for
  • famous for
  • afraid of
  • similar to
🧾 Nouns / phrases
  • in charge of
  • at risk of
  • on time / in time
  • by email / on the phone
  • in the end / at the end
