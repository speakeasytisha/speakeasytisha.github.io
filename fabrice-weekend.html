1. Past simple – weekend stories

We use the past simple to talk about finished actions in the past: yesterday, last weekend, on Saturday, in 2020…

  • Regular verbs: work → worked, watch → watched, visit → visited.
  • Irregular verbs: go → went, have → had, see → saw.
  • Negative: didn’t + base form → I didn’t work on Sunday.
  • Questions: Did + subject + base form → Did you go out?

1. Past simple – regular / irregular

Yesterday I ___ to the supermarket near my house.

2. Past simple – regular verb

3. Past simple – negative

4. Past simple – question

5. Past simple – irregular verb

They ___ dinner at a restaurant on Friday night.

2. Past continuous – background & interruptions

We use the past continuous (was / were + -ing) for actions in progress in the past, often as a background for another action in the past simple.

  • Form: I was working, you were watching TV, they were eating.
  • Use: long actions, background, stories: It was raining and people were waiting for the train.
  • Past simple + past continuous: short action interrupts a longer one: I was cooking when the phone rang.

4. Connectors – making your ideas flow

Connectors help you add information, contrast ideas, and explain reasons: and, but, however, because, so, although, therefore, while, or…

22. Contrast connector

23. Giving a reason

24. Adding information

25. However

28. Therefore / so

29. Choosing between options

30. Adding a last detail
