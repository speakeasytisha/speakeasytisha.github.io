CLOE Past Simple Foundation

A clear step-by-step page to understand how to form the past simple before moving into exam practice. Start with one verb, then one pronoun, then build affirmative, negative, and questions.

🎯 What is the goal?

In the CLOE exam, learners often need to say what they did, where they went, who they met, what they prepared, and what happened during a project, a meeting, an event, a trip, or a problem.

🕒 When do we use the past simple?

Use the past simple for finished actions in the past. On utilise le past simple pour parler d’actions terminées dans le passé.

  • Yesterday
  • Last week / last month / last year
  • This morning (if the morning is finished)
  • On Monday / in Paris / during the salon

🧱 Start with one verb and one pronoun

We start with the same verb and the same pronoun to make the change easy to see.

Present

I organise the event.

J’organise l’événement.

➡️
Past simple

I organised the event.

J’ai organisé l’événement.

Step 1

Choose the subject: I

Step 2

Choose the base verb: organise

Step 3

For a regular verb, add -d / -ed: organised

Result

I organised the event.

✅ Affirmative

Subject + past verb + complement

I organised the salon.

She contacted the venue.

They prepared the schedule.

Structure : sujet + verbe au passé + complément

❌ Negative

Subject + did not (didn’t) + base verb + complement

I did not organise the transport.

She didn’t contact the florist.

We didn’t change the menu.

Important: after did / didn’t, the verb goes back to the base form.

Après did / didn’t, on remet le verbe à la base.

❓ Questions

Did + subject + base verb + complement?

Did you organise the seating plan?

Did she call the client?

Did they prepare the documents?

Important: after Did, the verb goes back to the base form.

🔍 The changes step by step

Affirmative

  1. Choose the subject: I
  2. Choose the verb: organise
  3. Add the past ending: organised
  4. Add the rest: I organised the event.

Negative

  1. Choose the subject: I
  2. Add did not / didn’t
  3. Use the base verb: organise
  4. Add the rest: I didn’t organise the event.

Question

  1. Start with Did
  2. Add the subject: you
  3. Use the base verb: organise
  4. Add the rest: Did you organise the event?

✍️ Practice 1 · Only I + the same verb

Stay in the past simple. Look at the tags before you write.

Affirmative: subject + past verb → I organised

Negative: subject + didn’t + base verb → I didn’t organise

Question: Did + subject + base verb → Did I organise?

🔄 Practice 2 · Same verb, more pronouns

The verb stays the same in the affirmative. In questions and negatives, use did / didn’t + base verb.

Affirmative: She organised. / He organised.

Negative: They didn’t organise.

Question: Did we organise?

📚 Practice 3 · Regular or irregular?

Write the past simple form of each verb.

Regular verbs: prepare → prepared / carry → carried

Irregular verbs: go → went / meet → met

🗣️ Practice 4 · Connector builder

Put the story in a logical order:

🧾 Useful debrief language for the CLOE exam

First, I prepared the documents and confirmed the schedule.

Then, I contacted the suppliers and welcomed the guests.

After that, I checked the different areas and solved a small problem.

Finally, I spoke with the team and wrote a follow-up email.

☎️ Short phone model

Client: Hello. How was the salon in Paris?

Planner: It went well, thank you. We welcomed many visitors and met new suppliers.

Client: Did you have any problems?

Planner: Yes, we had a small delay at the beginning, but we solved it quickly.

Client: Great. What did you do after the event?

Planner: I wrote a follow-up email and prepared the next steps.

🎤 Simple CLOE-style speaking prompt

Describe a professional event that you organised or attended. Say what you did before the event, during the event, and after the event.

Safe speaking frame

Last week, I organised / attended…

First, I…

Then, I…

After that, I…

Finally, I…

Model answer

Last week, I organised a professional salon in Paris. First, I prepared the documents and confirmed the schedule. Then, I welcomed the guests and contacted the suppliers. After that, I solved a small problem with the seating area. Finally, I wrote a follow-up email and prepared the next steps.
