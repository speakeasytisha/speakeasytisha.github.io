1 • Where are they?

Location, state, direction and what each place is near

Nantucket

Massachusetts
  • Region: in the north-east of the United States.
  • State: Massachusetts.
  • Exact location: an island off the coast of Massachusetts, south of Cape Cod, in the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Near: Cape Cod, Martha’s Vineyard and the New England coast.
  • Best for: historic charm, a relaxed island atmosphere, maritime history and elegant village streets.
Model: Nantucket is in the north-east of the US, off the coast of Massachusetts, south of Cape Cod.

Key West

Florida
  • Region: in the south-east of the United States.
  • State: Florida.
  • Exact location: an island city at the south-western end of the Florida Keys.
  • Near: the Gulf of Mexico, the Straits of Florida and much closer to Cuba than to most US cities.
  • Best for: tropical colour, literary history, sunsets, island culture and lively streets.
Model: Key West is in the south-east of the US, in Florida, at the south-western end of the Florida Keys.

    3 • Historic and cultural angle

    What to see there and why these places are interesting

    Nantucket • maritime history and New England charm

    • Whaling Museum: one of the best places to understand Nantucket’s maritime past.
    • Brant Point Lighthouse: a classic symbol of the island.
    • Main Street and the town centre: cobblestones, small shops and traditional houses.
    • Story & screen connection: the whaleship Essex, a real Nantucket ship, inspired the story told in In the Heart of the Sea. The tragedy also helped inspire Moby-Dick.

    Nantucket is more historic, quieter and more traditional than Key West.

    Key West • island culture, literature and tropical history

    • Ernest Hemingway Home: a famous historic house connected with the writer’s life in Key West.
    • Old Town / Duval Street area: colourful houses, cafés, galleries and a relaxed island atmosphere.
    • Mallory Square: famous for sunset views and street life.
    • Shipwreck history: Key West became famous for its “wrecking” history, which is why the Shipwreck Treasure Museum is an important place to visit.
    • Story & screen connection: Hemingway’s To Have and Have Not is set in Key West and was adapted into a famous film.

    Key West is more tropical, more colourful and more lively at night than Nantucket.

    Best for history? Nantucket for whaling history; Key West for literary and shipwreck history.
    Best for charm? Both are charming, but in different ways.
    Best for nightlife? Key West.
    Best for calm village streets? Nantucket.

    4 • Detailed grammar

    Comparatives, superlatives and giving reasons

    Comparatives

    Form: adjective + -er / more + adjective + than

    • Nantucket is smaller than Key West.
    • Key West is more colourful than Nantucket.
    • Nantucket is quieter than Key West.
    • Key West is more lively at night than Nantucket.

    Superlatives

    Form: the + adjective + -est / the most + adjective

    • Brant Point is one of the most iconic places in Nantucket.
    • Mallory Square is one of the most famous places in Key West.
    • Key West is one of the southernmost destinations in the continental US.

    Location language

    • in the north-east / in the south-east of the US
    • in the state of…
    • off the coast of…
    • south of… / near… / at the end of…

    Example: Nantucket is off the coast of Massachusetts, whereas Key West is at the south-western end of the Florida Keys.

    Giving reasons

    • I would choose Nantucket because it looks calmer.
    • Key West seems more exciting, which is why I would recommend it to active travellers.
    • Nantucket is quieter, so it may be better for a peaceful holiday.

    Fast speaking formula

    Place + comparison + reason + preference

    Nantucket is in Massachusetts, in the north-east of the US. It is quieter and more traditional than Key West, so I would choose it for a relaxing holiday.

    7 • Oral exam practice

    Speak for 45–60 seconds

      Oral success tips

      • Start with the location.
      • Use at least two comparatives.
      • Add because / whereas / which means that.
      • Finish with your opinion or recommendation.

      8 • Writing studio

      Write a short comparison or recommendation

      Writing checklist

      • Write 80–125 words.
      • Use comparatives and reasons.
      • Mention the state and location.
      • Use at least one recommendation sentence.
      • End with a clear personal opinion.
