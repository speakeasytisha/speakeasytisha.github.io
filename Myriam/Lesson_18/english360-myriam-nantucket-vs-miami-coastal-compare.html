A lesson made for you

Two American coastal worlds — one powerful way to make your English longer and richer.

You already have strong travel vocabulary. In this lesson, you will use it to describe real photographs, compare two very different destinations, give personal recommendations and write structured answers for English 360° practice.

2. The big comparison

Use the facts you can see in the images — not just your imagination.

🌾 Nantucket

  • smaller, slower and more traditional
  • cobblestone streets, local shops and historic houses
  • strolling, cafés, boutique browsing and a relaxed island rhythm
  • ideal for a calm, charming and personal escape

🌴 Miami

  • larger, more urban and more cosmopolitan
  • Art Deco architecture, palm trees, tropical gardens and waterfront views
  • restaurants, culture, nightlife and city sightseeing
  • ideal for an energetic city-and-coast holiday
Detailed grammar

3. Compare with precision

These patterns will help you speak and write beyond short answers.

Short adjectives: -er than

Use this for short adjectives.

quiet → quieter than
Miami is busier than Nantucket.
Nantucket is smaller than Miami.
  • calm → calmer than
  • large → larger than
  • lively → livelier than

Long adjectives: more / less … than

Use more or less with longer adjectives.

Miami is more cosmopolitan than Nantucket.
Nantucket is less crowded than Miami.
  • more traditional than
  • more relaxing than
  • less commercial than

Contrast: whereas / unlike / however

Use these words to link two ideas.

Miami is lively, whereas Nantucket is peaceful.
Unlike Miami, Nantucket has few tall buildings.
Miami is exciting; however, it may be crowded.

Extend with which / where / because

These words make your answer more natural and detailed.

Nantucket is quieter, which means that it may be more relaxing.
Miami is a city where you can combine beaches and nightlife.
I would choose Nantucket because I enjoy calm places.

This / that / these / those in photos

Use them to point to visible details.

This cobblestone street looks historic.
That Art Deco hotel looks impressive.
These small shops make Nantucket look welcoming.
Those palm trees make Miami feel tropical.

Useful comparison verbs

Use dynamic verbs to make your travel advice stronger.

prefer, recommend, choose, explore, stroll, relax, enjoy

I would recommend Miami to travellers who enjoy city energy.
