Official exam briefing

How English 360° works

Use this section to reassure Myriam before training with the destination content.

How long?

Plan for about 45–60 minutes. Some provider pages describe the test as 45 minutes; the official English 360° site says all 5 competencies are assessed in around one hour.

How is it taken?

It is online, with a personal link. The platform may use ID verification, webcam/microphone permissions and proctoring tools.

How is it graded?

Comprehension and grammar are algorithm-based; speaking and writing are assessed by qualified native English evaluators.

Hardware / setup checklist

  • Computer, tablet or smartphone accepted by the platform
  • Stable internet connection
  • Personal English 360° link
  • Working microphone
  • Camera/webcam access for proctoring/ID checks if requested
  • Photo ID ready
  • Quiet room, charged device, browser permissions allowed

What to watch out for

  • Do not answer with only one short sentence.
  • Use connectors: because, whereas, which means that.
  • For oral answers, do not wait for perfection: speak clearly and keep going.
  • For writing, use paragraphs and stay close to the task.
  • For image tasks, say what you can see before giving opinions.

Grammar focus

Make descriptions and comparisons stronger

Location language

in Massachusetts • off the coast of Massachusetts • south of Cape Cod • at the tip of Cape Cod • near Boston Harbor

Nantucket is an island off the coast of Massachusetts, south of Cape Cod.

Comparatives

Short adjectives: quiet → quieter than. Long adjectives: historic → more historic than.

Boston is larger and more urban than Provincetown.

Superlatives

Use the most + adjective or adjective + -est.

Boston is the most urban destination in this lesson.

Reasons and recommendations

Use because, so, which means that, which is why.

Martha’s Vineyard is relaxing, which is why I would recommend it to families.
