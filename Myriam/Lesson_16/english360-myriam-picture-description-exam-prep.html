Pedagogical intro

The easy method to describe any picture

1

Start broad

Say where the scene is and what it generally shows.

“This picture shows a train station.”
2

Describe the people

Say who you can see and what they are doing.

“A passenger is waiting with a suitcase.”
3

Add details

Use there is / there are, prepositions, colours, objects and atmosphere.

“In the background, there is a departure board.”
4

Give an idea or hypothesis

Say what may be happening or what people could need.

“She may be asking for help because her bag is missing.”

    Grammar that goes with picture description

    Mini grammar boosters + exercises

    1) There is / There are

    Use this to describe what you can see.

    There is + singular noun
    There are + plural noun

    2) Present continuous

    Use this to say what people are doing now.

    subject + am / is / are + verb-ing

    3) Prepositions

    Use prepositions to locate things in the image.

    in the foreground • in the background • next to • behind • on • under

    4) Hypothesis

    Use may / might / could to guess what is happening.

    She may be tired. • They could be waiting for a train.

    Speaking checklist

    Model writing

    What to include

    • Time connector: first / then / after that / finally
    • Past simple for the story
    • Clear problem + solution
    • Logical ending
    • Around 80–125 words
    Exam success

    What to watch out for on the test

    Do this

    • Keep sentences short and clear.
    • Use there is / there are correctly.
    • Say what you can really see before you guess.
    • Add one opinion or one hypothesis at the end.
    • Use polite English in travel situations.

    Avoid this

    • Speaking too vaguely: “It is nice.”
    • Forgetting the verb: “A man in the station.”
    • Mixing tenses badly.
    • Talking too little.
    • Writing without connectors.

    Remember

    • You do not need perfect English.
    • You need clear communication and structure.
    • Travel situations are especially useful practice.
    • This skill supports listening, oral and writing.
