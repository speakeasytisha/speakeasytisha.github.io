0) English 360° exam blueprint (clear + detailed)

This page mirrors the official structure: Listening → Reading → Grammar → Oral → Writing. You can always open models when you want.

Before you start (technical + ID check)
✅ Technical requirements (recommended)
• Device: laptop/desktop strongly recommended (writing is easier). Smartphones/tablets can work. • Internet: stable connection. • Audio: headphones + microphone (recommended for clear speaking). • Webcam + microphone: used for proctoring/monitoring. • Browser: use a modern, up‑to‑date browser. Disable unnecessary extensions. • Permissions: allow microphone + camera access. • Link: have your personal English 360° link ready. • Quiet room: avoid background noise and interruptions.
🪪 ID verification (proctoring)
Have a valid photo ID ready (ID card/passport). English 360° uses identity verification (photo ID + face check) and proctoring (webcam/microphone monitoring).
⚠️ What to watch out for
• Avoid copy/paste and switching tabs. • Close extra tabs and disable browser extensions. • Don’t use AI tools or external help during the exam (the platform can flag suspicious behaviour). • Answer quickly: the test is timed. • For speaking: speak clearly, don’t rush, and use a simple structure.
Grading & “barème” (what evaluators look for)
Listening & Reading (auto‑scored)
Criteria: • lexical knowledge • grammatical knowledge • comprehension • fluency / speed
Grammar (auto‑scored)
Criteria: • conjugation • prepositions • modal verbs • tenses • subject‑verb agreement
Speaking (interaction + production) — scored by evaluators
Criteria: • sentence building / accuracy • vocabulary / idioms • pronunciation • grammar / tenses • reactivity / fluency
Writing — scored by evaluators
Criteria: • structure (clear paragraphs) • vocabulary (appropriate level) • grammar (tenses, prepositions, subject‑verb agreement) • spelling • clarity & logical flow

Exam-day strategy

1) What to master (most likely grammar + vocabulary)

Short, exam-safe rules + micro drills. Use Practice mode to see hints.

Tenses (core)
Present simple (habits) Present continuous (now) Past simple (yesterday/last…) Present perfect (experience / since/for) Future (going to / will)
Conditionals (very common)
Real future: If + present → will If I have time, I will practise. Imaginary now: If + past → would If I were rich, I would travel.
Requests + connectors
Could you please…? Would it be possible to…? I’d appreciate… Connectors: because / however / in addition / overall
