1) Why present perfect makes conversations amazing ✨

💬 Catching up

You can share updates without giving a precise date.

  • I’ve been busy lately.
  • I’ve worked a lot this week.
  • I’ve met my daughter‑in‑law.

✨ Real “news” words

just / already / yet make your story sound real.

  • I’ve just finished.
  • I’ve already sent it.
  • I haven’t finished yet.

🧭 Great follow‑up questions

You can keep the conversation going easily.

  • Have you ever…?
  • How long have you…?
  • Have you… yet?
French learner tip: present perfect is often “passé composé” in French, but used differently: it connects the past to now.
