2) Self-introduction review

This section helps you review how to present yourself clearly and professionally for Part 1 of the exam.

Model answer

My name is Yanis. I am twenty-five years old and I live in Schiltigheim. I work as a receptionist in a three-star hotel. My main duties are welcoming guests, answering the phone, replying to emails, and making reservations. I sometimes use English at work, especially on the phone and by email. I am improving my English because I would like to become a steward.

Useful patterns

  • I work as… → I work as a receptionist.
  • My main duties are… → My main duties are welcoming guests and answering the phone.
  • I use English… → I use English on the phone and by email.
  • I would like to… → I would like to become a steward.

3) Grammar review hub

Review the grammar you have already studied. Each block gives you the rule, examples, a comparison, a note area, and a short practice task with immediate feedback.

Grammar A — Present simple for your job and routine

Rule: Use the present simple for your job, your routine, and your responsibilities.

Example: I work as a receptionist.
Example: I answer the phone.
Example: I use English at work.
✅ I work as a receptionist.
❌ I’m working a receptionist.

Quick check

Grammar B — Questions and negatives with do / does

Rule: Use do and does for present simple questions and negatives.

Question: Do you use English at work?
Negative: I do not work at an airport.
Short answer: Yes, I do. / No, I don’t.
✅ Do you answer the phone?
❌ You answer the phone?

Quick check

Grammar C — Articles: a / an / the / no article

Rule: Use a before a singular noun with a consonant sound, an before a vowel sound, the for something specific, and sometimes no article at all.

a receptionist
an email
the phone
breakfast / emails / work
✅ I work in a hotel.
❌ I work in hotel.

Quick check

Grammar D — Prepositions for work

Rule: Some verbs and expressions need a specific preposition. Learn them as fixed groups.

work in a hotel
work at reception
speak to a passenger
reply to emails
use English on the phone / by email
✅ I use English on the phone.
❌ I use English in the phone.

Quick check

Grammar E — Adjectives and adverbs

Rule: Use an adjective to describe a noun or a person. Use an adverb to describe how you do something.

a calm passenger
to speak calmly
a polite steward
to answer politely
✅ The passenger is calm.
✅ The steward speaks calmly.

Quick check
