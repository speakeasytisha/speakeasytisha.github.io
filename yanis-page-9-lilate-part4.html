Yanis — Read, Identify & Respond ✈️

In this lesson, you practise the next LILATE step: reading a professional support, identifying the main information, and answering clearly in a passenger or steward situation.

Dans cette leçon, vous travaillez l’étape suivante du LILATE : lire un support professionnel, repérer les informations principales et répondre clairement dans une situation liée aux passagers ou au métier de steward.

1) What this part of the exam wants

You may read a text or look at a document, identify the useful information, and answer questions clearly.

Vous pouvez lire un texte ou regarder un document, repérer les informations utiles et répondre clairement aux questions.

LILATE target

📄
Read or consult a support
A message, a notice, a small timetable, a board, or a short text.
Un message, une notice, un petit horaire, un tableau ou un texte court.
🔍
Find the useful information
Look for the main idea, the key details, and the next action.
Chercher l’idée principale, les détails importants et l’action suivante.
🧠
Understand the general meaning
You do not need every word. You need the important meaning.
Vous n’avez pas besoin de chaque mot, mais du sens important.
💬
Answer professionally
Explain the information clearly to a passenger or in a work situation.
Expliquer l’information clairement à un passager ou dans une situation professionnelle.

2) Grammar workshop — question words to find key information

These words help you understand a document and ask the right follow-up questions.

Ces mots vous aident à comprendre un document et à poser les bonnes questions de suivi.

Rule + concrete examples

👤
Who = person
Who is the passenger? Who needs help?
Who = la personne
❓
What = thing / information
What is the problem? What is the new gate?
What = la chose / l’information
📍
Where = place
Where is boarding? Where should the passenger go?
Where = le lieu
⏰
When = time
When does boarding start? When is the next flight?
When = le moment / l’heure
🧾
Why = reason
Why is the flight delayed? Why is the passenger worried?
Why = la raison
➡️
What next? = next action
What should the passenger do next?
Que faut-il faire ensuite ?

Useful pattern

What is the problem?
Quel est le problème ?

Where is the gate?
Où est la porte ?

What should the passenger do next?
Que doit faire le passager ensuite ?

3) Grammar workshop — there is / there are + because / so / but

These structures help you describe a support and explain information clearly.

Ces structures vous aident à décrire un support et à expliquer les informations clairement.

There is / There are

1️⃣
There is + singular noun
There is a delay. There is a new gate. There is an important message.
Il y a + singulier
2️⃣
There are + plural noun
There are two flights. There are three important details.
Il y a + pluriel
🚫
Negative
There is no delay. There are no seats available.
Forme négative

Because / so / but

🧠
because = reason
The flight is delayed because of the weather.
because = parce que / à cause de
➡️
so = result / consequence
The gate has changed, so the passenger must go to C4.
so = donc
⚖️
but = contrast
The passenger is worried, but the situation is clear now.
but = mais

7) Listen and identify the useful information

Listen to the announcement. Then answer the questions.

Écoutez l’annonce. Puis répondez aux questions.

Announcement

This announcement is about a gate change, a short connection, and what the passenger must keep ready.
Cette annonce parle d’un changement de porte, d’une correspondance courte et de ce que le passager doit garder prêt.
Transcript:
Attention please. Flight AF 438 to Paris is delayed by twenty minutes because of the weather. Boarding will now begin at gate C4. Passengers with a short connection should go to desk B2. Please keep your passport and boarding pass ready.
Transcription : Le vol AF 438 à destination de Paris a vingt minutes de retard à cause de la météo. L’embarquement commencera désormais à la porte C4. Les passagers avec une correspondance courte doivent se rendre au comptoir B2. Veuillez garder votre passeport et votre carte d’embarquement prêts.

8) Writing ladder — simple, better, stronger

Compare the 3 levels. Then write your own version.

Comparez les 3 niveaux. Puis écrivez votre propre version.

Level 1 — Simple

There is a delay. Go to gate C4.

Très simple. L’idée est claire, mais il manque des détails.

Level 2 — Better

There is a twenty-minute delay. Please go to gate C4 and keep your boarding pass ready.

Mieux : il y a plus d’informations et une consigne claire.

Level 3 — Stronger

Your flight is delayed by twenty minutes because of the weather, so please go to gate C4 and keep your passport and boarding pass ready. If you have a short connection, please go to desk B2.

Plus fort : raison + conséquence + prochaine étape.

How to improve step by step

1️⃣
Say the main information
There is a delay. The gate is C4.
2️⃣
Add one useful detail
twenty minutes / passport / boarding pass
3️⃣
Add the reason or the result
because / so / but
4️⃣
Give the next step politely
Please go to... Please keep... If you need help...
