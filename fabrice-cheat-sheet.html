Oral structure (the “safe” plan)

Keep it simple, calm, and structured.

✅ Answer clearly ✅ Add 2 details ✅ Use 1 connector ✅ Close politely

6 blocks (30–60 seconds)

  1. Greeting + name
  2. Work for Renault + field
  3. 2 responsibilities
  4. Email / phone / work communication
  5. Travel situations you can manage
  6. Short closing sentence

Tip

If you forget a word, rephrase calmly. Clear structure matters more than difficult vocabulary.

Fluency = calm + simple English + confidence.

How to talk about Renault (simple English)

Based on your Renault sessions: self-presentation, work vocabulary, emails, and the final Fabrice masterclass.

Easy self-introduction

Hello, my name is Fabrice. I work for Renault in quality. I check information, follow quality procedures, and communicate with colleagues and suppliers. I can also write simple emails, answer questions, and explain practical work situations. Overall, I am serious, practical, and motivated to keep improving my English.

Work phrases
  • I work for Renault.
  • I work in quality.
  • I work on quality missions.
  • I check documents and information.
  • I follow procedures.
  • I report problems and possible solutions.
Meetings / suppliers / phone
  • I join meetings and ask questions.
  • I talk to colleagues and suppliers.
  • I confirm the next steps.
  • I write simple emails.
  • I answer basic questions.
  • I explain practical situations clearly.

Email and phone support

Short, reusable structures for professional communication.

Email builder

Greeting — Dear Mr. ___ / Hello ___,

Opening — Thank you for your email. / I am writing about...

Information — I am sending the updated information.

Request — Could you please confirm the next step?

Closing — Please let me know if you have any questions. / Kind regards,

Phone support

Hello, this is Fabrice calling.

I am calling about the quality meeting.

Is now a good time to talk?

I would like to confirm the next step.

Thank you for your time.

Quick email model

Dear Mr. Smith,

Thank you for your email.

I am writing about the quality update for next week.

Could you please confirm the meeting time?

Please let me know if you have any questions.

Kind regards,
Fabrice

Travel survival English

Airport, hotel, restaurant, directions, and car rental.

Airport

Where is gate 12?

Where can I check in?

Can I take this bag on the plane?

Hotel

I have a reservation.

Could I have a quiet room, please?

What time is breakfast?

Restaurant

I would like to order.

Could we have the menu, please?

Could you recommend something?

Directions

Where is the station?

How can I get there?

Is it far from here?

Car rental

I would like to rent a car.

Is insurance included?

What documents do you need?

Ready-to-say models

A2 (simple)

Hello, my name is Fabrice. I work for Renault in quality. I check information, follow procedures, and communicate with colleagues and suppliers. When I travel, I can ask for directions, reserve a hotel, and order food. To sum up, I am serious and motivated to keep improving my English.

B1 (expand)

Hello, my name is Fabrice. I work for Renault in quality. My job includes checking information, following quality procedures, and communicating with colleagues and suppliers. I also use English in practical situations such as emails, meetings, and phone calls. When I travel, I can manage simple situations at the airport, hotel, restaurant, or car rental desk. Overall, I am organised and I learn through practice.

Travel mini model

Hello, I have a reservation for two nights. Could I have a quiet room, please? Also, what time is breakfast? Thank you very much.

Checklist

  • Start with: Hello, my name is Fabrice...
  • Say where you work and 2 responsibilities.
  • Use 1 connector: also / for example / overall / to sum up.
  • For travel, ask one clear question at a time.
  • In emails: greeting → purpose → detail → request → closing.
