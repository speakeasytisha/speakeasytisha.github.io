Accuracy booster

Stop translating from French: build natural English

These points target your observed needs: sentence structure, past simple, present perfect, for/since/ago/during, and common French-transfer errors.

Past simple

Use it for finished actions in the past.

Subject + V2 / regular verb + -ed

I studied law. I worked with property developers. I stopped one year ago.

Present perfect

Use it for experience or a situation connected to now.

Subject + have/has + past participle

I have worked in real estate for twenty-four years.

For / since / ago / during

For + duration: for 24 years

Since + starting point: since 2001

Ago after a finished time: one year ago

During + event/period noun: during my career

French-transfer fixes

❌ I am interested by → ✅ I am interested in

❌ I chosed → ✅ I chose

❌ I’m looking job → ✅ I’m looking for a job

❌ I haven’t any contact → ✅ I don’t have any contacts
