Safe speaking frame

The 4-sentence method

1. Be polite: Excuse me, could you help me, please?

2. Say the problem: There is a problem with my room.

3. Ask for a solution: Could you check it, please?

4. Confirm: Thank you. That’s perfect.

Step 4

Grammar focus: simple problem sentences

Use short, safe patterns. They help you speak clearly without translating too much.

There is / There are

There is a problem with my room.

There are no towels in the bathroom.

It doesn’t work

The Wi-Fi doesn’t work.

The key card doesn’t work.

Can / Could you…?

Can you help me, please?

Could you check it, please?

I would like / I need

I would like a quiet room, please.

I need a taxi, please.

Step 6

Listening: a problem at the hotel

Listen first, answer the questions, then reveal the transcript.

Dialogue

You are at hotel reception. Your key card doesn’t work and you ask for help.

Frankie: Good evening. Could you help me, please? My key card doesn’t work. I can’t open the door.

Receptionist: Of course. What is your room number?

Frankie: Room 214.

Receptionist: Thank you. I can make a new key card for you.

Frankie: That’s great. Thank you very much.

Final mission

Explain a problem in 5 clear sentences

Use this safe structure:

  1. Excuse me, could you help me, please?
  2. There is a problem with...
  3. It doesn’t work / I can’t...
  4. Could you check it, please?
  5. Thank you. That’s perfect.

A2+ model:

Excuse me, could you help me, please? There is a problem with my room. The Wi-Fi doesn’t work and I need to send a message. Could you check it, please? Thank you very much.
