Step 3

Grammar compass: one verb, three time zones

Use the time marker to choose the verb form. Keep the sentence short, clear and correct.

☀️

Present simple

Today / usually / every day

I work. · I start at 8. · I like walking.

🌙

Past simple

Yesterday / last weekend / two days ago

I worked. · I went shopping. · I had lunch.

🌱

Future with going to

Tomorrow / next week / this summer

I am going to work. · We are going to travel.

He / She / It + s

I work → She works.

I go → He goes.

I watch → She watches.

Questions need a helper

Do you work today?

Did you work yesterday?

Are you going to work tomorrow?

Useful verb patterns

I need to practise.

I would like to travel.

I like walking.

Step 7

Listening: a week, a problem and a plan

Listen once for the general idea. Listen again for the verbs. Then reveal the transcript.

Dialogue

Two people talk about yesterday, today and tomorrow.

A: Hi Frankie. Did you work yesterday?

B: Yes, I worked in the morning and I finished at four.

A: What are you doing today?

B: Today, I am learning English. We are practising verbs and questions.

A: Great. And what are you going to do tomorrow?

B: Tomorrow, I am going to relax. I am also going to walk with my dog if the weather is nice.

Step 8

Speaking studio: build longer answers

You can use the model, then personalise it orally.

A2 safe answer

Yesterday, I worked in the morning. Today, I am learning English. Tomorrow, I am going to relax.

A2+ better answer

Yesterday, I worked in the morning and I finished at four. Today, I am practising English because I want to speak more easily. Tomorrow, I am going to relax and walk with my dog.

B1- stretch answer

Yesterday was quite normal: I worked, then I came home and relaxed. Today, I am focusing on English verbs because they help me speak with more structure. Tomorrow, I am going to have a quiet day, but I may go for a walk if the weather is nice.

Step 10

Final verb mission and homework

Use this structure at the end of the lesson to show progress.

Final oral mission

  1. Say one sentence about yesterday.
  2. Say one sentence about today.
  3. Say one sentence about tomorrow.
  4. Add one reason with because.
  5. Answer one follow-up question.

Homework

Prepare 6 sentences:

  • 2 sentences about yesterday
  • 2 sentences about today or usually
  • 2 sentences about tomorrow or next week

Remember: short, clear sentences are better than long confusing ones.
