Step 3

Grammar focus: your plan or your promise?

Use going to for a plan you already have. Use will for a promise, an offer, or a decision you make now.

🗓️

Going to = a plan

am / is / are + going to + base verb

You are going to draw at home.

You are going to visit your family this weekend.

You are not going to stay at home.

Are you going to travel next month?

🤝

Will = a promise or offer

will + base verb

Don’t worry, I’ll help you.

I’ll send you the address.

I’ll carry your bag.

The phone is ringing. I’ll answer it.

💡

Keep it simple

✅ You are going to go.

❌ You are going to went.

✅ I’ll help you.

❌ I’ll helped you.

After going to and will, use the simple verb.

Quick going to grammar guide
PositiveNegativeQuestion
You’re going to paint.You aren’t going to paint.Are you going to paint?
She’s going to travel.She isn’t going to travel.Is she going to travel?
We’re going to meet at 2.We aren’t going to meet at 2.Are we going to meet at 2?

Listening: making a weekend plan

Dialogue: Saturday plans

Click Listen when you are ready. The transcript stays hidden at first.

Anna: Hi, would you like to go for a walk on Saturday?

Claire: Yes, I would love to. What time are we going to meet?

Anna: We are going to meet at two o’clock near the park.

Claire: Great. I am going to bring my dog.

Anna: Perfect. I will bring some water. After the walk, are you going to have lunch with me?

Claire: Yes, I am. That sounds lovely.

Final oral mission

Plan a perfect Saturday

Speak for 6–8 sentences. You can read the prompts first, then try without looking at them.

  1. Say when your plan is.
  2. Say who you are going to be with.
  3. Say two things you are going to do.
  4. Include drawing or painting if you want to.
  5. Make one invitation.
  6. Make one promise or offer with will.
  7. Say why you are looking forward to it.

Optional homework

Your creative weekend plan

Write 6–8 sentences about a future weekend. Include one plan with going to, one invitation, one sentence with will, and one creative activity: drawing or painting.

Example: Next Saturday, I’m going to paint at home in the morning. In the afternoon, I’m going to visit my family. Would you like to have lunch with us? I’ll make a dessert. I’m looking forward to it because I enjoy creative time and family time.
