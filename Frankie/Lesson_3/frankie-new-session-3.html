Step 4

Grammar toolkit: simple polite travel English

Today’s grammar is practical. Frankie uses short structures to ask questions, make requests and explain needs.

1. I would like…

I would like + noun / to + verb

I would like a ticket, please.

I would like to check in, please.

2. Can I…?

Can I + verb?

Can I pay by card?

Can I have the menu, please?

3. Could you…?

Could you + verb?

Could you help me, please?

Could you repeat, please?

4. I need… / I have…

I need + noun · I have + noun

I need a taxi.

I have a reservation.

Teacher note: The goal is not to explain every modal verb. The goal is to help Frankie use useful sentence frames automatically in real situations.

Step 7

Listening: a simple travel day

Listen first for the general idea, then listen again for details. The transcript stays hidden until Frankie wants help.

Frankie: Good afternoon. I would like to check in, please.

Receptionist: Do you have a reservation?

Frankie: Yes, I have a reservation in the name of Dumarquez.

Receptionist: Thank you. Your room is on the second floor. Breakfast is from seven to ten.

Frankie: Great, thank you. Can I have the Wi-Fi password, please?

Receptionist: Of course. It is written on your key card.

Hint: Listen for reservation, room floor, breakfast time and Wi-Fi password.
