⚙️ Verb Magic — The Engine of English

A verb is an action or a state. Verbs make English move.

Action verbs

Use action verbs for things you do.

work, help, speak, wait, go, sell, show, check

State verbs

Use state verbs for situations, feelings, or identity.

be, have, like, love, need, want, understand

Sort the verbs

Choose if the verb is an action or a state.

✨ Grammar Spell 1 — Present Simple

Use the present simple for routines, jobs, habits, facts, and repeated actions.

When do I use it?

  • Facts: I live near Nantes.
  • Jobs: I work in a boutique.
  • Habits: I go to the gym four times a week.
  • Customer service: I help guests.

Structure

I / you / we / they + verb

he / she / it + verb + s

I help guests. / She helps guests.

Negative

I / you / we / they + do not + verb

he / she / it + does not + verb

I do not work here. / She does not work here.

Questions

Do / Does + subject + verb?

Do you work here? / Does she help guests?

✨ Grammar Spell 2 — Present Continuous

Use the present continuous for actions happening now, current activity, and temporary situations.

When do I use it?

  • Now: I am helping a guest.
  • At the moment: Guests are waiting.
  • Today / temporary: She is working in the boutique today.

Structure

am / is / are + verb + ing

I am helping. / She is working. / They are waiting.

✨ Grammar Spell 3 — A / An / The / Nothing

This is very important for natural English in attractions, boutique, and interviews.

A / An

Use for one thing when it is not specific.

a before consonant sound: a map, a bag

an before vowel sound: an attraction, an entrance

The

Use for something specific or known by both people.

The castle is on the left.

No article Ø

Use no article for general ideas.

I love Disney. I need English. I study literature.

🏰 Grammar Spell 4 — There is / There are

Use this to describe what exists in the park or in the boutique.

There is

Use for ONE thing.

There is a shop near the castle.

There are

Use for MORE THAN ONE thing.

There are two entrances.
