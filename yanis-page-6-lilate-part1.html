2. Grammar — build better English for the exam

Read the rule, look at the concrete examples, compare right and wrong English, then write your own examples. This part is longer on purpose so you can really understand before you speak.
Lis la règle, regarde les exemples concrets, compare le bon et le mauvais anglais, puis écris tes propres exemples. Cette partie est volontairement plus longue pour que tu comprennes vraiment avant de parler.

Rule 1

Basic English word order

Use: subject + verb + object + place + time.
On utilise : sujet + verbe + complément + lieu + temps.

This order helps the examiner understand you quickly. In the LILATE, clear order is very important because you need to sound simple and organised.
Cet ordre aide l’examinateur à te comprendre rapidement. Au LILATE, un ordre clair est très important car tu dois paraître simple et organisé.

✅ I answer customer emails at the hotel every day.

✅ I speak to guests at reception in the morning.

✅ I use English by email very often.

✅ I welcome passengers on board every day.

❌ I at the hotel answer emails every day.

✅ I answer emails at the hotel every day.

❌ I by email often use English.

✅ I often use English by email.

English usually does not follow French word order. Put the verb early. Put place and time later.
L’anglais ne suit généralement pas l’ordre français. Mets le verbe tôt. Mets le lieu et le temps plus tard.
Rule 2

Adjectives = before the noun

The adjective comes before the noun.
L’adjectif se place avant le nom.

Use adjectives to describe yourself, your work style, your hotel, or your future role. This makes your English richer without making it too difficult.
Utilise les adjectifs pour te décrire, décrire ta façon de travailler, ton hôtel ou ton futur rôle. Cela rend ton anglais plus riche sans le rendre trop difficile.

✅ a friendly receptionist

✅ a professional steward

✅ a calm passenger

✅ a busy hotel

✅ a helpful colleague

❌ a customer polite

✅ a polite customer

❌ a hotel busy

✅ a busy hotel

You can also put two adjectives before the noun: a friendly and professional receptionist.
Tu peux aussi mettre deux adjectifs avant le nom : a friendly and professional receptionist.
Rule 3

Adverbs = how you do the action

Adverbs describe the verb.
Les adverbes décrivent l’action du verbe.

Adverbs tell the examiner how you speak, answer, explain, or help. This is useful for customer service and for sounding more professional.
Les adverbes disent à l’examinateur comment tu parles, réponds, expliques ou aides. C’est utile pour le service client et pour paraître plus professionnel.

✅ I speak clearly.

✅ I answer emails quickly.

✅ I explain things politely.

✅ I work carefully.

❌ I speak polite.

✅ I speak politely.

❌ I answer emails quick.

✅ I answer emails quickly.

Very often: adjective + -ly → polite / politely, quick / quickly, careful / carefully. But some words stay adjectives: calm, friendly, busy.
Très souvent : adjectif + -ly → polite / politely, quick / quickly, careful / carefully. Mais certains mots restent des adjectifs : calm, friendly, busy.
Rule 4

Frequency adverbs

Use frequency before the main verb, but after be.
On place la fréquence avant le verbe principal, mais après be.

Use these words to talk about your routine: always, usually, often, sometimes, never. This is perfect for describing your job and your habits for the exam.
Utilise ces mots pour parler de ta routine : always, usually, often, sometimes, never. C’est parfait pour décrire ton travail et tes habitudes pour l’examen.

✅ I usually answer the phone.

✅ I often use English by email.

✅ I am always polite with customers.

✅ I am sometimes stressed, but I stay calm.

✅ I never ignore a customer.

❌ I use often English by email.

✅ I often use English by email.

❌ I always am polite.

✅ I am always polite.

With be: I am always calm. With other verbs: I often answer emails.
Avec be : I am always calm. Avec les autres verbes : I often answer emails.

5. LILATE checkpoint — speak like a future steward

Use only what you know, but say it more clearly and more professionally. You can now see the models and hear them.
Utilise seulement ce que tu connais déjà, mais dis-le plus clairement et plus professionnellement. Tu peux maintenant voir les modèles et les écouter.

Checkpoint A — Introduce yourself

Say: your name, your current job, one strength, when you use English, and your goal.
Dis : ton nom, ton travail actuel, une qualité, quand tu utilises l’anglais et ton objectif.

Model:

My name is Yanis. I work as a receptionist in a three-star hotel. I am friendly and professional. I often use English by email and on the phone. My goal is to become a steward.

Checkpoint B — Welcome and help

Say: welcome, offer help, ask one simple question, and reassure the person.
Dis : accueille, propose ton aide, pose une question simple et rassure la personne.

Model:

Good afternoon. Welcome. How can I help you today? What seems to be the problem? I am here to help you.

Checkpoint C — Ask and clarify

Say: ask for information, ask for repetition, and check understanding.
Dis : demande une information, demande de répéter et vérifie la compréhension.

Model:

Could you repeat that, please? What time is your flight? Let me check if I understand correctly.

Checkpoint D — Full mini answer

Put everything together: self-introduction + strength + English use + goal + simple help language.
Assemble tout : présentation + qualité + utilisation de l’anglais + objectif + langage simple d’aide.

Model:

Good afternoon. My name is Yanis. I work as a receptionist in a three-star hotel. I am calm, friendly, and professional. I often use English by email and on the phone. My goal is to become a steward. How can I help you today?

You are building exactly what the exam needs: clear self-introduction, clear job description, clearer questions, and clearer help language.
Tu construis exactement ce dont l’examen a besoin : une présentation claire, une description claire du travail, des questions plus claires et un langage d’aide plus clair.
