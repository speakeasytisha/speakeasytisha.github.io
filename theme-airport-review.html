Airport & Hotel – Trip Review

Review your journey to San Francisco: airport, customs, hotel and a final polite email using clear vocabulary and past simple.

Your score: 0 / 0

1. Airport vocabulary review

Key words from the airport lesson: use them to talk about your arrival at San Francisco International Airport.

terminal

The building where passengers arrive, check in and board their flights.

gate

The number/letter where you wait to board the plane (for example: Gate A12).

boarding pass

The document (paper or on your phone) that lets you board the plane.

security

The place where you show liquids, take out your laptop and go through the scanner.

customs

The control after your flight where an officer checks your passport and asks questions about your bags.

baggage claim

The area with carousels where you pick up your checked luggage.

Quick airport quiz

1. Where do you usually remove liquids and your laptop?

2. You see “B12” on your boarding pass. What is B12?

3. After the flight, where do you collect your suitcase?

2. Build your airport story

Use the options to create a short paragraph about your arrival at San Francisco International Airport. You can then copy it and change details to make it more personal.

3. Hotel review – past simple & adjectives

Use the past simple to talk about a finished stay and adjectives to describe your room, the staff, the location and the breakfast.

Past simple – examples:

  • We stayed at a modern hotel near Union Square.
  • The room was clean and quiet.
  • The staff were very friendly and helpful.
  • We didn’t use the pool because we were tired.

Adjectives for reviews: comfortable, clean, noisy, modern, central, convenient, friendly, helpful, expensive, cheap.

Grammar & vocabulary quiz

2. Choose the best description of the room.

3. Choose the correct negative sentence.

4. Build your hotel review

Use the dropdown menus to create a short hotel review. Then personalise it by changing details.

5. Write a polite email to the hotel

Use this builder to write a short, polite email to the hotel: to say thank you, to ask a question or to mention a small problem.

Typical structure:

  • Subject: Question about my recent stay – May 10–12
  • Greeting: Dear Hotel Team,
  • Opening line: say who you are and when you stayed.
  • Body: thank them, give a detail, mention a question or a small problem politely.
  • Closing: Thank you for your help. Kind regards, + your name.
