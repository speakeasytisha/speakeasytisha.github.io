1. Your route today

This two-hour lesson moves from safe sentences about your job to a short, guided professional meeting. Every task includes a model so you always have a clear starting point.

1 • Say who you are

Your role

You can explain what you manage, who you work with and what you do every week.

Vous pouvez expliquer ce que vous gérez, avec qui vous travaillez et ce que vous faites chaque semaine.

2 • Report simple facts

Your club numbers

You can talk about new memberships, cancellations, staff and basic priorities.

Vous pouvez parler des nouvelles adhésions, des résiliations, de l’équipe et des priorités simples.

3 • Interact

Your manager meeting

You can ask a simple question, respond, and say what you need to do next.

Vous pouvez poser une question simple, répondre et dire ce que vous devez faire ensuite.

Speak safely. Use short sentences. A clear simple sentence is much better than a long sentence that makes you freeze.
Parlez en toute sécurité. Utilisez des phrases courtes. Une phrase simple et claire est beaucoup mieux qu’une longue phrase qui vous bloque.

3. Grammar for clear professional English

You already know the present simple. Today, you will use it professionally and add a useful new structure: have to / need to.

Grammar reminder

Present simple = your usual work

Use the present simple for your job, routines, facts and regular meetings.

Utilisez le présent simple pour votre travail, vos habitudes, les faits et les réunions régulières.

I / You / We / They
I manage two clubs.
Je gère deux clubs.
He / She / It
The manager checks the report.
Le responsable vérifie le rapport.
Remember: with he / she / it, add -s.
The manager works. The club opens at 6 a.m.
Rappel : avec he / she / it, ajoutez -s.
Useful model

I meet the club managers every week.
Je rencontre les responsables de club chaque semaine.

We review the membership figures.
Nous faisons le point sur les chiffres des adhésions.

The manager checks the report.
Le responsable vérifie le rapport.

New grammar

Have to / need to = an obligation or next action

Use have to or need to before a verb to say something is necessary.

Utilisez have to ou need to avant un verbe pour dire que quelque chose est nécessaire.

Positive
I have to check the figures.
I need to call the manager.
Je dois vérifier les chiffres. / Je dois appeler le responsable.
Question
Do you have to call today?
Do we need to check it?
Est-ce que vous devez appeler aujourd’hui ? / Devons-nous vérifier cela ?
Important: after have to and need to, use the base verb.
✅ I need to check.   ❌ I need to checks.
Important : après have to et need to, utilisez le verbe à l’infinitif sans to supplémentaire.
Useful model

I need to check the cancellations.
Je dois vérifier les résiliations.

We have to prepare the weekly report.
Nous devons préparer le rapport hebdomadaire.

Do you need to speak to the team?
Avez-vous besoin de parler à l’équipe ?

Quick number reminder

How many…? / There are…

Use How many with people and things you can count: members, cancellations, clubs, reports, staff.

Utilisez How many avec les personnes et les choses que vous pouvez compter : adhérents, résiliations, clubs, rapports, salariés.

QuestionAnswer
How many clubs do you manage?I manage two clubs.
How many members of staff are there?There are about eleven members of staff.
How many cancellations are there this week?There are five cancellations.

D’accord. Vous devez vérifier le planning du soir. Envoyez-moi un point vendredi, s’il vous plaît.
