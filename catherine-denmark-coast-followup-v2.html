Say it about your trip

2) Reading — a beautiful trip by the sea

Last week, you went to Denmark to visit your son and your daughter-in-law. They live near the coast, so the atmosphere was calm, fresh, and relaxing. During your stay, you spent time with them, talked about family life, and enjoyed simple moments together. You walked by the sea, admired the landscape, and discovered new places.

One afternoon, while you were walking along the coast, you were looking at the water and talking about the area. The weather was cool but pleasant, and the light on the sea was beautiful. You may also have visited a town, had a meal together, or simply enjoyed being at home with them.

Since the trip, you have kept lovely memories. You have shared time, conversation, and affection. Now you can talk about what you did, what was happening, and what this visit has meant to you.

Comprehension check

1. Why did you go to Denmark?

2. What was happening during the coastal walk?

3) Grammar workshop — tell the story clearly

Past simple

Use: finished actions in the past.

Time words: yesterday, last week, during the trip, on Sunday.

✅ I went to Denmark last week.

✅ We walked by the coast.

✅ I had a wonderful time.

❓ Did you enjoy the trip?

Past continuous

Use: background actions or actions in progress at a past moment.

Form: was / were + verb-ing

✅ We were walking along the coast.

✅ I was talking to my daughter-in-law.

✅ While we were walking, we saw the sea birds.

Present perfect

Use: past experience connected to now.

Form: have / has + past participle

✅ We have shared lovely moments.

✅ I have visited Denmark before.

✅ I have already visited Denmark before.

Prepositions — movement, place, time

Movement: go to Denmark, drive to the coast, walk to the harbour

Place: stay in Denmark, be at home, walk on the beach, sit by the sea

Time: in the morning, on Sunday, at 10 o’clock

✅ I went to Denmark.

✅ I was in Denmark for a few days.

✅ We had coffee at home.

✅ We walked by the coast in the afternoon.

Adjectives and adverbs — describe places, people, and actions

Adjective: describes a noun or comes after be.

Examples: a beautiful place, the sea was calm, your daughter-in-law is kind.

Adverb: describes a verb. Many adverbs end in -ly.

Examples: she speaks kindly, we walked slowly, they welcomed you warmly.

Important: good → well

beautiful → beautifully warm → warmly quiet → quietly slow → slowly kind → kindly good → well

5) Denmark, countries and nationalities

Use this section to talk about people, countries, and cultures naturally. This is useful when you speak about your family, your trip, Africa, and the people you meet.

Country → nationality

  • 🇩🇰 Denmark → Danish — They live in Denmark. They are Danish.
  • 🇫🇷 France → French — You live in France. You are French.
  • 🇬🇧 England → English — He comes from England. He is English.
  • 🇺🇸 The United States → American — She is American.
  • 🌍 African describes nationality or origin in Africa, but for one person it is often better to name the country too: Kenyan, South African, Moroccan, Egyptian...

Useful model sentences

  • 💬 My son lives in Denmark. His wife is Danish.
  • 💬 I live in France, so I am French.
  • 💬 We met people from different countries.
  • 💬 During our trip to Africa, we saw amazing wildlife.
  • 💬 I love learning about different cultures and ways of life.

6) Real-life dialogues — practical and natural

Dialogue 1 — talking about the trip

You: I had a wonderful time in Denmark.

Daughter-in-law: I’m so glad. What did you enjoy the most?

You: I loved spending time with you and walking by the coast.

Daughter-in-law: What was the atmosphere like?

You: It was calm, fresh, and very relaxing.

Useful phrases
  • ✨ I had a wonderful time.
  • 🌊 I loved walking by the coast.
  • 🏡 I felt very comfortable in your home.
  • 💬 I really enjoyed our conversations.

Dialogue 2 — planning a day out

You: What would you like to do today?

Family: We could go to the harbour or walk by the sea.

You: I would love to see the harbour. What time should we leave?

Family: We could leave around ten.

You: Perfect. I’ll be ready at ten.

Planning language
  • 🕒 What time should we leave?
  • 📍 I would like to visit…
  • ☕ Shall we have coffee first?
  • 🚶 We could go for a walk after lunch.

Dialogue 3 — talking about your work and interests

Daughter-in-law: Can you tell me more about what you do?

You: I used to work as a social worker.

You: Now, I work as an assessor with young people in court.

Daughter-in-law: And you also love animals, don’t you?

You: Yes, I wanted to volunteer at an animal shelter, but there were already too many volunteers.

7) Writing studio — a thank-you note to your son and daughter-in-law

💌 Openings

  • Dear both,
  • Hello my dears,
  • Thank you so much for…

💗 Useful expressions

  • I had a wonderful time with you.
  • Thank you for your warm welcome.
  • I loved our time together.
  • I already miss you.
  • I love you very much.

🌟 Closing lines

  • Take care and see you soon.
  • With all my love,
  • Lots of love,

A2 model

Dear both,
Thank you for your warm welcome in Denmark. I had a very nice time with you. I loved walking by the coast and spending time together. I already miss you. Lots of love.

B1 model

Dear both,
Thank you so much for welcoming me so warmly during my stay in Denmark. I had a wonderful time with you. I especially enjoyed our walks by the coast, our conversations, and the peaceful atmosphere. I already miss you both and I am very grateful for these lovely moments. With love.

B2 model

Dear both,
I just wanted to thank you again for the wonderful time I had with you in Denmark. Your welcome was so warm and generous, and I felt completely at home with you. I will cherish our walks by the sea, our meals together, and all the quiet, meaningful moments we shared. I love you very much and I miss you already. With all my love.
