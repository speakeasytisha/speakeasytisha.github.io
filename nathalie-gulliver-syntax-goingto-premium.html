Part 1 • English sentence logic

The English sentence skeleton

Big idea: English usually starts with the person or thing, then the action, then the information.

En anglais, on commence souvent par la personne/la chose, puis l’action, puis les informations.

SUBJECT + VERB + COMPLEMENT

I travel by train.

You like quiet hotels.

We are looking for platform four.

French thinking

Je voyage souvent en train.

English structure

I often travel by train.

Part 2 • One verb first

Present Simple with one verb: to travel

Use Present Simple for: habits, routines, facts, and schedules.

On utilise le présent simple pour les habitudes, les faits, les routines et les horaires.

Step A — Affirmative

Only he / she / it changes. Add -s.

SubjectVerbExample
ItravelI travel by train.
YoutravelYou travel by ferry.
He / She / IttravelsShe travels by plane.
We / TheytravelThey travel every year.

Step B — Negative

Use do not / does not + base verb. After does, the -s disappears.

SubjectStructureExample
I / you / we / theydo not travelI do not travel every weekend.
he / she / itdoes not travelShe does not travel every weekend.

Part 4 • Nathalie’s question

Why is “about” at the end?

Short answer: English often keeps the small word with the verb idea. Do not learn “about” alone. Learn the expression block.

Réponse simple : en anglais, le petit mot reste souvent avec l’idée du verbe. On apprend le bloc complet.

talk about

parler de

What are you talking about?

look for

chercher

What are you looking for?

wait for

Who are you waiting for?

travel with

Who are you travelling with?

Part 5 • Travel prepositions

By / on / in / at — the travel logic

BY = transport method

Use by when you say the general way you travel.

by train • by plane • by ferry • by bus

Not: by a train

ON = public transport

Use on when you are physically inside public transport.

on the train • on the bus • on the ferry

IN = small/private vehicle

Use in with cars and taxis.

in a taxi • in a car

AT = exact travel place

Use at for travel locations.

at the station • at the airport • at the hotel

Activity 5 — Choose the right preposition

Part 6 • Future plans

Future with going to

Use going to for plans, intentions and future projects.

On utilise going to pour les projets et intentions.

SUBJECT + BE + GOING TO + BASE VERB

Step A — One verb first: travel

SubjectBEgoing tobase verbSentence
Iamgoing totravelI am going to travel.
Youaregoing totravelYou are going to travel.
Sheisgoing totravelShe is going to travel.
Wearegoing totravelWe are going to travel.

Step B — Negative

Put not after BE.

I am not going to travel this weekend.

Step C — Question

Move BE before the subject.

Are you going to travel this summer?
