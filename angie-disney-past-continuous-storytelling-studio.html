⭐ Grammar Step 1 — What is the Past Continuous?

Use the past continuous for an action that was happening at a specific moment in the past.

Structure

Subject + was / were + verb-ing

I was helping a guest.

I / he / she / it

Use was.

She was speaking English.

You / we / they

Use were.

They were waiting in line.

Why use it?

It creates the background of a story.

I was working when a guest asked for help.

QCM — choose was or were

🪄 Grammar Step 2 — Past continuous + past simple

This is a key storytelling structure for VTest and interviews.

1
Background action
I was helping a guest
2
Connector
when
3
Interrupting action
a child cried
4
Professional reaction
I stayed calm

Model story

I was helping a customer in the boutique when another guest asked for directions. I stayed calm, listened carefully, and helped both guests politely.

🔗 Grammar Step 3 — WHEN vs WHILE

WHEN

Use when before the shorter interrupting action.

I was helping a guest when the phone rang.

WHILE

Use while before the longer action in progress.

While I was helping a guest, the phone rang.

Past continuous

Longer background action.

I was explaining the rules...

Past simple

Short action that happened.

...a guest interrupted me.

Choose WHEN or WHILE

🎯 Grammar Step 4 — Spelling rules for -ING

Most verbs

Add -ing

help → helping

Verb ending in -e

Remove e + ing

make → making

Short verb CVC

Double final consonant

run → running

Do not change

Verbs ending in -y stay the same

play → playing

Fill in the -ING form
