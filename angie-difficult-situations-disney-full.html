✨ Grammar Spell 1 — Reassurance + WILL

Will is perfect when you decide to help now. It sounds active, professional, and reassuring.

Rule

I / You / He / She / We / They + will + verb

I will help you. / I will call security. / I will check for you.

Use it for

  • spontaneous help: I will help you.
  • guest care action: I will call my manager.
  • reassurance: I will stay with you.

✨ Grammar Spell 2 — LET ME + verb

Let me is a very useful customer-service expression. It means: “I will do this for you.”

Rule

Let me + base verb

Let me help you. / Let me check. / Let me show you.

Why it helps

  • It sounds kind.
  • It gives control.
  • It helps you continue speaking.

Build the sentence

✨ Grammar Spell 3 — DON’T + verb

Use Don’t to reassure someone or give a clear instruction.

Rule

Don’t + base verb

Don’t worry. / Don’t run. / Don’t panic.

Professional tone

For guests, add a calm phrase after it: Don’t worry. I can help you.

Make it professional

Rewrite the idea as a calm guest-care sentence.

❓ Question Magic — Ask useful questions

Questions help you understand the problem and continue the conversation.

Yes / No questions

Do / Can / Are + subject + verb?

Do you need help?
Can I help you?
Are you looking for something?

WH questions

Where / What / When + auxiliary + subject?

Where is your family?
What happened?
When does the show start?

🎤 Pronunciation & Pronouns — he / she / his / her

Important for Angie’s interview and self-introduction.

Rule

he / his = boy or man
she / her = girl or woman
they / their = plural or neutral

Examples

My brother is kind. His name is Tom.
My sister is funny. Her name is Emma.
My friends are here. Their bags are blue.
