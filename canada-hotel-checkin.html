3) Polite requests (Modals) — the key to great service (10 minutes)

Upgrade basic English into professional‑level hotel English. Practise can / could / would / may / should + typical check‑in questions.

Structure + examples

Can = neutral / friendly. Could & would = more polite. May = formal. Should = advice.

  • ✅ Could you check if breakfast is included?
  • ✅ Would it be possible to get a quiet room?
  • ✅ May I see your ID, please?
  • ✅ You should keep your key card away from magnets.

French tip: “Est‑ce que vous pouvez…” often maps to Could you…? rather than Can you…? in professional settings.

6) Spell your email address out loud (5 minutes)

Hotel staff often confirm emails on the phone. In English: @ = “at”, and use dot (not “point”) for emails.

Example: alex.smith@company.ca → “alex dot smith at company dot c a”.

Teacher notes

  • Time targets are flexible. Spend extra time on dictations + role‑play.
  • Repeat key phrases aloud (hotel English needs confidence).
  • Switch accents to train your ear (Canadian/US/UK).

Quick reminders

  • Emails: “at” (@), “dot” (.), “underscore” (_).
  • Room numbers: say each digit or group (e.g., 1207 “twelve oh seven”).
  • Politeness: “Could you…” beats “Can you…” in professional settings.
